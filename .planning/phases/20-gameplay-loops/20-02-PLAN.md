```markdown
# Gameplay Loop Strategies

## Animal Farming Strategy
[When to breed animals, pen management, food sources from animals. Breed cows for leather+beef, sheep for wool+mutton, chickens for feathers+eggs, pigs for porkchops. Need 2 of same animal + their food item (wheat for cows/sheep, seeds for chickens, carrots for pigs). Build fenced pens. Breed every 5 minutes cooldown.]

## Crop Farming Strategy
[When to plant, what to plant first (wheat = easiest, needs seeds from breaking grass). Use !farm to till and plant, !harvest when crops are mature. Bone meal speeds growth. Build near water (4 blocks max from water source). Replant after harvest for continuous supply.]

## Crop Harvesting Tips
[Mature wheat is tall and golden (age 7). Carrots/potatoes show tops poking out at age 7. Beetroot matures at age 3. Use !harvest to auto-detect mature crops and replant. Don't harvest early — you get nothing useful.]

## Bone Meal Farming
[Craft bone meal from bones (skeleton drops). Bone meal instantly grows crops 2-5 stages. Efficient: kill skeletons at night, use bones for bone meal, speed up daytime farming. 1 bone = 3 bone meal.]

## Mob Hunting for Resources
[Hunt skeletons for bones (bow, bone meal), spiders for string (bows, fishing rods), zombies for iron/carrots (rare drops), creepers for gunpowder (TNT), endermen for ender pearls (End portal). Use !hunt to seek mobs proactively. Best hunting: night time, open areas with torchlight retreats.]

## Hostile Mob Drop Table
[Skeleton: bones, arrows, rarely bow. Zombie: rotten flesh, rarely iron ingot/carrot/potato. Creeper: gunpowder. Spider: string, rarely spider eye. Witch: glass bottles, sticks, redstone, glowstone dust, sugar, spider eyes. Enderman: ender pearl. Blaze: blaze rod. Wither skeleton: coal, bone, rarely wither skull.]

## Exploration Strategy
[Use !explore to navigate to new areas. Explore in all 4 cardinal directions systematically. Look for villages (trading), temples (loot), mineshafts (ores), ocean monuments, strongholds. Log what you find. Return to base before night if undergeared. Bring food and a sword.]

## Village Discovery
[Villages have villagers with professions. Librarian (bookshelf): trades paper for emeralds, sells enchanted books. Farmer (composter): buys crops. Blacksmith (blast furnace): buys iron, sells diamond tools. Cleric (brewing stand): buys rotten flesh. Butcher (smoker): buys raw meat. Villages also have crops, chests with loot, and iron golems for protection.]

## Villager Trading Basics
[Right-click villager to open trades. Each profession has different trades. Librarians are most valuable — enchanted books. Farmers buy wheat/carrots/potatoes for emeralds. First trade is cheap, prices go up with demand. Villagers need a bed and workstation to have a profession. Trading enough levels them up for better offers.]

## Smelting Management
[Smelt raw ores immediately — raw_iron, raw_gold, raw_copper become ingots. Cook raw meat for better food (cooked beef heals 8 vs raw beef 3). Use coal as fuel (8 items per coal). Charcoal works the same (smelt any log). Blast furnace smelts ores 2x faster. Smoker cooks food 2x faster.]

## Furnace Fuel Efficiency
[1 coal/charcoal smelts 8 items. 1 plank smelts 1.5 items (waste). 1 log smelts 1.5 items. Lava bucket smelts 100 items (best single fuel). Blaze rods smelt 12 items. For large operations: build multiple furnaces and run them in parallel with !smelt.]

## Enchanting Setup Guide
[Need: enchanting table (4 obsidian + 2 diamonds + 1 book), 15 bookshelves for max level enchantments, lapis lazuli for each enchant. Place bookshelves 1 block away from enchanting table with air gap. Level 30 enchantments need player XP level 30. Kill mobs and smelt ores for XP. Best enchantments: Fortune III (more ore drops), Efficiency V (fast mining), Protection IV (damage reduction), Sharpness V (weapon damage).]

## Enchanting Priority
[First enchant: pickaxe with Fortune or Efficiency. Second: sword with Sharpness. Third: armor with Protection. Unbreaking III on everything extends durability 4x. Mending (from librarian villager) auto-repairs gear from XP orbs — most valuable enchantment.]

## Nether Portal Construction
[Build portal frame: 4 wide x 5 tall obsidian frame (10 obsidian minimum — corners optional). Mine obsidian with diamond pickaxe. Light with flint and steel (1 iron + 1 flint from gravel). Nether is dangerous: ghasts shoot fireballs, blazes shoot fire, magma cubes, wither skeletons. Bring: iron+ armor, bow+arrows, food, cobblestone for bridges.]

## Nether Resources
[Nether quartz: XP-rich, used for comparators/observers. Nether gold ore: mine with any pickaxe, gives gold nuggets. Ancient debris: mine at Y=15 with diamond pickaxe, smelt for netherite (upgrade diamond gear). Glowstone: break for dust, craft back into blocks for lighting. Blaze rods: from blazes at nether fortresses, needed for brewing + eyes of ender.]

## Nether Survival Tips
[Build a shelter immediately around your portal. Mark your path with cobblestone (doesn't naturally occur in nether). Beds EXPLODE in the nether — never place one. Bring a flint and steel in case your portal goes out. Gold armor makes piglins neutral. Water cannot be placed in the nether — bring fire resistance potions or be very careful near lava.]

## Storage Organization
[Use !deposit to put items in chests, !withdraw to take items out. Build separate chests for: ores/ingots, building materials, food, tools/weapons, miscellaneous. Label chest areas with signs or item frames. Keep a central storage room near your base. Regularly deposit excess inventory — a full inventory means missed drops.]

## Chest Management Tips
[Group similar items: all stone types together, all wood types together, all food together. Keep a "quick access" chest near the entrance with torches, food, tools, weapons. Don't hoard cobblestone — deposit extra in a dedicated chest or toss it. Sort after every mining trip.]

## Tool Progression Path
[Start: wooden pickaxe (3 planks + 2 sticks). Upgrade immediately to stone pickaxe (3 cobblestone + 2 sticks). Mine iron ore at any Y level (Y 16-64 most common). Smelt raw_iron to iron_ingot. Craft iron pickaxe + iron sword. Mine diamonds below Y=16 (Y=-58 to Y=16, best at Y=-59). Craft diamond pickaxe. Each tier unlocks new ores: wood→stone/coal, stone→iron, iron→diamond/redstone/gold/lapis/emerald, diamond→obsidian.]

## Armor Progression
[Craft armor from ingots: helmet (5), chestplate (8), leggings (7), boots (4) = 24 ingots total. Iron armor first — 24 iron ingots. Diamond armor when you have 24+ diamonds. Shield: 1 iron + 6 planks, blocks all frontal damage. Always wear armor — naked combat is suicide after early game.]

## When to Upgrade Gear
[Upgrade tool tier when you have 3+ of the next tier material. Don't skip tiers — you need stone pickaxe to mine iron, iron pickaxe to mine diamond. Replace broken tools before they break — check durability. A diamond pickaxe has 1561 uses vs iron's 250.]

## Daily Activity Loop
[Morning: check farms, harvest if mature, replant. Eat if hungry. Midday: mine or explore — longest safe window. Afternoon: smelt ores, craft upgrades, organize storage. Dusk: return to base, prepare for night. Night: mine underground (safest), or hunt mobs on surface if well-geared. Build by torchlight. Repeat.]
```

```javascript
// ── Progression Hint ──

// Dynamic gear tier detection from bot inventory — injected into system prompt Part 2.
// Pure JS, zero LLM cost. Inspects inventory for best tool tier and suggests next upgrade.
export function buildProgressionHint(bot) {
  const items = bot.inventory?.items() || []
  const names = new Set(items.map(i => i.name))

  // Check tiers from best to worst
  if (names.has('netherite_pickaxe') || names.has('netherite_sword')) {
    return 'Gear: NETHERITE — you have endgame gear. Focus on building, enchanting, and exploring.'
  }
  if (names.has('diamond_pickaxe') || names.has('diamond_sword')) {
    return 'Gear: DIAMOND — pursue enchanting (need enchanting table + 15 bookshelves + lapis). Consider nether expedition for netherite.'
  }
  if (names.has('iron_pickaxe') || names.has('iron_sword')) {
    return 'Gear: IRON — mine below Y=16 for diamonds (need 3 for pickaxe, 2 for sword). Craft iron armor if you have 24+ iron ingots.'
  }
  if (names.has('stone_pickaxe') || names.has('stone_sword')) {
    return 'Gear: STONE — mine at Y=16-64 for iron ore. Smelt raw_iron with !smelt. Craft iron pickaxe + iron sword.'
  }
  if (names.has('wooden_pickaxe') || names.has('wooden_sword')) {
    return 'Gear: WOOD — mine cobblestone immediately. Craft stone pickaxe (3 cobblestone + 2 sticks).'
  }
  return 'Gear: NONE — craft a wooden pickaxe immediately (3 planks + 2 sticks). Punch a tree first for logs.'
}
```
