# Structure Knowledge

---

## Villages

### Village Biome Types
Villages spawn in six biomes, each with distinct aesthetics and materials. Plains villages use oak wood and stone. Desert villages use sandstone and smooth sandstone. Savanna villages use acacia wood. Taiga villages use spruce wood. Snowy plains villages use spruce with snow layers. Meadow villages (rare) use spruce and stone. Identify the village type by its building materials to predict what loot and trades to expect.

### Villager Professions and Job Sites
Each villager profession requires a specific job site block. Armorer uses a blast furnace. Butcher uses a smoker. Cartographer uses a cartography table. Cleric uses a brewing stand. Farmer uses a composter. Fisherman uses a barrel. Fletcher uses a fletching table. Leatherworker uses a cauldron. Librarian uses a lectern. Mason uses a stonecutter. Shepherd uses a loom. Toolsmith uses a smithing table. Weaponsmith uses a grindstone. Nitwits and unemployed villagers have no job site and cannot trade. Destroy and replace a job site block to reset a villager's trades if the villager has not yet been traded with.

### Villager Trading
Villagers have five trade tiers: Novice, Apprentice, Journeyman, Expert, and Master. Trading unlocks higher tiers. Each trade uses emeralds as currency — buy or sell to the villager. Librarians sell enchanted books; cycle trades by breaking and replacing the lectern before your first trade. Farmers sell golden carrots, glistering melon, and pumpkin pie. Clerics sell ender pearls and glowstone. Weaponsmiths sell enchanted swords and axes. Armorers sell enchanted iron and diamond armor. Lock in a good trade before using it — trades are locked per villager once used.

### Iron Golem Protection
Villages naturally spawn iron golems: one per 10 villagers (roughly). Iron golems attack any mob that harms a villager, including you if you hit a villager. An iron golem drops 3–5 iron ingots and a poppy on death. To prevent golem aggression after accidentally hitting a villager, gain enough hero of the village status or simply avoid hitting villagers. Build your own iron golem farm near a village by placing four iron blocks in a T-shape with a carved pumpkin on top — the golem spawns facing south.

### Beds and Village Ownership
Each villager claims one bed. Beds define the village boundary. Remove a bed to reduce the village's effective population. Add beds near a village to allow it to expand its population through villager breeding. Villagers breed when they have excess food (carrots, potatoes, bread, wheat) and there are unclaimed beds nearby. Place extra beds inside enclosed structures to trigger breeding. Link a villager to a specific bed by placing the bed in a closed room with only that villager.

### Village Defense and Raids
Killing a pillager banner captain near a village (or entering a village with Bad Omen) triggers a raid: waves of pillagers, vindicators, ravagers, evokers, and witches. Surviving all waves grants Hero of the Village, giving major discounts from all village traders. Keep a totem of undying in your offhand during raids — evokers and vindicators deal heavy damage. Ring the village bell to reveal all raiders with a glowing outline.

## Desert Temples

### Layout and Approach
Desert temples are large sandstone pyramids that generate fully underground in sand-heavy deserts. Look for the orange and blue terracotta cross pattern on top. The structure has four chests arranged around a central chamber underground — accessible via a hole in the main floor. Do not drop straight down through the floor hole.

### TNT Trap
A pressure plate sits on a block of blue terracotta directly below the floor hole. Stepping on or landing on this plate detonates nine TNT blocks below, destroying all four chests and killing you. Disarm the trap before looting: dig down from the side wall, bypass the pressure plate entirely, then break the pressure plate before approaching the chests. Alternatively, break the TNT from above through the floor without triggering the plate.

### Loot
Each of the four chests contains randomized loot: bones, rotten flesh, gunpowder, sand, gravel, gold ingots, iron ingots, emeralds, enchanted books, horse armor, diamonds (rare), and saddles. The combined four chests give reasonable early-game loot. Enchanted books from desert temples can include high-level enchantments.

## Jungle Temples

### Layout and Traps
Jungle temples are mossy cobblestone and cobblestone structures, three floors tall, hidden inside dense jungle. The ground floor has a tripwire trap: two tripwire hooks connected by string trigger a dispenser firing arrows. Cut the string with shears to disarm without triggering. The second trap is a second tripwire deeper inside. Disarm both before moving freely.

### Lever Puzzle
The left staircase leads to a hidden back room with a lever puzzle. Three levers control pistons that block access to the main chest room. The correct combination to open the passage: flip levers in the right sequence (left=down, middle=up, right=down in vanilla default — verify visually). The puzzle reveals a second chest. You can bypass the puzzle entirely by breaking through the wall directly behind the levers.

### Loot
Jungle temples have two chests. The main chest (ground floor hidden room) and the puzzle-room chest. Loot includes gold ingots, iron ingots, bones, rotten flesh, bamboo, horse armor, saddles, emeralds, enchanted books, and diamonds (rare). Neither chest is trapped.

## Woodland Mansions

### Location and Rarity
Woodland mansions only generate in dark forest (roofed forest) biomes, far from spawn — often 5,000–20,000+ blocks away. Use a woodland explorer map from a cartographer villager (master tier) to locate one. The map marks the mansion with an icon. Mansions are multi-story, very large, and procedurally generated with dozens of rooms per floor.

### Hostile Mobs
Woodland mansions permanently spawn evokers and vindicators in specific rooms. Vindicators wield iron axes and deal massive melee damage — attack from a distance. Evokers summon vexes (flying, intangible attackers) and fang attacks along the ground. Kill evokers in melee only after clearing their vexes. Evokers are dangerous at all stages of the game — bring strong armor and weapons.

### Totem of Undying
Evokers always drop one totem of undying on death. Totems prevent death: when you would die, the totem activates, restoring 1 heart and applying Regeneration II and Absorption. Hold the totem in your offhand. Farm evokers at woodland mansions or during village raids for a steady supply.

### Mansion Loot
Chests inside mansion rooms contain: enchanted books, redstone, lapis lazuli, gold ingots, iron ingots, coal, bread, and diamond horse armor (rare). Some rooms have special loot: the obsidian room has a diamond block, the pumpkin room has a chest with gold, the spider room has a chest. Thoroughly clear every floor and search behind walls — rooms can be hidden.

### Darkness and Navigation
Mansions are very dark inside. Bring plenty of torches. Mobs spawn freely in unlit rooms. Place torches on every explored room to prevent respawning. The mansions do not respawn their named mobs once killed — the evokers and vindicators are finite.

## Ocean Monuments

### Structure and Location
Ocean monuments are large prismarine structures found underwater in deep ocean biomes. They are the only source of prismarine, dark prismarine, and sea lanterns in the world without crafting. Three elder guardians reside inside — one in the top chamber, two in the wings.

### Mining Fatigue
Elder guardians inflict Mining Fatigue III on any player within 50 blocks every 60 seconds — slowing your mining to near zero. Kill all three elder guardians before attempting to hollow out the monument. Use a boat to approach from the surface, then dive with potions. Milk from a cow cures Mining Fatigue instantly. Bring several milk buckets to maintain mining speed.

### Guardians
Regular guardians patrol inside and outside the monument, shooting laser beams that deal significant damage. They also deal thorns-like damage when hit with melee. Wear Depth Strider III boots and use a sword with Looting and Smite (guardians are undead). Guardians drop prismarine shards, prismarine crystals, and raw cod.

### Sponge Rooms and Gold
Monuments have one or more sponge rooms containing wet sponges. Collect all wet sponges and smelt or place in the Nether to dry them — dry sponges absorb large quantities of water and are essential for dewatering the interior. The treasure chamber at the center contains eight gold blocks (worth 72 gold ingots) surrounded by dark prismarine. This is the primary gold reward.

### Dewatering Strategy
To turn a monument into a base or farm: kill all three elder guardians first, drain water using sponges placed systematically from the top down, then seal and light the interior. Use sand columns dropped from above to displace water quickly before sponging.

## Pillager Outposts

### Structure and Location
Pillager outposts are tall stone tower structures that generate in most overworld biomes. They are the most common hostile structure. The tower has multiple floors, a chest at the top, and a small platform at the peak. Surrounding buildings include cages, dark oak logs, and hay bales.

### Pillagers
Pillagers wield crossbows and attack on sight. They respawn continuously near the outpost (within ~72 blocks) even after clearing. Outposts are not farmable without specific trap designs. Approach with a shield — crossbow bolts are blocked by shields. Clear the tower floor by floor; pillagers on the balconies have high ground advantage.

### Iron Golem Cages
Most outposts have 1–3 cage structures made of dark oak logs imprisoning iron golems. Break open the cages to free the golems — they immediately attack nearby pillagers. Use freed golems as combat support while clearing the outpost.

### Bad Omen and Raids
Killing a pillager raid captain (identifiable by the ominous banner on their back) applies Bad Omen I to you. Entering a village with Bad Omen triggers a raid. In 1.21, the ominous bottle system was added: raid captains drop Ominous Bottles instead of directly applying Bad Omen, giving you control over when to trigger a raid. Save ominous bottles for when you are prepared for a full raid.

### Outpost Chest Loot
The chest at the top of the outpost tower contains: iron ingots, gold ingots, dark oak logs, wheat, hay bales, carrots, potatoes, arrows, and crossbows (sometimes enchanted). Not exceptional loot but worth grabbing while clearing.

## Ruined Portals

### Overview
Ruined portals generate in every overworld biome and in the Nether. They are partially constructed nether portal frames made of obsidian and crying obsidian. Each ruined portal comes with a chest and a pool of lava nearby. They are the easiest early source of obsidian without a diamond pickaxe — collect the pre-placed obsidian blocks.

### Completing the Portal
Most ruined portals are 80–95% complete. Count the missing obsidian blocks (you need a complete 4×5 or 2×3 frame). Each missing block requires one obsidian. Mine the crying obsidian blocks from the frame — they cannot be used in portal frames but are used for respawn anchors in the Nether.

### Chest Loot
Ruined portal chests contain fire charges, flint and steel, gold ingots, gold nuggets, iron ingots, obsidian, enchanted golden apples (rare), golden swords, golden helmets, and golden leggings. Prioritize enchanted golden apples — they provide Absorption IV, Fire Resistance, and Regeneration II, making them extremely powerful early game.

## Strongholds

### Locating Strongholds
Strongholds are underground dungeon complexes that contain the End Portal. Throw eyes of ender — they travel toward the nearest stronghold and either float in the direction of travel or fall to the ground above the stronghold. Follow the eye's path using multiple throws to triangulate. Strongholds generate in rings around (0,0): the first ring is 1,280–2,816 blocks from spawn and contains 3 strongholds.

### End Portal Room
The End Portal room contains a silverfish spawner, a lava pool, and the portal frame with 12 end portal frame blocks. Up to 4 eyes of ender may already be filled in. Fill the remaining frames with eyes of ender to activate the portal. Stand outside the frame when placing the last eye — the portal activates instantly and you can fall through.

### Silverfish Spawners
Silverfish spawners appear in the End Portal room and sometimes elsewhere in the stronghold. Silverfish are weak alone but emerge in groups from infested stone blocks, which look identical to regular stone. Silverfish alert nearby silverfish when hit — avoid breaking stone near the spawner without a weapon ready. Bring a sword enchanted with Bane of Arthropods for fast kills.

### Libraries
Most strongholds contain one or two library rooms — tall rooms filled with bookshelves, a chest, and sometimes a second-floor balcony. Library chests contain: paper, books, enchanted books (often high-level), compasses, maps, and bread. Libraries are worth finding for their enchanted books.

### Stronghold Navigation
Strongholds are maze-like with no fixed layout. Navigate systematically: light every corridor with torches, mark explored dead ends, and follow corridors that curve or descend since the portal room tends to be near the bottom. Carry a water bucket to extinguish lava traps.

## Mineshafts

### Overview
Abandoned mineshafts are large underground wooden tunnel networks. They generate at Y=0 to Y=50 in normal worlds. Mesa/badlands biomes have surface-level mineshafts starting at Y=80+. Follow minecart rails to navigate — they always lead somewhere.

### Cave Spider Spawners
Cave spider spawners appear in mineshafts inside cobweb clusters. Cave spiders are small (fit through half-blocks), fast, and apply Poison on hard difficulty. Destroy cobwebs with a sword or shears before engaging. Kill the spawner immediately on sight — cave spiders are more dangerous than regular spiders. Bring milk as an antidote to Poison.

### Chest Minecarts
Mineshafts contain chest minecarts scattered along rails. Right-click a chest minecart to open it without destroying it, or break it to drop the chest. Loot includes: bread, coal, gold ingots, iron ingots, iron pickaxes, rails, torches, lapis lazuli, redstone, melon seeds, pumpkin seeds, beetroot seeds, and diamond (rare).

### Rails as Resources
Mineshafts contain large quantities of rail (regular rail, not powered or detector). Rails are otherwise expensive to craft (6 iron ingots + 1 stick = 16 rails). Collect all rails during exploration — useful for minecart transport systems later. Bring extra torches: mineshafts are very dark and sprawling.

## Nether Fortress

### Locating Fortresses
Nether fortresses generate as large brick bridge-and-tower structures in the Nether. They spawn in specific X-axis bands: scan along the Z-axis (north/south) while keeping X constant, roughly in bands 200 blocks wide. Travel in the X direction if you cannot find one. Fortresses can generate partially buried inside netherrack.

### Blaze Spawners
Nether fortresses contain 2–3 blaze spawners. Blazes are required kills: they drop blaze rods, which are mandatory for crafting blaze powder and brewing stands. You need a brewing stand and blaze powder to brew all potions. Kill at least 10–12 blazes for sufficient blaze rods. Blazes shoot fire charges — use a shield to block or strafe. Build a platform above the spawner to safely farm blazes.

### Wither Skeleton Skulls
Wither skeletons spawn only inside nether fortresses. They drop wither skeleton skulls (2.5% base drop rate, increased by Looting). Three skulls on four soul sand blocks in a T-pattern summon the Wither boss. The Wither drops one nether star, used for crafting a beacon. Farm 3 skulls patiently — with Looting III it takes roughly 40–80 kills on average.

### Nether Wart
Nether wart grows only in soul sand patches near fortress staircases and in a few dedicated rooms. Nether wart is the base ingredient for all awkward potions — every useful potion starts with an awkward potion. Collect all nether wart and replant some on soul sand near your base. Nether wart does not require light and grows in any dimension.

### Fortress Loot Chests
Fortress chests contain: nether wart, saddles, iron ingots, gold ingots, diamonds, diamond horse armor, iron sword, iron pickaxe, flint and steel, obsidian, and fire charge. Prioritize finding all chests before committing to a specific area of the fortress.

## Bastion Remnants

### Four Bastion Types
Bastion remnants are large piglin structures in the Nether, found in every biome except basalt deltas. Four types exist: Housing (large, many rooms, scattered loot), Stables (ground level, hoglin pens), Treasure (center gold block platform, best loot), and Bridge (spans a gap, fewer rooms). Treasure bastions have the highest concentration of gold and netherite loot. The treasure room sits in the center surrounded by magma cube spawners.

### Piglin Brutes
Piglin brutes spawn inside bastions only. They are more dangerous than regular piglins: they deal 7.5 hearts of damage per hit, are immune to fire, do not barter, and cannot be pacified with gold armor. Kill brutes before exploring the bastion — they ignore gold armor entirely. They drop gold axes and occasionally a golden axe enchanted.

### Gold and Bartering
Bastions contain large quantities of gold blocks, visible on the treasure platform and in housing walls. Wear full gold armor (even just one gold piece) to prevent regular piglins from attacking. Trade gold ingots with piglins by throwing gold at their feet — bartering gives random loot including: ender pearls, string, obsidian, iron boots, iron nuggets, potions, spectral arrows, and fire charges. Bartering is the fastest source of ender pearls without killing endermen.

### Netherite Upgrade Template
Bastions are the only source of the Netherite Upgrade Smithing Template, found in treasure room chests. One template lets you upgrade one diamond item to netherite at a smithing table (requires netherite ingot). Duplicate the template using 7 diamonds + 1 netherrack + 1 template at a smithing table. Farm multiple bastions or duplicate to upgrade all gear slots.

### Bastion Navigation Tips
Mark the entrance before exploring — bastions are labyrinthine. Bring at least 2 stacks of gold ingots for bartering and appeasement. Kill all piglin brutes first. Do not open chests or break blocks near piglins without gold armor — this makes them hostile. The treasure room is usually in the lowest section of the bastion, accessible from the large central hall.

## End Cities

### Reaching End Cities
End cities generate on the outer End islands, accessible only through the End gateway portals that appear after defeating the Ender Dragon. Throw an ender pearl through the gateway (the small obsidian frame) to teleport to the outer islands. End cities are scattered across the outer islands — travel in a straight line until you spot one. Bring an elytra from a previous playthrough or chorus fruit to navigate the gaps.

### Shulkers
Shulkers are the only mob found in end cities. They appear as lavender boxes attached to walls, floors, and ceilings. Shulker projectiles apply the Levitation status effect — you float upward for 10 seconds, then fall. Always carry a water bucket to break a fall after levitation ends. Shulkers drop shulker shells (50% chance, increased by Looting). Two shulker shells + one chest = one shulker box (portable, stackable inventory with 27 slots). Shulker boxes are among the most important items in the game.

### Elytra
End ships — a separate floating vessel connected to many end cities by a single bridge — contain the elytra. The elytra hangs inside the ship's hull in an item frame at the bow. An ender dragon head is mounted at the ship's prow — pick it up. The elytra lets you glide: equip it in the chest slot, fall off a high point, and press jump to deploy. Combine with firework rockets to achieve powered flight. The elytra is the most important mobility item in the game.

### End City Loot
End city chests contain: iron/gold/diamond/chain armor (often enchanted), iron/gold/diamond tools and weapons (often enchanted), saddles, horse armor, emeralds, and beetroot. The enchantments are random but often high-level due to the dungeon's late-game status. Thoroughly loot every floor and every room of each tower.

### Chorus Plants and Fruit
Chorus plants grow naturally throughout the outer End islands. Break them at the base to harvest all chorus fruit above. Chorus fruit teleports you randomly within 8 blocks when eaten — useful for escaping fall damage or getting unstuck, but risky near ledges. Popped chorus fruit (from smelting) crafts purpur blocks and end rods. Bring chorus fruit as emergency teleport food.

## Ancient Cities

### Deep Dark Biome
Ancient cities generate exclusively in the deep dark biome, at Y=-52 or lower, beneath large cave systems. The deep dark is identifiable by the sculk blocks (flat, dark blue-green) covering the floor. You will hear heartbeat-like sounds when a warden is nearby. The deep dark generates in large mountainous areas — check the surface terrain for tall mountains to predict where deep dark caves exist below.

### Warden Danger
The warden is the most dangerous mob in the game: it deals 22.5 hearts per hit through armor, has 500 health, and is blind (uses sound and vibration to locate targets). Avoid triggering sculk shriekers — each activation adds a warning level. Three warning levels summon the warden. Crouch (sneak) at all times in the deep dark to reduce vibration output. Do not sprint, place blocks carelessly, shoot arrows, or fight other mobs in the area. If a warden spawns, run — do not fight it until you have Netherite armor with Protection IV.

### Sculk Mechanics
Sculk spreads when mobs die in the deep dark. Sculk sensors detect vibrations (movement, breaking blocks, attacking) and transmit signals to sculk shriekers. Sculk shriekers summon the warden and apply Darkness. Identify shriekers (they have a raised pattern in the center) and disable them first — either mine them with Silk Touch or plan a route avoiding them. Wool blocks suppress vibrations — place wool on floors you must walk on.

### Ancient City Loot
Ancient cities have the best loot in the overworld: Swift Sneak enchanted book (exclusive here — reduces sneaking speed penalty), disc fragments (combine 9 to craft the Pigstep disc), echo shards (craft recovery compass), sculk catalysts, sculk sensors, reinforced deepslate (cannot be obtained in survival), and rare enchanted items. The main loot chests sit on the central altar platform. Approach by crouching the entire way.

### Ancient City Strategy
Enter the city from the edges, not the center. Disable all visible sculk shriekers before collecting loot. Bring a Silk Touch pickaxe to collect sculk and sensors. Bring wool to suppress vibrations from necessary movement. Carry a bucket of water to extinguish fires. If the warden appears, throw snowballs or shoot arrows away from yourself to distract it (it walks toward the sound). Have a planned escape route before you enter.

## Buried Treasure

### Locating Buried Treasure
Buried treasure chests are marked on explorer maps found in shipwrecks and ocean ruins. The X marker on the map indicates the exact surface block above the chest. Stand at the X (the center of the white cross) and dig down 2–3 blocks. Treasure chests always generate under the top layer of the terrain — usually under sand or gravel on beaches, occasionally under the ocean floor.

### Heart of the Sea
Every buried treasure chest contains exactly one heart of the sea. The heart of the sea is the primary crafting ingredient for a conduit — place it in the center of an 8-nautilus-shell crafting recipe. A conduit, when activated inside a water structure (3×3 frames of prismarine), grants Conduit Power: unlimited underwater breathing, night vision, and haste underwater. Conduits are essential for ocean monument clearing and underwater bases.

### Other Loot
Beyond the heart of the sea, buried treasure chests contain: iron ingots, gold ingots, iron swords, TNT, feathers, emerald, leather tunic, and cooked fish. The non-heart loot is secondary — focus on collecting the heart for conduit construction.

## Shipwrecks

### Three Chest Types
Shipwrecks contain up to three chests, each occupying a specific section of the wreck. The supply chest (in the stern/back section) holds: paper, wheat, carrots, potatoes, rotten flesh, gunpowder, glass bottles, and TNT. The treasure chest (in the upper section, if present) holds: iron ingots, gold ingots, emeralds, and lapis lazuli. The map chest (in the bow/front section) holds: compasses, paper, a buried treasure explorer map, and empty maps. Prioritize the map chest to find buried treasure.

### Orientation
Shipwrecks generate in various orientations: upright, on their side, upside down, or partially buried. The three chest sections are always in the same relative part of the ship regardless of rotation. If the ship is upside down, the chests are at the bottom (which is now the "top" of the inverted hull). Fully explore the wreck — drowned mobs sometimes spawn inside flooded sections.

### Drowned Spawning
Drowned zombies spawn in and around shipwrecks. They can hold tridents and nautilus shells. Drowned with tridents will throw them at you from range. Kill drowned near shipwrecks to collect nautilus shells — you need 8 for a conduit. Farm drowned systematically if building a conduit.

## Witch Huts

### Location and Identification
Witch huts (swamp huts) are small wooden structures on stilts that generate in swamp biomes. They are easy to identify: small square platform with a cauldron inside, usually surrounded by swamp water and lily pads. The cauldron inside may contain a random potion.

### Witch Behavior
A witch always spawns inside her hut on world generation and respawns there. Witches throw splash potions: Poison, Slowness, Weakness, and Harming. They also drink potions to heal and gain Fire Resistance or Water Breathing. Kill quickly with a bow from range. Witches drop various potions, glass bottles, redstone, glowstone dust, gunpowder, sticks, and spider eyes.

### Cats
Witch huts always generate with a black cat inside. The cat does not despawn like most passive mobs and can be tamed with raw fish. Cats scare away creepers (creepers will not approach a player holding a cat or with a tamed cat nearby) and phantoms. Breed cats in your base by feeding tamed cats raw fish.

## Igloos

### Overworld Generation
Igloos generate in snowy plains and snowy taiga biomes. They are small snow-block structures with a single room, a crafting table, a furnace, a bed, and a carpet. About half of all igloos have a basement; the other half are empty.

### The Basement Secret
Igloos with basements have a trapdoor hidden under the carpet. Drop through to find a stone-walled basement with: a villager (regular), a zombie villager trapped behind iron bars, a brewing stand with a Splash Potion of Weakness, and a golden apple in a chest. Use the potion and golden apple to cure the zombie villager — throw the splash weakness at the zombie villager, then right-click it with the golden apple. After 2–5 minutes, the zombie villager transforms into a Cleric. This cured villager offers discounted trades permanently.

### Igloo Loot
The chest in igloo basements contains: golden apple, coal, wheat, potatoes, apples, and bread. The golden apple is key for curing the zombie villager. If the igloo has no basement, there is no chest.

## Trial Chambers (1.21)

### Overview
Trial chambers are the major new structure added in Minecraft 1.21 (Java Edition 1.21). They generate underground in the overworld, typically at Y=-20 to Y=-40. They are large, copper-decorated dungeon complexes with multiple rooms and corridors. The walls and floors use copper blocks, tuff bricks, and copper grates.

### Trial Spawners
Trial spawners are the defining feature: they spawn mobs based on the number of players nearby and cannot be broken normally. Each trial spawner spawns a fixed type of mob: zombies, skeletons, spiders, cave spiders, slimes, or zombified piglins in later rooms. After all mobs from a spawner are killed, the spawner rewards you with loot thrown out directly or placed in nearby vaults. Trial spawners have a cooldown period before reactivating.

### Breeze Mob
The breeze is a new mob exclusive to trial chambers. It leaps around the room and launches wind charge projectiles that deal damage and push you (and entities, including mobs) backward. Wind charges also interact with toggleable blocks: buttons, levers, trapdoors, and doors. Dodge the wind charges by strafing. Breeze drops breeze rods, used to craft wind charges and the mace weapon.

### Ominous Trial Chambers
Entering a trial chamber with Bad Omen (from an ominous bottle) activates Ominous Trial Spawners — harder variants that spawn additional mobs and add witch effects to regular spawners. Ominous spawners yield better loot: heavy cores, wind charges, ominous bottles, and enchanted books. Prepare carefully before attempting ominous mode.

### Trial Vault Loot
Trial chambers contain vault blocks — orange-glowing blocks that open once per player per world. Insert a trial key (dropped by trial spawners) into a vault to collect loot: wind charges, emeralds, iron ingots, diamonds, enchanted books, iron/golden equipment, and the heavy core. The heavy core combines with a mace head (blaze rod) to craft the mace — a new weapon that deals extra damage when falling from height.

### Mace Weapon
The mace is the new 1.21 weapon. It deals progressively more damage based on fall distance before hitting: 1.5 bonus hearts per block fallen. A 10-block fall adds 15 extra hearts of damage. The mace resets fall damage on a successful hit. Enchant it with Density (more fall damage), Breach (ignores armor), or Wind Burst (launches you upward on kill, enabling chain attacks). The mace is most effective when attacking from above or using Wind Burst to chain aerial hits.

## Nether Structures

### Nether Fossil
Nether fossils are large bone block structures that generate in the soul sand valley biome. They are purely decorative and contain no loot. Collect bone blocks for bone meal or decoration.

### Nether Ruins (Netherack Ruins)
Small scattered ruins of nether bricks appear in the nether wastes. No chests, no loot, but they indicate a fortress may be nearby — follow nether brick fragments in the direction they increase to find a fortress.

## Building Techniques

### Foundation First
Always start with the foundation. Lay the full footprint of your structure at ground level before building upward. Use a temporary material (dirt, netherrack) to mark the outline. Verify dimensions before committing to expensive materials. A standard house footprint is 9×9 to 11×11 blocks for comfortable interior space.

### Wall Construction
Build walls 4–5 blocks tall for a spacious single-story interior. Use mixed materials to avoid flat, textureless walls: alternate stone brick with cracked stone brick, or oak planks with oak logs at corners. Place windows every 2–4 blocks for a realistic appearance. Windows are 1 or 2 blocks tall — use glass panes, not glass blocks, for a thinner window frame.

### Roof Styles
Four primary roof types: Flat roof (easiest, add a parapet wall 1 block tall at the edge), Gabled roof (pitched triangular ends, slope from center ridge), Hipped roof (all four sides slope toward a central ridge), and Dome roof (circular or rectangular stepped pyramid using stairs). Use stairs and slabs for pitched roof profiles — full blocks look boxy. Match roof material to wall material with a contrasting accent (dark oak logs on spruce walls, stone slabs on brick walls).

### Interior Design
Divide large interiors using half-walls (fences, walls, or 2-block-tall barriers). Place a bed, crafting table, furnace, and chests in every base room as minimum. Use trapdoors as decorative cabinet doors, item frames as labels, barrels as rustic storage, and bookshelves for library aesthetics. Lighting: use lanterns (hang from ceiling with a fence post), candles (stackable, place multiple on one block for brightness), or sea lanterns for modern builds. Never leave interior below light level 8 or mobs spawn.

### Stairs and Slabs for Detail
Use stairs upside-down (right-click the top face of a block, then place a stair against the underside) to create crown molding, window sills, and furniture. Slabs at half-block height create step detail on walls and floors. Combine upper and lower slabs to simulate a full block with a visible seam. Use slab floors in kitchens and bathrooms for a layered tile effect.

## Material Properties

### Blast Resistance
Obsidian has the highest blast resistance (1200) of all obtainable blocks — use it for TNT-proof structures. Blast resistance values: Obsidian 1200, Crying Obsidian 1200, Ender Chest 3000 (highest), Ancient Debris 1200, Reinforced Deepslate (unobtainable in survival). Standard stone 6, stone brick 6, cobblestone 6, dirt 0.5, wood planks 3. TNT explosions have a radius of ~4 blocks. Never use wood, wool, leaves, or sand/gravel in structures near lava or creeper-prone areas.

### Flammability
Flammable blocks can be ignited by fire, lava, or lightning: wood planks, logs, slabs, stairs (all wood types), fences, gates, leaves, wool, carpet, bookshelves, bamboo, sugarcane. Fire spreads from any flammable block to adjacent flammable blocks within 4 blocks. In the Nether, fire burns indefinitely on netherrack. Use stone, brick, concrete, or metal blocks in any lava-adjacent structure. Leave at least one non-flammable block gap between fire sources and wood.

### Transparency and Light Passage
Fully transparent blocks (let full light through and do not reduce sky light): glass, glass panes, ice, barriers. Partially transparent (reduce sky light by one per layer): leaves, slabs (top half), trapdoors, carpets. Opaque (block all light): all full solid blocks. Sea lanterns, glowstone, and beacons emit the maximum light level of 15. Light level 7 is the minimum to prevent hostile mob spawning; light level 8+ is safe. Place torches or lanterns so their light radiates to at least 7 on every floor tile.

### Gravity-Affected Blocks
Gravity blocks fall when unsupported: sand, red sand, gravel, concrete powder, anvils, pointed dripstone (falling stalactites kill entities), suspicious sand/gravel. Never build structures using gravity blocks as primary material — they collapse when support blocks are removed. Use concrete powder and lava or water to convert powder to concrete: drop concrete powder through water to solidify it instantly.

## Finding Structures

### Exploration Strategy for Structures
Travel in expanding spirals from your base to locate structures efficiently. Structures generate based on biome and region seed — some areas have no structures for hundreds of blocks. Use cartographer villager maps (buy an "explorer map" for emeralds) to locate woodland mansions and ocean monuments. Use shipwreck treasure maps to locate buried treasure. Travel by boat along coastlines to spot ocean structures. Explore underground at Y=10-50 for dungeons, mineshafts, and strongholds.

### Cartographer Villager Trading
Cure a zombie villager (splash weakness potion + golden apple) near a cartography table to get a discounted cartographer. Buy "Ocean Explorer Map" (locates nearest ocean monument) and "Woodland Explorer Map" (locates nearest mansion) from Expert-level cartographers. Maps show an "X" at the target structure. Navigate to the X to find the structure. Cartographer maps are the most efficient way to find end-game structures without random exploration.

### Looting Priority by Structure
Top tier (always raid): Ocean Monument (prismarine, sponge, gold blocks), Woodland Mansion (evoker = totem of undying), Ancient City (swift sneak books, echo shards, disc fragments, enchanted golden apples). Mid tier: Bastion Remnant (netherite scraps, gold), Nether Fortress (blaze rods, nether wart). Accessible tier: Villages (food, beds, trades), Desert Temple (gold, diamonds chance), Buried Treasure (heart of the sea, diamonds). Early tier: Dungeons (spawner, basic loot), Shipwrecks (maps, food, emeralds).

## Additional Structures Quick Reference

### Desert Wells
Desert wells are small sandstone structures (1×3×1) that spawn in desert biomes. They contain no loot. The well provides a water source in an otherwise waterless biome — use buckets to collect infinite water from it by creating a 2×2 pool next to the original source blocks.

### Ocean Ruins (Underwater Ruins)
Ocean ruins are clusters of broken stone brick or sandstone structures on the ocean floor. They come in warm and cold variants. Cold ruins use stone bricks; warm ruins use sandstone. Each ruin may contain 0–1 chests and drowned zombies. Loot includes: emeralds, gold ingots, coal, wheat, leather armor, wooden tools, stone axes, saplings, suspicious sand/gravel (containing archaeological artifacts), and enchanted fishing rods.

### Geodes (Amethyst)
Amethyst geodes generate underground from Y=-58 to Y=30. They are spherical structures with three layers: outer smooth basalt, middle calcite, inner amethyst blocks with budding amethyst. Budding amethyst grows amethyst clusters over time — only budding amethyst generates new crystals (cannot be moved with Silk Touch without destroying the budding property). Mine fully grown amethyst clusters (large clusters with 4 points) for 4 amethyst shards each. Amethyst is used for: spyglass (2 copper ingots + 1 amethyst shard), tinted glass (4 amethyst + 1 glass block), and calibrated sculk sensors.

### Mob Spawner Dungeons
Small dungeons are 5×5×5 rooms made of cobblestone and mossy cobblestone that contain one mob spawner (zombie, skeleton, or spider) and 1–2 chests. Loot: saddle, golden apple, enchanted book, iron ingots, bread, wheat, coal, redstone, string. Convert dungeons into XP farms by building a water funnel leading mobs to a kill point and adding a torch to the spawner to pause it safely.

### Coral Reefs
Coral reefs generate in warm ocean biomes (lukewarm and warm). They contain: tube coral, brain coral, bubble coral, fire coral, horn coral — each with block, fan, and dead variants. Mine with Silk Touch to collect live coral — coral dies (turns grey) immediately if not in water. Live coral is decorative only. Fish, sea pickles, and sea turtles spawn near reefs.

## Structure Raiding Safety

### Gear Up Before Raiding Any Structure
Never raid a structure without: full iron armor minimum, iron sword, food (20+ cooked items), torches (64+), and a water bucket. Ocean monuments additionally require: Potion of Water Breathing (3+ minutes), Potion of Night Vision, and an Aqua Affinity helmet. Woodland mansions require full diamond armor with Protection IV — evokers have a lethal curse attack. Bastions require full diamond armor + gold helmet to avoid immediate piglin aggro. Ancient cities require full diamond armor + sneak movement the entire time.

## Village Trading Economy

### Iron Golem Farming Near Villages
Iron golems spawn naturally in villages (one per 10 villagers). Golems attack hostile mobs and protect villagers. Do not attack iron golems — killing one earns you "Village Hero" or "Gossip" negative reputation and causes all villagers to refuse trades. Iron golems drop 3-5 iron ingots when killed by the player — build an iron golem farm (trap golems in a lava blade) for passive iron ingot production. Each golem kill = 3-5 iron; well-designed farms produce 200+ iron/hour.

### Village Raid Mechanic
Kill a pillager patrol captain (banner on its back) anywhere in the world to receive "Bad Omen" status effect. Entering a village with Bad Omen active triggers a Raid — waves of pillagers, vindicators, ravagers, evokers, and witches attack the village. Survive all waves (5–7 waves on normal) to get the "Hero of the Village" effect: 50% discount on all villager trades for several minutes. This is the fastest way to unlock cheap villager trades. Bring full iron+ armor, a shield, and a bow for raids.
