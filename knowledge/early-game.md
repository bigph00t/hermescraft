# Early Game Progression — Minecraft 1.21.1

Step-by-step sequence for a player (or AI bot) starting with an empty inventory.
All Y-levels are Java Edition. Ore distribution unchanged since 1.18.

---

### First 5 Minutes (Minutes 0–5)

1. Identify the nearest tree. Any log type works: oak, birch, spruce, jungle, acacia, dark oak, mangrove.
2. Hold left-click on any log block until it breaks. Collect the log item.
3. Punch down the entire tree trunk (leaves drop saplings and occasionally apples — ignore them for now).
4. Collect a minimum of 4 logs. 8–10 logs is better; takes ~60 seconds per full tree.
5. Open inventory (E). Place 1 log in the 2×2 crafting grid. Convert all logs to planks (1 log → 4 planks).
6. Craft a Crafting Table: fill all 4 slots of the 2×2 grid with planks (4 planks → 1 crafting table).
7. Place the crafting table on the ground (right-click).
8. Open the crafting table (right-click). Craft sticks: 2 planks stacked vertically → 4 sticks.
9. Craft Wooden Pickaxe: 3 planks across top row + 2 sticks down center column.
10. Craft Wooden Axe: 3 planks in top-left L-shape + 2 sticks down center (faster log chopping).
11. If a hostile mob is visible within melee range, craft Wooden Sword: 2 planks stacked center + 1 stick below.
12. Pick up the crafting table and carry it — do not leave it behind.

**Minimum inventory after minute 5:** wooden pickaxe, wooden axe, crafting table, 8+ planks.

---

### Minutes 5–10: Stone Age

1. Locate exposed stone. Hillsides, cliffs, and ravine walls all work. Stone is the pale gray block.
2. Mine 14+ cobblestone with the wooden pickaxe (stone drops cobblestone when mined).
   - 3 for stone pickaxe
   - 3 for stone axe (optional, replaces wooden axe)
   - 2 for stone sword
   - 8 for furnace
   = 16 total; mine a few extra for contingency.
3. Re-place crafting table. Craft:
   - **Stone Pickaxe**: 3 cobblestone top row + 2 sticks center column.
   - **Stone Sword**: 2 cobblestone stacked center + 1 stick below.
   - **Stone Axe**: 3 cobblestone in L-shape top-left + 2 sticks center.
   - **Furnace**: 8 cobblestone around the border of the 3×3 grid, center empty.
4. Place furnace. You now have all core day-1 tools.
5. Look for coal ore while mining (black speckled stone). Coal is common from Y=0 to Y=128, peak at Y=96.
   - Mine coal with stone pickaxe. Each block drops 2–5 coal.
6. Craft torches: 1 coal + 1 stick → 4 torches. Place these in your hotbar immediately.
7. If no coal found: smelt 1 log in the furnace using planks as fuel → 1 charcoal. Use charcoal as coal substitute.

**Minimum inventory after minute 10:** stone pickaxe, stone sword, furnace, 8+ torches, crafting table.

---

### Minutes 10–20: Shelter Before Sunset

Minecraft day runs 10 minutes (12000 ticks). Sunset begins at tick 12000 (~minute 12 of game time). Hostile mobs (zombies, skeletons, creepers, spiders) spawn in darkness after sunset.

**Priority goal: be inside with a light source before the sky turns orange.**

#### Shelter Options (pick fastest available)

**Option A — Hillside Burrow (fastest)**
1. Find a hillside with exposed stone or dirt.
2. Dig 4 blocks straight in, then open a 3×3×3 room inside.
3. Place crafting table, furnace, and torches inside.
4. Seal the entrance with any blocks (dirt, cobblestone, planks).
5. Optional: leave a 1-block gap to monitor daylight through.

**Option B — Surface Hut**
1. Collect 20+ dirt or cobblestone.
2. Build a 5×5 footprint with 3-block-high walls.
3. Fill the roof (dirt or planks).
4. Place a door: 6 planks in a 2×3 arrangement → 3 doors. Place in the opening.
5. Place torches inside on walls.

**Getting a Bed (skips the night entirely — highest priority)**
1. Kill 3 sheep. Sheep drop 1 wool each when killed. White, gray, brown, black — any color works.
   - Sheep are found in plains, forests, savannas. They are passive — approach and left-click.
2. Craft Bed: 3 wool across the top row + 3 matching planks across the middle row.
3. Place bed inside shelter. Right-click to sleep as soon as the sky dims (night message: "You can sleep now").
4. Sleeping skips the night and resets your spawn point to the bed location.

**If no sheep found before sunset:**
- Seal yourself in a hillside burrow with torches.
- Wait underground or mine until the sun rises (10 real-time minutes).
- Do not open the entrance until you see full daylight.

**Torches:**
- Coal or charcoal from the furnace. Smelt 1 log (any wood) as fuel + 1 log as input → 1 charcoal.
- Place torches on walls or floors inside shelter to prevent mob spawning (mobs need light level ≤0 to spawn in 1.18+).

**Food — do not neglect hunger:**
- Kill cows, pigs, or chickens for raw meat. Smelt in furnace (use planks or coal as fuel).
- Cooked beef (steak): 8 hunger + high saturation — best early food.
- Cooked porkchop or chicken also viable.
- Bread: 3 wheat (harvested from wild grass or found in villages) → 5 hunger. Slower to set up.
- Eat when hunger bar drops below 6 icons (sprint will be disabled at 6, healing stops at 3).

**End of day-1 checklist:**
- [ ] Shelter with door or sealed entrance
- [ ] Crafting table placed inside
- [ ] Furnace placed inside
- [ ] Stone pickaxe + stone sword in hotbar
- [ ] Torches on walls
- [ ] Bed placed (ideally)
- [ ] Some cooked food

### Day 2: The Iron Age

Iron is the single most important material in early Minecraft. It unlocks the pickaxe required for diamonds, a sword that can handle most mobs, a bucket for water safety, and a shield for blocking.

**Iron ore spawns:** Y=-64 to Y=320. Two peaks:
- **Y=15 to Y=16** — best underground level for reliable iron away from the surface.
- **Y=232** — extreme mountain peaks (only relevant if you spawned in mountain biomes).

**For standard early mining: target Y=15.**

#### Mining for Iron

1. From inside your shelter, dig a staircase (diagonal steps, never straight down — fall hazard and lava risk) down to Y=15. Check your coordinates with F3 (Java Edition).
2. At Y=15, dig a straight horizontal tunnel (branch mine). Iron ore appears as stone blocks with orange-tan flecks.
3. Mine iron ore with stone pickaxe. Drops raw iron (1.17+ — not iron ore blocks).
4. Watch for coal seams along the way — mine all coal you find.
5. Place torches every 8 blocks to stay lit (prevents mob spawning).
6. Collect at least 16 raw iron for first-priority items. 24+ if you want full armor eventually.

**Minimum iron requirements — Day 2 priority order:**

| Item | Iron ingots |
|---|---|
| Iron Pickaxe | 3 |
| Iron Sword | 2 |
| Bucket | 3 |
| Shield | 1 (+ 6 planks) |
| **Day 2 minimum** | **9** |
| Full iron armor (helmet + chestplate + leggings + boots) | 24 |

#### Smelting Iron

1. Return to furnace. Place raw iron in the top slot.
2. Place fuel in the bottom slot: coal, charcoal, wood planks, or logs.
   - 1 coal smelts 8 items. 1 log smelts 1.5 items. Planks smelt 1.5 items each.
3. Each raw iron → 1 iron ingot.

#### Crafting Iron Tools (in priority order)

1. **Iron Pickaxe**: 3 iron ingots top row + 2 sticks center column. Required to mine gold, diamonds, and redstone.
2. **Iron Sword**: 2 iron ingots stacked center + 1 stick below. Significantly stronger than stone.
3. **Bucket**: 3 iron ingots in a V shape (left-center, right-center, bottom-center). Fill from any water source.
4. **Shield**: 1 iron ingot center-top, surrounded by 6 wood planks in a kite shape. Blocks most melee and ranged attacks when held offhand and right-clicked.

**Water bucket — always carry this.** If you fall into lava, immediately place water to extinguish yourself. Pour water onto lava to create obsidian (needed for nether portal later).

### Days 3–5: Establishing a Base

With iron tools secured, focus shifts from survival to infrastructure.

#### Base Construction

1. Build a proper house: minimum 7×7 interior, wood or cobblestone walls, plank or stone roof.
2. Interior essentials: crafting table, double chest (2 chests side-by-side = double chest), furnace, bed.
3. Add a second double chest for overflow storage.
4. Use signs or item frames on chests to label contents.

#### Wheat Farm

1. Find a flat area within 5 blocks of water (crops need to be within 4 horizontal blocks of a water source to grow).
2. Equip a hoe (craft: 2 planks or 2 of any material in top-left L-shape + 2 sticks). Right-click dirt or grass → farmland (darker, lower block).
3. Get seeds: punch tall grass (the 1-block tall grass) until seeds drop. Each grass has ~12.5% chance to drop 1–3 seeds.
4. Right-click farmland with seeds to plant. Wheat grows through 8 stages (takes 1–3 real-time minutes per stage with light).
5. Harvest at full growth (bright yellow stalks): yields 1 wheat + 1–4 seeds. Re-plant immediately.
6. 3 wheat → 1 bread in a crafting table (3 wheat in a horizontal row).
7. Aim for 20+ wheat plants as soon as possible.

#### Animal Pen

1. Locate cows and sheep. They spawn in plains, forest, and most surface biomes.
2. Hold wheat to attract them. Animals follow you if wheat is in your hand within ~6 blocks.
3. Build a fenced pen: 6 planks → 2 fence gates; use fence gate as entrance.
   - Fence: 4 planks + 2 sticks → 3 fence sections.
   - Fence Gate: 4 planks + 2 sticks in specific pattern → 1 gate.
4. Lead at least 2 cows and 2 sheep into the pen.
5. Breed with wheat: right-click two adult animals of the same species with wheat → baby spawns. 5-minute cooldown before re-breeding. Babies take 20 minutes to mature.
6. Cows provide: beef (cooked in furnace → steak), leather (4 leather → crafting table recipe → leather armor).
7. Sheep provide: wool (shear with iron shears for renewable wool) and mutton.

#### Cave Exploration

1. Find a natural cave entrance or dig down from your base staircase.
2. Bring: iron sword, iron pickaxe, 30+ torches, cooked food, bucket of water.
3. Place torches on the LEFT wall as you go in. On the way out, torches are on your RIGHT — this prevents getting lost.
4. Mine all coal and iron you encounter.
5. Watch for gravel (falls when unsupported), lava pools (below Y=10, common around Y=-54), and hostile mobs.
6. If you hear cave sounds (ambient hisses, growls), mobs are nearby in an unlit section.

#### Iron Armor

When you have accumulated 24 iron ingots, craft full iron armor:
- **Helmet**: 5 ingots — top row + left and right of middle row.
- **Chestplate**: 8 ingots — left and right of top row + full middle row + full bottom row.
- **Leggings**: 7 ingots — top row + left and right of middle and bottom rows.
- **Boots**: 4 ingots — left and right of bottom two rows.

Full iron armor reduces incoming damage by ~60%. Priority order if ingots are limited: chestplate > leggings > boots > helmet.

### Day 5+: Advanced Progression

#### Diamond Mining

Diamonds are required for: enchanting table (2 diamonds), diamond pickaxe (3 diamonds), nether portal mining (need diamond pick to mine obsidian), and eventual netherite upgrades.

**Diamond ore spawns:** Y=-64 to Y=16 only. Peak density at Y=-59.
**Recommended mining level: Y=-54 to Y=-58** — same high diamond density but fewer lava lake hazards (lava lakes cluster around Y=-54; mining one level above reduces lava exposure).

1. Dig a staircase from your base down to Y=-54.
2. Branch mine: dig a main corridor, then branch tunnels every 2 blocks to maximize coverage.
3. Diamond ore is blue-flecked dark stone (deepslate at this depth — dark gray, harder texture).
4. Mine only with iron pickaxe or better. Stone pickaxe cannot mine diamond ore.
5. Watch for lava — always have your water bucket ready. Lava flows at Y=-54 are common.
6. Minimum diamonds needed before nether: 5 (2 for enchanting table + 3 for diamond pickaxe).

#### Enchanting Setup

Requirements: 2 diamonds + 4 obsidian + 1 book = 1 enchanting table.
For maximum (level 30) enchantments: surround table with 15 bookshelves, each 2 blocks away with 1 air gap between shelf and table.

- **Book**: 3 paper (from sugarcane, 3 sugarcane in a row → 3 paper) + 1 leather.
- **Obsidian**: Form where water flows over a lava source block. Pour water bucket onto lava lake, then mine with diamond pickaxe. Requires 4 obsidian for enchanting table; 10 for nether portal.
- **Bookshelf**: 6 planks + 3 books → 1 bookshelf. Need 15 bookshelves total = 45 books = 135 paper = ~45 sugarcane.

#### Nether Portal

1. Collect 10 obsidian (minimum frame — 4 corners can be any block but obsidian is most common).
   - Standard portal frame: 4 wide × 5 tall = 10 obsidian for the frame only (corners excluded).
2. Craft Flint and Steel: 1 iron ingot + 1 flint (drops when mining gravel, ~10% chance per block).
3. Build the portal frame. Right-click the inside of the frame with flint and steel to ignite.
4. Step through the purple portal. Nether is dangerous — bring full iron armor minimum, food, and weapons.

### Biome-Specific Early Game

#### Desert

- No trees at spawn. Do not panic.
- Scan for an oasis (small water pool with trees nearby) or the edge of an adjacent forest.
- Travel up to 200–300 blocks in any direction before building a temporary shelter.
- Cacti drop XP when broken but are useless as tools. Sand can be smelted into glass.
- Villages are common in deserts — find one for instant shelter, beds, chests, and a crafting table.
- Emergency shelter: dig straight down 4 blocks into sand, place a block above your head to seal the hole.

#### Ocean / Beach

- Sand and gravel cannot be crafted into tools.
- Swim toward shore. There is always land within a few hundred blocks in a standard world.
- Sugar cane grows naturally on sand and dirt adjacent to water — grab it for paper (books, maps) but it is not urgent.
- Do not build shelter on the beach — it offers no stone. Travel inland.

#### Swamp / Mangrove Swamp

- Mangrove logs function identically to other logs. Harvest freely.
- Water is abundant — falling in is a minor nuisance, not a threat, unless you are overencumbered or have armor.
- Slimes spawn in swamp biomes at night (and underground in certain chunks). They are not a major threat — stone sword kills them in 2–3 hits.
- Bogged mobs (1.21 new hostile) spawn in swamps — treat like skeletons (ranged attackers, keep distance).
- Clay is common in swamp shallows — can be smelted into bricks for building.

#### Taiga / Snowy Taiga

- Spruce logs are abundant. Same as oak for all crafting purposes.
- Sweet berries grow on berry bushes. They restore 2 hunger each. Useful as emergency food but walking through the bush deals 1 damage per second — walk around them.
- Wolves spawn here. Do not attack wolves unless provoked. Tame with bones (from skeleton drops) by right-clicking the wolf repeatedly with bones until hearts appear.
- Powder snow patches (snowy biomes only): appear identical to regular snow but players sink in. Leather boots prevent sinking. Avoid until you have leather boots.

#### Plains

- Easiest biome to start. Grass, cows, sheep, pigs, and horses are all common.
- Villages appear frequently — if you find one, sleep in a bed immediately to set your spawn.
- Flat terrain means no natural shelter; build immediately.
- Tall grass is abundant → seeds are easy to collect → wheat farm is fastest here.

#### Mountain / Windswept Hills

- Stone is everywhere — stone tools are trivially fast to craft.
- Trees are sparse on peaks. Collect all logs you pass.
- Emerald ore spawns only in mountain biomes (Y=-16 to Y=256, best at Y=236). Not useful early game — worth noting for trading with villagers later.
- Fall damage is a serious hazard. Always pillar down (place a block under your feet as you descend) rather than jumping.
- Goats spawn here. They headbutt players if you stand still too long. Keep moving.

### Recovery After Death

#### Item Despawn Mechanics

- Dropped items persist for **5 real-time minutes** (6000 game ticks) once the chunk containing them is loaded.
- If you die far from base, items will not despawn until you re-enter the same chunk. Sprint directly to your death coordinates.
- Items dropped in spawn chunks (the area around world origin, ~320×320 blocks) despawn on the 5-minute timer regardless of your location.

#### Recovery Procedure

1. Note your coordinates before doing anything dangerous. Java Edition: F3 shows X/Y/Z in real time.
2. On respawn, immediately check your last known coordinates.
3. Sprint (double-tap W, or hold Ctrl) toward your death location. Do not stop to gather resources.
4. If you died to mobs: expect those mobs to still be present. Approach with whatever weapon you respawned with.
5. If you died in lava: items not made of netherite are gone. Do not waste time searching.
6. If you died from a fall into a cave: items are on the cave floor. Use your water bucket to descend safely.
7. **Recovery Compass** (1.19+, available in 1.21.1): crafted from echo shard + 8 compass = recovery compass. Points toward your last death location. Extremely useful in large cave systems.

#### Respawn Point

- **No bed set**: respawn at world spawn. This could be hundreds or thousands of blocks from your base.
- **Bed set but destroyed**: respawn at world spawn. You will see the message "Your home bed was missing or obstructed."
- **Bed set correctly**: respawn within 2–3 blocks of the bed.
- **Critical habit**: sleep in your bed every night, not just for mob skipping but to update your respawn point as your base moves.
- If you die far from base with no bed, use F3 (Java) or coordinates HUD (Bedrock) to navigate home. Consider placing a visible beacon, tall dirt pillar, or distinctive building at your base.
