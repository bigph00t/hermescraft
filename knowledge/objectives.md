# Minecraft 1.21.1 Gameplay Objectives

Each objective is a complete step-by-step plan. Prerequisites listed. Quantities are exact. Use these plans when deciding how to tackle a goal.

---

## Survival Essentials

### First 10 Minutes (Punch Tree to Shelter)
**Prerequisites:** None. You just spawned.

1. Punch the nearest tree trunk 4 times to collect 4 oak logs (or any wood type).
2. Open inventory. Craft logs into planks: 4 logs → 16 planks.
3. Craft a crafting table from 4 planks. Place it on the ground.
4. At the crafting table, craft 2 sticks from 2 planks.
5. Craft a wooden pickaxe: 3 planks + 2 sticks.
6. Find stone (grey blocks on hills or underground). Mine 3 stone blocks with the wooden pickaxe.
7. Craft a stone pickaxe: 3 cobblestone + 2 sticks.
8. Mine 3 more stone blocks. Craft a stone axe: 3 cobblestone + 2 sticks (optional but speeds wood gathering).
9. Gather 20 more logs using the axe by punching or chopping trees.
10. Find coal: black-speckled stone on cliff faces or in shallow caves. Mine 8–16 coal with the stone pickaxe.
11. Craft torches: 1 coal + 1 stick = 4 torches. Make 8–12 torches.
12. Find a hillside or flat area. Dig a 4-wide × 3-tall × 5-deep hole into a hill, or build a 5×5 square room with planks.
13. Place your crafting table inside. Block the entrance with planks or dirt, leaving a 1×2 doorway.
14. Place torches inside so light level stays above 7 (prevents mob spawns).
15. Craft a door: 6 planks arranged in a 2×3 column → 3 doors. Place one in the entrance.

**Result:** You have a lit shelter, stone tools, and torches before nightfall. You are safe to wait out the first night.

### Get Full Stone Tools
**Prerequisites:** Crafting table. At least 4 logs (for sticks). Access to stone or cobblestone.

1. Gather 12 cobblestone total (mine with wooden pickaxe or better).
2. Craft 4 sticks from 2 planks.
3. At crafting table, craft the following (uses 12 cobblestone, 4 sticks total):
   - Stone pickaxe: 3 cobblestone + 2 sticks
   - Stone axe: 3 cobblestone + 2 sticks
   - Stone shovel: 1 cobblestone + 2 sticks
   - Stone sword: 2 cobblestone + 1 stick
4. Optional: Craft a stone hoe (2 cobblestone + 2 sticks) if you plan to farm.

**Result:** Full stone tool set (pickaxe, axe, shovel, sword). Ready for efficient early-game gathering.

### Get Full Iron Gear (Exact Iron Counts Per Item)
**Prerequisites:** Stone pickaxe (minimum), furnace, 8 logs for fuel, crafting table.

Iron cost per item (in ingots):
- Iron pickaxe: 3
- Iron axe: 3
- Iron shovel: 1
- Iron sword: 2
- Iron helmet: 5
- Iron chestplate: 8
- Iron leggings: 7
- Iron boots: 4
- Shield: 1 iron + 6 planks (separate from armor set)

Total for pickaxe + sword + full armor + shield: 3 + 2 + 5 + 8 + 7 + 4 + 1 = **30 iron ingots minimum**. Add shovel and axe: +4 = **34 ingots for the complete set**.

1. Mine at least 34 raw iron ore. Iron is found Y=-24 to Y=56 (peak Y=16) and Y=80 to Y=232 in mountains.
2. Build a furnace: 8 cobblestone arranged in a ring pattern (leave center empty).
3. Place furnace. Load raw iron into top slot, logs or coal into fuel slot.
4. Each raw iron smelts in 10 seconds. 34 ingots requires 34 smelting cycles = ~6 minutes.
5. With 34 ingots, craft at crafting table:
   - Iron pickaxe (3): 3 ingots across top row + 2 sticks down center
   - Iron sword (2): 2 ingots in center column + 1 stick below
   - Iron helmet (5): 5 ingots — top 3 + side 2, no bottom row
   - Iron chestplate (8): 8 ingots — all except top-center
   - Iron leggings (7): 7 ingots — top 3 + sides × 2 rows, no boots row
   - Iron boots (4): 4 ingots — sides of bottom 2 rows
   - Shield (1 + planks): 1 ingot in top-center, 6 planks filling rest
6. Equip all armor pieces and put sword + shield in hotbar.

**Result:** Full iron armor + iron pickaxe + iron sword + shield. You can now mine diamonds, explore caves, and survive most overworld encounters.

### Set Up a Secure Base
**Prerequisites:** At least 64 cobblestone, 32 planks, 16 torches, a door, a crafting table, a furnace, a chest.

1. Choose a location: flat ground near resources (trees, cave entrance, water). Avoid swamps, ocean edges, and valleys with many mob spawns.
2. Clear a 9×9 footprint area on flat ground with your shovel.
3. Build walls 3 blocks high using cobblestone. 9×9 outer perimeter = 32 blocks perimeter. 3 tall = 96 wall blocks. Leave a 2-wide × 2-tall gap on one side for the door.
4. Build a roof using cobblestone or planks. 9×9 roof = 81 blocks.
5. Place torches every 4 blocks on interior walls so no dark corner exists (light level ≥ 8 everywhere inside).
6. Place a door in the entrance.
7. Inside: place crafting table, furnace, and chest(s) in the back corners.
8. Dig a 1×1 hole in the floor in the back corner, 3 deep, to create an emergency escape shaft.
9. Place a torch at the base of the shaft.
10. Outside: place torches every 6 blocks around the perimeter of the base exterior to prevent mob spawns within 24 blocks.

**Result:** A cobblestone safe house with no dark spawning spots inside, a lit perimeter, storage, crafting, and smelting stations.

### Build a Sustainable Food Source
**Prerequisites:** Hoe, 5+ wheat seeds or carrots or potatoes (from grass, villages, or farming), a water bucket or nearby water source, crafting table.

1. Find a flat area within 4 blocks of a water source (river, lake, or place a water bucket in a hole).
2. Till a 9×9 farmland grid with the hoe (81 farmland blocks). The central 1×1 water source hydrates all 80 surrounding blocks.
3. If no natural water nearby: craft a bucket (3 iron ingots), find water, right-click to fill, return, dig a 1×1 pit in the center of your 9×9, empty the bucket into it.
4. Plant seeds, carrots, or potatoes on all 80 farmland tiles.
5. Wait 10–30 minutes real time for crops to fully mature (or use bone meal to speed up each plant).
6. Harvest when fully grown. Wheat yields 1 wheat + 0-3 seeds. Carrots and potatoes yield 2-5 per plant.
7. Replant immediately. Keep 10+ seeds/crops in reserve for replanting.
8. Craft bread (3 wheat in a row) for portable food — each bread restores 5 hunger.
9. Add 2–4 chickens or cows in a separate pen nearby: breed with seeds/wheat. Cows give beef + leather. Chickens give eggs + feathers + raw chicken.

**Result:** A self-sustaining 9×9 crop farm producing 80+ food items per harvest cycle, plus optional animal farm for meat and materials.

### Create an XP Farm Setup
**Prerequisites:** Full iron gear, torches, trapdoors or slabs, buckets of water, a spawner location (cave dungeon) or a dark room design, sword.

**Option A: Spawner-Based (Fastest)**
1. Find a dungeon (mossy cobblestone room underground with a cage-like spawner block in center).
2. Note the spawner type (zombie, skeleton, or spider).
3. Clear all mobs currently in the room.
4. Place torches on and around the spawner to temporarily disable it.
5. Dig the room out to 9×9 around the spawner, 5 blocks tall.
6. Remove all torches from the room (leave them on the spawner only if you want to pause it).
7. Build a 2×2 water channel in one corner that pushes mobs to a corner hole.
8. The corner hole drops mobs down 22 blocks (leaves them at 1 HP).
9. At the bottom, stand at the landing zone and hit mobs once to kill and collect XP.
10. Remove the torches from the spawner to activate it. Mobs begin spawning and flowing.

**Option B: Dark Room (No Spawner)**
1. Build a 16×16×3 room at least 24 blocks underground (Y=40 or below, away from caves).
2. Seal all light sources. Floor must be completely dark.
3. Build a 1-block-wide channel around the perimeter with water flowing to a corner.
4. Dig a 22-block drop shaft at the collection corner.
5. Stand at collection point and kill weakened mobs.

**Result:** Reliable XP source. Level 1–30 achievable in 5–15 minutes depending on spawn rates. Use for enchanting pickaxes, armor, and books.

## Resource Acquisition

### Get 64 Cobblestone Efficiently
**Prerequisites:** Any pickaxe (wooden or better).

1. Find exposed stone on a hillside, cliff, or in a shallow cave. Stone is extremely common at any Y level below surface.
2. Mine in a straight line into the hill, 64 blocks in or across a wide stone wall.
3. Stone drops cobblestone when mined (without Silk Touch). Each block = 1 cobblestone.
4. A straight 4-wide × 4-tall × 4-deep cut into a hill yields 64 cobblestone in under 2 minutes with a stone pickaxe.

**Result:** 64 cobblestone in 1–2 minutes. Used for furnaces, base walls, tools.

---

### Get a Stack of Wood Quickly (64 Logs)
**Prerequisites:** Axe (any material). Stone or iron axe preferred.

1. Find a forest biome or any cluster of trees (oak, birch, spruce, dark oak, jungle, acacia, cherry, mangrove — all count).
2. Target large trees first. A single large oak can yield 10–15 logs. A jungle tree yields 30–50 logs.
3. Chop from the base upward. After cutting the bottom, jump and chop higher trunks.
4. Collect all dropped logs before they despawn (5 minutes).
5. For oak or birch forests: chop 8–10 trees to reach 64 logs.
6. For spruce 2×2 mega trees: 2 trees typically yield 64+ logs.
7. Leave saplings on the ground to auto-collect when you walk over them (for replanting).

**Result:** 64 logs in 3–8 minutes. Converts to 256 planks. Enough for most early-game building and crafting.

### Get Iron in Bulk (Mine Trip Planning)
**Prerequisites:** Stone pickaxe (minimum), 16+ torches, food (8+ hunger bars), sword for cave mobs.

1. Eat food until hunger bar is full.
2. Load hotbar: stone/iron pickaxe, sword, torches, food, shovel.
3. Find a cave entrance or dig a staircase down to Y=16 (press F3 to see coordinates).
4. At Y=16, mine in a long tunnel heading in any cardinal direction.
5. Branch mine: every 3 blocks along the main tunnel, dig side tunnels 16 blocks deep to the left and right.
6. Iron ore appears as orange-spotted grey stone. Mine every iron ore you see.
7. Branch-mine approach at Y=16 covers approximately 1 ore per 6–10 blocks traveled.
8. For a 64-ingot haul (enough for full armor set + tools): expect to mine for 20–40 minutes in tunnels.
9. Return to surface when pickaxe durability reaches 20% or food is below 4 hunger.
10. Smelt all raw iron at furnace. Fuel: use coal, wood, or any logs (1 log smelts 1.5 items).

**Result:** 30–80 iron ingots depending on time spent. Enough for full iron set (34 ingots) per trip.

### Get Diamonds (Branch Mine at Y=-58)
**Prerequisites:** Iron pickaxe (required — stone pickaxe cannot mine diamonds), 32+ torches, food, sword, armor recommended.

1. Eat food. Equip best available armor.
2. Dig a staircase straight down from Y=0 to Y=-58. Count steps using F3 Y coordinate.
3. At Y=-58, dig a main corridor heading north or east, 50+ blocks long.
4. Every 3 blocks, dig perpendicular branches 16 blocks in each direction.
5. Diamond ore appears as bright blue-teal flecks in deepslate (dark grey stone at this depth).
6. Mine every diamond ore with iron pickaxe or better. Do NOT use stone pickaxe — drops nothing.
7. Use Fortune III pickaxe if available: each ore drops 1–4 diamonds instead of 1.
8. Typical yield: 1–2 diamond ore per 20 blocks of branch tunnel. Full branch-mine session (1 hour): 10–30 diamonds.
9. Watch for lava pools at this depth. Never mine directly below you — lava can flow up and destroy drops.
10. If diamonds fall into lava: you lose them. Always check ahead before mining.
11. Return to surface when pickaxe at 20% durability or inventory near full.
12. Craft: diamond pickaxe (3), diamond sword (2), diamond axe (3), full diamond armor (24) = 35 diamonds total for full set.

**Result:** Diamond haul. Each full mining session yields 10–30 diamonds. Full diamond gear requires 35 diamonds.

### Get Obsidian (10 for Portal, 14 for Full Portal Frame)
**Prerequisites:** Diamond pickaxe (only pickaxe that can mine obsidian), water bucket, lava source.

**Minimum portal:** 10 obsidian blocks. Full rectangle portal: 14 blocks (4-wide × 5-tall frame with corners).

1. Find a lava lake. Common underground Y=-55 to Y=-63 (near bedrock layer). Also visible in surface lava pools in badlands or mountains.
2. Fill a water bucket at any water source.
3. Stand at the edge of the lava lake.
4. Pour water onto the lava surface by right-clicking the water bucket at the lava edge.
5. Water flowing over lava converts it to obsidian (top layer of lava source blocks).
6. Mine each obsidian block with the diamond pickaxe. Each block takes ~10 seconds to mine.
7. Pick up water bucket again after collecting obsidian (right-click a flowing water block).
8. Repeat for each lava block.
9. For 10 obsidian: mine a 10-block patch of converted lava. Takes about 2 minutes of mining.

**Alternate (Pre-placed Lava):** Bring a lava bucket, pour lava in a flat shape matching your portal needs, pour water to convert, mine.

**Result:** 10–14 obsidian blocks for building a Nether portal. Diamond pickaxe durability cost: ~10–14 uses (very low).

### Get Leather
**Prerequisites:** Sword, open plains or forest biome.

**Option A: Kill Cows (Fastest)**
1. Locate cows — most common in plains, forest, and savanna biomes.
2. Kill 6–10 cows with a sword. Each cow drops 0–2 leather (average 1 per cow).
3. Cows also drop 1–3 raw beef per kill. Cook it in a furnace for cooked beef (high saturation food).
4. 6 leather = saddle trade with leatherworker villager. 7 leather = 1 book. 24 leather = set of leather armor (28 for boots too).

**Option B: Breed Cows for Sustainable Supply**
1. Build a 5×5 fenced pen (20 fence posts, 1 fence gate).
2. Lure 2 cows into the pen using wheat (hold wheat in hand, they follow).
3. Feed both cows wheat: right-click each with wheat. Hearts appear = breeding.
4. Wait 5 minutes for baby cow to grow to adult (or use 8 wheat on baby to speed growth).
5. Keep 2 adults always. Kill any extras for leather.

**Option C: Trade with Villagers**
1. Find a village. Look for a leatherworker (brown apron, works at a cauldron).
2. Leatherworker novice trade: 6 leather → 1 emerald (sell leather, not buy it). Not useful for acquiring leather.
3. Leather is not easily bought from villagers — kill cows instead.

**Result:** 6–10 leather per hunting trip. Used for: books (1 each), leather armor (24–28 total), saddle trades, item frames (1 leather + 8 sticks each).

### Get String
**Prerequisites:** Sword, nighttime or cave access.

**Option A: Kill Spiders at Night**
1. Wait until night (or go into a cave).
2. Spiders spawn at night on the surface at light level ≤ 7.
3. Kill spiders with sword. Each spider drops 0–2 string (average 1 per spider).
4. Cave spiders (found in mineshafts) are smaller and more dangerous but same drops.
5. Kill 10–20 spiders to get a reliable supply of 10–20 string.

**Option B: Find a Mineshaft**
1. Mineshafts generate underground, often accessible via deep caves.
2. Cobwebs in mineshafts break into 1 string each when hit with a sword.
3. A single mineshaft typically has 50–200 cobwebs = 50–200 string.
4. Clear mineshaft cobwebs systematically — huge string yield in 5–10 minutes.

**String uses:** Bow (3 string + 3 sticks), fishing rod (3 sticks + 2 string), wool (4 string = 1 wool), lead (4 string + 1 slimeball).

**Result:** 10–20 string per night hunting trip. Mineshaft cobweb clearing: 50–200 string per session.

### Get Gunpowder
**Prerequisites:** Sword, iron armor recommended, open area for safe combat.

1. Go outside at night or enter a cave. Creepers spawn at light level ≤ 7.
2. Spot creepers at a distance — they are green, no legs visible (actually 4 stubby legs), silent approach.
3. Do NOT let creepers get within 3 blocks — they will start their 1.5-second fuse countdown then explode.
4. Attack strategy: sprint-hit and back up immediately. Hit the creeper once, then back up beyond 3 blocks before it can activate.
5. Repeat sprint-hit-retreat until it dies (creepers have 20 HP, sword does 6–10 damage per hit = 3–4 hits).
6. Creepers drop 0–2 gunpowder (average 1 per kill). Looting III increases to 0–5.
7. Kill 10–20 creepers for a reliable gunpowder supply.
8. Gunpowder uses: TNT (4 sand + 5 gunpowder), fireworks (1 gunpowder + 1 paper), splash potions (brewing).

**Result:** 10–20 gunpowder per night session. Used for TNT, fireworks, and splash/lingering potions.

### Get Bone Meal in Bulk
**Prerequisites:** Composter (3 wooden slabs), OR skeleton spawner/grinder, OR excess crops.

**Option A: Composting Crops (Sustainable)**
1. Craft a composter: 7 wooden slabs arranged in a U-shape (bottom + sides, open top). Costs 7 slabs = 7 planks.
2. Place the composter.
3. Right-click it with organic items: seeds, wheat, saplings, leaves, melons, pumpkins, vegetables.
4. Each item has a chance to fill a layer (0–100% depending on item type). Seeds: 30%. Wheat: 65%. Melons/pumpkins: 65%. Tall grass: 30%.
5. After 7 layers fill, right-click to collect 1 bone meal.
6. Compost ratio: approximately 8–14 items per bone meal on average.

**Option B: Skeleton Farm (Fastest)**
1. Build a skeleton mob farm (see XP Farm Setup, Option A with skeleton spawner).
2. Skeletons drop 0–2 bones each. Looting III: up to 5 bones per kill.
3. Each bone crafts into 3 bone meal at a crafting table (1 bone = 3 bone meal, no table needed — just 1×1 slot).
4. A 10-minute session at a skeleton farm can yield 20–50 bones = 60–150 bone meal.

**Result:** Bone meal is essential for: instant crop growth, tree farming (saplings to instant-trees), dye crafting (white dye from bone meal), and bonemeal dispensers.

### Get Sand and Glass
**Prerequisites:** Shovel (any material), furnace with fuel, for glass.

1. Travel to a desert biome (tan/yellow terrain), a beach, or a riverbank — all have sand on the surface.
2. Sand blocks on the surface are exposed and fast to mine with a shovel.
3. Mine 64 sand: takes under 1 minute with an iron shovel in a desert.
4. For glass: place sand in furnace top slot, fuel (coal or wood) in bottom slot.
5. Each sand smelts into 1 glass in 10 seconds. 64 sand = 64 glass = ~10 minutes smelting.
6. Glass pane crafting: 6 glass in a 3×2 grid = 16 glass panes (much more coverage than full glass blocks).

**Desert temples** are also found in deserts — loot while collecting sand.

**Result:** 64 glass blocks from 64 sand. Glass panes stretch this to cover 170+ blocks of window space.

## Building Objectives

### Build a Simple Shelter (5 Minutes, Minimal Materials)
**Prerequisites:** 16 dirt or cobblestone, 2 torches, 1 door or 4 planks for a door.

1. Find a hillside with exposed flat wall of dirt or stone.
2. Dig a 3-wide × 2-tall × 4-deep hole into the hill with a shovel or pickaxe.
3. Place 2 torches on the walls inside to light it.
4. Block the entrance with dirt or cobblestone, leaving a 1×2 opening.
5. Craft a door (6 planks in a 2×3 grid = 3 doors) and place in opening.
6. Stand inside. You are safe from mobs that cannot open doors (most).

**Result:** Minimal safe shelter usable in under 5 minutes with zero pre-gathered materials beyond what spawns near you. Zombies can break down doors on Hard difficulty — brace the door with a block behind it.

### Build a Proper House (Full Detail)
**Prerequisites:** 150+ cobblestone, 64+ planks, 32+ glass panes, 16 torches, 2 doors, crafting table, furnace.

Material list:
- Cobblestone: 150 (walls and floor)
- Planks: 64 (interior, roof detail, furniture base)
- Glass panes: 16 (windows)
- Torches: 16 (interior + exterior lighting)
- Door: 2 (front + back)
- Stairs: 32 cobblestone stairs (roof eaves)
- Slabs: 16 cobblestone slabs (roof top layer)

Steps:
1. Clear a 9×9 footprint area.
2. Lay the floor: fill the 9×9 interior with cobblestone or planks (81 blocks).
3. Build walls 4 blocks high around the 9×9 perimeter: 32 blocks per row × 4 = 128 wall blocks. Leave 2 openings for doors (front and back, each 1-wide × 2-tall).
4. Add windows: on each wall face, remove 2–4 wall blocks at Y=2 (eye level) and fill with glass panes.
5. Place doors in both doorways.
6. Build the roof: start a cobblestone stair layer at wall top, angling upward. Create a peaked roof by stacking stairs inward each layer until they meet at the ridge. Fill the ridge with slabs.
7. Interior: place crafting table, furnace, 4 large chests against walls, a bed (3 wool + 3 planks).
8. Lighting: torches on each wall at 2-block height. Place 4 torches outside around the entrance.
9. Exterior: surround the base with cobblestone walkway 1 block wide. Place torches on exterior walls.

**Result:** A fully enclosed, lit, furnished house with storage, crafting, smelting, and a bed for setting spawn point. Covers all basic survival needs.

### Build a Farm with Fence and Gate
**Prerequisites:** Hoe, water source nearby, 24 fence posts (crafted: 2 sticks + 2 planks, horizontal, per 2 fence blocks), 2 fence gates, 1 water bucket.

1. Choose a flat area adjacent to or within 4 blocks of water.
2. Clear a 10×10 area with shovel.
3. Build a fence perimeter: place fence posts around the 10×10 edge (40 fence blocks total for perimeter). Leave a 1-block gap for the gate on the short wall.
4. Place 2 fence gates in the gap (gates are 1 wide, use 2 for a 2-wide opening).
5. Inside, dig a 1×1 hole in the center and fill with water bucket.
6. Hoe all interior dirt blocks (8×8 = 64 farmland blocks, leaving the center water hole).
7. Plant seeds, carrots, or potatoes on all 64 farmland tiles.
8. Place 2 torches inside the farm near the water for night light (light level ≥ 9 for crop growth).
9. To keep animals out: fence gates stop animals when closed. Close gates when not farming.

**Result:** A 64-plot fenced farm protected from animal trampling. Produces 64–256 crops per full harvest depending on crop type.

### Build a Storage Building with Organized Chests
**Prerequisites:** 100+ cobblestone, 128+ planks or sticks (for chests), 32 signs or labels, 16 torches.

Chest crafting: 8 planks in a ring = 1 chest = 27 storage slots. Large chest: 2 chests side by side = 54 slots.

1. Build a dedicated 7×9 building (same method as proper house but smaller).
2. Inside, build 2 rows of large chests along both long walls: 4 large chests per wall = 8 large chests total = 432 storage slots.
3. Leave a 3-block-wide central walkway between the rows.
4. Label each chest with a sign (crafted: 6 planks + 1 stick = 3 signs): categories such as — Ores, Stone, Wood, Food, Tools, Armor, Mob Drops, Rare/Misc.
5. Suggested sorting:
   - Row 1: Raw materials (ores, logs, stone, sand, gravel, clay, dirt)
   - Row 2: Processed (ingots, planks, glass, bricks, wool)
   - Row 3: Food (bread, meat, vegetables, seeds)
   - Row 4: Combat (weapons, armor, mob drops, potions)
   - Row 5: Building (slabs, stairs, fences, doors, torches)
   - Row 6: Rare/Special (diamonds, emeralds, ender pearls, blaze rods, books)
6. Place shulker boxes (late game) for compact category storage.

**Result:** Organized 432-slot storage system. Labels prevent inventory chaos. Scales by adding more chests as needed.

### Build a Mine Entrance with Ladder Shaft
**Prerequisites:** 32 cobblestone, 20 ladders (crafted: 7 sticks = 3 ladders), 16 torches, pickaxe.

1. Choose your mine entrance location: ideally near base, away from other structures.
2. Dig a 3×3 shaft straight down to your desired depth (Y=16 for iron, Y=-58 for diamonds). Mark depth with F3.
3. At the shaft top, build a 3×3 cobblestone frame 2 blocks above ground to mark the entrance visually.
4. On one inner wall of the shaft, place ladders all the way down (attach to wall blocks by right-clicking the wall face).
5. Place torches every 6 blocks down the shaft on the opposite wall from ladders.
6. At the bottom, hollow out a 5×5 base room. Place crafting table, furnace, 2 chests (supplies cache), and torches.
7. From the base room, begin your branch-mine tunnels heading in cardinal directions.
8. Mark explored tunnels with signs or different block types at entrances.

**Result:** A safe, lit shaft to mining depth with ladder access, a base camp with caching station, and clear tunnel organization. Prevents getting lost underground.

### Build a Dock or Pier at Water
**Prerequisites:** 64 planks, 16 oak fence posts, 32 oak wood blocks, 4 torches, 1 trapdoor (optional for hatch).

1. Find a lake, river, or ocean edge.
2. Build a foundation platform 3 blocks wide extending from the shore into the water. Place planks directly on or just above the water surface (stand on shore, place out over water).
3. Extend the platform 10–15 blocks into the water.
4. Add fence posts along both edges of the platform for railings: 2 rows × 10–15 = 20–30 fence posts.
5. At the end of the dock, build a small 3×3 platform (the dock head) for a boat launch area.
6. Place torches at platform entry point and at the dock end.
7. Optional: hang a trapdoor at the dock end that opens flush with the water for easy boat boarding.
8. Optional: build a small dock house (3×3, 2 high) at the shore end for storage.

**Result:** A functional dock. Useful as a landmark, boat launch, and aesthetic structure. Boats can be placed in water at the dock end for easy travel.

### Build Walls Around a Settlement
**Prerequisites:** 400+ cobblestone (for a 20×20 perimeter wall), pickaxe, 20+ torches.

Wall perimeter for a 20×20 area: 20×4 sides = 80 blocks per layer. 3 layers high = 240 blocks. Add battlements (top layer alternating) = ~300 total.

1. Mark out the settlement boundary using temporary blocks (dirt or sand) as corner markers.
2. For a 20×20 settlement: place corner markers 20 blocks apart in a square.
3. Connect corner markers with a foundation trench 1 deep. Fill with cobblestone to ground level.
4. Build the wall 3 blocks high on the foundation.
5. Add a gate (2 fence gates, or a 2-wide gap with door) on the south-facing wall.
6. Add battlements at the top: alternate full blocks and half-removed (slab height) blocks across the top layer for a crenellation look.
7. Place torches on the interior side of the wall every 6 blocks to light the perimeter.
8. Optionally, add a 1-block-wide walkway at the top by placing slabs inside the wall-top.
9. Dig a 1×1 moat 2 blocks deep around the outside of the wall and fill with water from a bucket (extend the moat water with more buckets of water at each end — water spreads 7 blocks).

**Result:** A defensive perimeter wall around your settlement, with a gate entrance, lit interior perimeter, and optional moat. Prevents zombie, skeleton, and creeper intrusion.

### Build a Road Between Two Points
**Prerequisites:** 64+ gravel or cobblestone or dirt path blocks, shovel, hoe (for dirt paths).

1. Identify the two endpoints (e.g., base to mine entrance, or base to village).
2. Walk the route and mark it with temporary dirt pillars at each turning point.
3. Clear the path of obstacles: chop trees, fill holes with dirt, flatten bumps.
4. The path should be 3 blocks wide (center + 1 each side).
5. Material choice:
   - Dirt path: right-click dirt blocks with hoe to turn them into permanent path blocks (darker, flat-topped, no grass regrowth).
   - Gravel: place gravel for a natural-looking road. Slightly uneven.
   - Cobblestone: most durable and permanent. Best for main roads.
6. Place torches or lanterns every 8 blocks along the road center so it is lit at night.
7. Add a 1-block-wide border of different material (grass, stone, or fencing) on each side of the road for aesthetic contrast.

**Result:** A navigable, lit road connecting two points. Speeds travel, prevents getting lost, and looks intentional in the world.

## Farming Objectives

### Set Up a Wheat Farm (Step by Step)
**Prerequisites:** Hoe (any material), water source or bucket, wheat seeds (from breaking tall grass or harvesting wheat), 2 torches.

1. Find a flat area.
2. Dig a 1×1 hole in the center of a future 9×9 plot. Place water in it (bucket or adjacent to water source).
3. Till all 8×8 = 64 dirt blocks surrounding the water hole with the hoe: right-click each dirt or grass block.
4. Plant seeds: right-click each farmland tile with wheat seeds.
5. Wheat has 8 growth stages (0–7). Each stage takes approximately 5 Minecraft minutes per stage in good light.
6. Place 2 torches at the edge of the plot so light level ≥ 9 on all farmland (allows growth at night).
7. Harvest wheat when all stems are golden and fully grown (stage 7). Right-click or break with hoe.
8. Each plant yields 1 wheat + 0–3 seeds. With 64 plants, expect 64 wheat + 64–192 seeds.
9. Replant seeds immediately (keep 64 seeds reserved before eating extras).
10. Wheat crafting: 3 wheat in a row = 1 bread (5 hunger). 3×3 wheat = 9 bread from 64 wheat.

**Result:** A 64-plot wheat farm producing ~64 wheat per harvest. Harvest every 10–20 real minutes. Bread supply for sustained play.

### Set Up an Animal Farm (Pen, Breeding, Feeding)
**Prerequisites:** 20+ fence posts (3 planks + 2 sticks = 3 fences per craft), 2 fence gates, wheat/seeds/carrots (breeding food), sword.

1. Build a 7×7 fenced pen: 24 fence blocks for perimeter, 1 fence gate.
2. Locate animals to bring: cows (follow wheat), sheep (follow wheat), pigs (follow carrots), chickens (follow seeds).
3. Hold the appropriate food in hand and walk back to the pen — animals follow within 8 blocks.
4. Lead 2 of each desired animal into the pen. Close the gate.
5. Breed the pair: right-click each animal with the breeding food. Hearts appear. Baby spawns after 5 seconds.
6. Wait 5 minutes for baby to grow into adult. You can speed growth by feeding the baby (1 food = 10% growth).
7. Breeding cooldown: 5 minutes per animal. Keep only 2 breeding adults + kill adults beyond 10 per pen.
8. Each cow kill drops: 1–3 raw beef + 0–2 leather. Cook beef in furnace: cooked beef = 8 hunger + 12.8 saturation (best early food).
9. Separate pens for each animal type. Label with signs.

**Result:** Self-replenishing animal farm. Infinite food, leather, wool, and feathers. Breed each pen every 5 minutes for steady population.

### Set Up a Tree Farm (Spacing, Sapling Collection, Bone Meal)
**Prerequisites:** 8+ saplings (any wood type — oak, birch, spruce, dark oak, jungle, acacia, cherry), bone meal (optional for growth), axe.

Spacing rules by tree type:
- Oak/Birch: plant 3 blocks apart minimum (1-block air gap between each). 3×3 grid spacing.
- Spruce: 2×2 formation for mega trees, or 3 blocks apart for regular.
- Dark Oak: must be planted in 2×2 formation — 4 saplings per tree, no exceptions.
- Jungle: 2×2 for mega tree (requires bone meal), or single sapling 3 blocks apart.

1. Choose a flat area with open sky above (trees grow up to 10 blocks tall).
2. Till the ground — saplings can be planted directly on dirt or grass, no tilling needed.
3. Plant saplings in your chosen grid (oak example: 4×4 grid with 3-block gaps = 16 saplings in a 13×13 area).
4. Apply bone meal to each sapling: right-click with bone meal. Tree grows instantly (if air space above allows).
5. Without bone meal: trees grow randomly, ~5–30 minutes per tree.
6. When cutting trees: always collect all leaf blocks by breaking them (chance to drop sapling 5%).
7. Or simply walk under canopy — leaves decay automatically if trunk is cut, dropping saplings within 6 minutes.
8. Target: 1 sapling per tree cut, to maintain or grow your supply.

**Result:** Renewable wood farm. 16 oak trees in a 13×13 plot yields ~160 logs per harvest with bone meal acceleration.

### Set Up a Sugar Cane Farm
**Prerequisites:** 10+ sugar cane pieces (found on riverbanks, beaches), hoe or shovel, water source.

Sugar cane must be planted on sand, dirt, or grass that is directly adjacent (same Y-level) to water.

1. Find a water channel or dig a long trench and fill with water.
2. On the bank of the water trench, lay a flat row of sand or dirt blocks running parallel to the water.
3. Plant sugar cane on every sand/dirt block adjacent to water: right-click the block with sugar cane.
4. Sugar cane grows 1 block per Minecraft day (20 real minutes) up to 3 blocks tall.
5. Harvest the top 2 blocks when it reaches 3 tall (leave the bottom block planted — it regrows).
6. For a 20-block row of sugar cane: yields 40 sugar cane per full harvest cycle.
7. Sugar cane uses: paper (3 cane = 3 paper), sugar (1 cane = 1 sugar for cakes/potions), books (3 paper + 1 leather).

**Result:** A 20-block sugar cane farm producing 40 sugar cane per harvest. Replant once, harvest indefinitely.

### Automate Crop Harvesting (Water Flow, Collection Point)
**Prerequisites:** Harvested and established crop farm (wheat, carrots, or potatoes), 1 water bucket, 1 hopper (5 iron ingots + 1 chest), 1 chest, slabs.

1. Build your 9×9 crop farm as normal (see Wheat Farm objective).
2. At one edge of the farm, dig a 1×1 trench running the full 9-block width, 1 block below farmland level.
3. Place a chest at the end of the trench. Place a hopper on top of the chest pointing into it.
4. When ready to harvest: place 1 water bucket on the opposite end of the farm at farmland height.
5. Water flows across all farmland, breaking all crops and pushing them toward the trench.
6. Items fall into the trench and slide toward the hopper and into the chest.
7. Pick up the water bucket on the far side before all crops wash away (or place a block to stop water spread at the trench edge).
8. Replant: the farmland survives water flowing (hydration maintained). Immediately replant seeds.
9. Note: the water source will wash away any replanted seeds. Only run water harvest when ready to collect everything at once, then replant dry.

**Result:** Near-instant harvest collection. All drops auto-collected into a chest. Reduces 5-minute manual harvest to 30-second flush-and-collect.

## Exploration Objectives

### Explore Until You Find a Village
**Prerequisites:** Full stone tools, 16 torches, 8+ bread or food, map (optional: craft with compass + paper).

Villages generate in: plains, savanna, taiga, snowy tundra, desert, sunflower plains. They do NOT generate in forests, jungles, swamps, or oceans.

1. Head in one cardinal direction (pick north, south, east, or west and commit to it).
2. Travel 500–2000 blocks in that direction before changing course.
3. Watch for these village indicators: lit torches at night visible from a distance, crop fields at ground level, flat square building roofs, sounds of villagers (hmm sounds).
4. If no village found in 2000 blocks, shift 45 degrees and travel another 1000 blocks.
5. Villages generate roughly every 500–1500 blocks in biomes that support them.
6. Desert biomes: look for sandstone towers and flat-roofed buildings.
7. Plains: wood and cobblestone houses with hay bales.
8. Savanna: acacia wood structures, stone paths.
9. On arrival: do NOT attack villagers. Trade with them. Sleep in a bed to set spawn.
10. Village loot: chests in houses contain iron, food, emeralds, iron tools, sometimes enchanted books.

**Result:** A village discovered. Provides beds, villager trades, a resupply point, and a population center for future operations.

### Find and Loot a Desert Temple Safely
**Prerequisites:** Sword, shovel, 4 empty inventory slots, pickaxe.

Desert temples contain 4 chests buried beneath a colored wool floor pattern in the center room. They are protected by a pressure plate TNT trap.

1. Locate a desert biome. Travel through it watching for large sandstone structures (orange-tan, 15+ blocks tall, with distinctive patterned top).
2. Enter from the top (climb the exterior wall or climb in through the side opening above ground).
3. Inside: find the central room. Identify the colored wool floor pattern with a single pressure plate in the center.
4. **CRITICAL: Do NOT step on the pressure plate.** It detonates 9 TNT blocks directly below, destroying all 4 chests and their contents.
5. Instead: dig down through the floor beside the pressure plate (dig at the edge of the orange/blue pattern, not the center).
6. Drop into the chamber below. You will see 4 chests arranged in a square pattern.
7. The pressure plate wire is visible as a string-like block. Destroy the pressure plate from below to neutralize the trap.
8. Loot all 4 chests. Contents: random items including: iron/gold ingots, emeralds, diamonds (rare), bones, rotten flesh, horse armor, saddles, enchanted books (rare).
9. Also search the surface rooms: sometimes chests above ground.

**Result:** Temple looted safely. Expected average: 1–3 iron ingots, 1–2 gold ingots, 1–2 emeralds, bones/rotten flesh, ~5% chance per chest of diamonds or enchanted book.

### Find and Explore a Mineshaft
**Prerequisites:** Iron armor, iron sword, shield, 32 torches, 2 stacks food, iron pickaxe, bucket of water (for cave spiders — putting out fire from web burning is useful, not required).

1. Explore caves by following cave openings found at the surface or while mining underground.
2. Mineshafts generate between Y=-20 and Y=50. Look for wooden support beams, rail tracks, and cobweb-filled corridors.
3. Signs of a mineshaft from caves: wood planks appearing in cave walls, rail tracks embedded in the ground, the sound of cave spiders (higher-pitched than regular spiders).
4. Enter with torches ready. Place a torch at every junction and turning point so you can trace your way back.
5. Clear cobwebs with sword (yields string). Cobwebs slow movement — don't get stuck surrounded by mobs.
6. Cave spiders are poisonous and fit through 1×1 gaps. Kill with sword from distance. Milk from cows cures poison (or wait it out — poison can't kill, only reduce to 1 HP).
7. Loot minecart chests (brown chest sprites on rails): contain diamonds, gold, iron, redstone, enchanted books, melon seeds, rail, coal, bread.
8. Collect rail tracks for redstone projects or trading.
9. Mark explored sections with torches on the right-hand wall (consistent rule: left side unexplored, right side explored). This prevents circular navigation.
10. Exit the same way you entered, following your torch trail.

**Result:** A looted mineshaft. Expected yield: 3–8 minecart chests with ~3 items each (iron, gold, food, rails, seeds, occasional diamonds), 50–200 string from cobwebs.

### Map Your Surroundings (Systematic Exploration Spiral)
**Prerequisites:** Crafting table, 4 maps (optional but helpful), compass (4 iron + 1 redstone), paper (3 sugar cane), food supply for extended travel.

1. Start at your base. Craft a map: 1 compass in center + 8 paper surrounding = 1 empty map.
2. Right-click the empty map to create a filled map centered on your current position.
3. Explore in a clockwise spiral:
   - Step 1: Travel 100 blocks North, marking notable landmarks mentally or with named signs.
   - Step 2: Travel 100 blocks East.
   - Step 3: Travel 200 blocks South.
   - Step 4: Travel 200 blocks West.
   - Step 5: Travel 300 blocks North. Continue expanding the spiral.
4. As you move, the map auto-fills showing terrain, structures, and biome coloring.
5. When the map is fully filled (128×128 blocks by default scale), craft another map and zoom it out: surround the filled map with 8 more paper to create a zoomed-out version (2× area, 256×256).
6. Mark villages, temples, and key landmarks with signs: place a sign at the landmark, write its name, then place a sign at your base pointing direction and approximate distance.
7. Use `/locate` command if on a server (or the mod provides it) to find structures instantly.

**Result:** A documented understanding of your local geography. Villages, biomes, resources, and hazards all catalogued for future planning.

### Find a Specific Biome
**Prerequisites:** Food, a direction to travel, patience.

1. Stand on a high point (mountain or tall tower) and survey the horizon for color differences: orange = badlands, green = jungle, white = snowy, tan = desert, dark blue/green = taiga.
2. If biome not visible, travel in one direction for 1000+ blocks before changing course. Biomes repeat but can be large.

**Prerequisites:** Full iron armor minimum, iron sword, iron pickaxe. A base to return to.

Gear checklist (minimum viable):

- Iron sword (or diamond): piglins, zombie pigmen, blazes

- Gold nuggets or gold armor piece: wearing any gold armor prevents piglin aggression

2. Stock 64+ food.
3. Pack bow + arrows.

### Build a Nether Portal (Exact Obsidian Placement)
**Prerequisites:** 10 obsidian (minimum frame) or 14 (full frame with corners), flint and steel (1 iron ingot + 1 flint), diamond pickaxe to mine obsidian.

Minimum portal frame (10 obsidian): a 4×5 rectangle of obsidian — 2 blocks wide × 3 tall inner opening, with a 1-block frame.

Layout (X = obsidian, _ = air):
```
X X X X
X _ _ X
X _ _ X
X _ _ X
X X X X
```
This is 4 wide × 5 tall = 20 positions, minus 6 inner air = 14 obsidian for full frame.
Minimum (skip corners): 10 obsidian.

1. Place 2 obsidian on the ground, 2 apart (the bottom of the portal).
2. On top of each bottom block, stack 3 obsidian (left pillar: 4 high total including bottom, right pillar: 4 high total).
3. Bridge the top with 2 obsidian (connecting left pillar top to right pillar top).
4. Confirm shape: bottom row 2 + left pillar 3 + right pillar 3 + top row 2 = 10 obsidian. Inner opening 2 wide × 3 tall.
5. Light the portal: equip flint and steel (right-click to craft: 1 iron ingot + 1 flint). Stand inside the frame. Right-click any inner obsidian face with the flint and steel.
6. Purple swirling portal fills the frame within 2 seconds.
7. Walk into the portal and wait 4 seconds without moving. You teleport to the Nether.

**Result:** Active Nether portal. The portal creates a linked exit portal in the Nether. Mark both with signs and coordinates.

### Find a Nether Fortress
**Prerequisites:** Active Nether portal, full iron gear, bow + arrows, fire resistance potion (strongly recommended).

Nether fortresses are massive castle structures made of nether bricks (dark red brick material). They spawn in all Nether biomes but primarily appear as large bridge/corridor structures floating above or built into netherrack terrain.

1. Enter the Nether through your portal.
2. Walk away from your portal in one direction (pick a cardinal direction relative to Nether coordinates).
3. Fortresses generate in north–south strips. Travel east or west to find a new strip. Traveling north–south stays within the same strip and may not find a fortress.
4. Travel up to 500–1000 Nether blocks east or west. Fortresses appear as large nether-brick bridge structures visible across lava seas or hovering in the sky.
5. Signs of a nearby fortress: blaze sounds (crackling roar), wither skeleton footstep sounds, nether wart plants growing near ground (they only grow naturally in fortresses).
6. When you spot nether bricks (dark red/maroon squares), follow the structure to its main corridors.
7. Mark your portal entrance with a beacon, distinctive pillar, or sign so you can return.
8. Stay within 500 Nether blocks (~4000 Overworld blocks) of your portal when exploring.

**Result:** Nether fortress located. Contains blaze spawners, wither skeleton spawns, nether wart gardens, and chests with loot.

### Get Blaze Rods (Fight Blaze Strategy)
**Prerequisites:** Iron or diamond sword, shield, bow + 32 arrows, fire resistance potion (optional but reduces damage by 80%), full iron armor.

Blazes are found in Nether fortresses on or near blaze spawners (located in open-air spawner rooms). They float, shoot 3-burst fire charges, and deal fire + contact damage. Each blaze drops 0–1 blaze rod (average 1 per kill). You need 7 blaze rods minimum for brewing stand (1) + 12 blaze powder for 12 Eyes of Ender.

1. Find the blaze spawner room in the fortress (an open cage-like room with a spawner block and a staircase).
2. Drink fire resistance potion before engaging (if you have one): prevents all fire damage for 3 minutes.
3. Stand below blazes when fighting: their fire charges fly in arcs, and standing directly below makes them harder to hit you.
4. Use sword: sprint toward a blaze on the ground (blazes descend to attack at close range), hit it 3–4 times (iron sword: 6 damage/hit, blazes have 20 HP = 4 hits). Back up to avoid contact damage after each swing.
5. Use bow for flying blazes: lead your shot slightly. Blazes take 3–5 arrows to kill.
6. Use shield to block fire charges: hold right-click with shield to deflect incoming shots.
7. Kill 14 blazes for 7+ blaze rods (average 1 per kill, need luck for 1:1 ratio). Plan for 20 kills to guarantee 7 drops.
8. Collect blaze rods immediately — they despawn in 5 minutes.

**Result:** 7+ blaze rods. Craft brewing stand (1 blaze rod + 3 cobblestone). Convert remaining 6 rods into 12 blaze powder for Eyes of Ender and potion fuel.

### Get Nether Wart
**Prerequisites:** Access to a Nether fortress, empty inventory slots.

Nether wart grows naturally in red sand gardens inside Nether fortresses. It requires soul sand to grow (a brown, slow-moving sand found in Nether Soul Sand Valley biomes or near fortresses).

1. Inside the Nether fortress, look for staircase rooms descending below the main corridors. Nether wart gardens are in recessed staircase areas on soul sand, usually near blaze spawner rooms.
2. Harvest all nether wart by breaking the plants. Mature wart drops 2–4 nether wart pieces. Young plants drop 1.
3. A single garden typically yields 5–20 nether wart on first discovery.
4. To farm nether wart: bring soul sand back to the Overworld (it works there). Plant nether wart on soul sand. It grows without water or light level requirements but very slowly.
5. You need: 1 nether wart per Awkward Potion base. 1 Awkward Potion is the base for every useful potion. Gather at least 20 nether wart for a starter brewing session.

**Result:** 10–30 nether wart from a fortress garden. Plant on soul sand for a renewable supply. Required for all brewing recipes.

## Combat Objectives

### Clear a Cave of Mobs (Preparation and Strategy)
**Prerequisites:** Iron sword, iron armor, shield, 16 torches, food (8+ bars), bow + 16 arrows.

1. Before entering: eat food until hunger bar is full.
2. Equip armor, hold sword in main hand, shield in off hand.
3. Enter cave during daytime so no new outdoor mobs spawn at the entrance.
4. Place a torch every 6 blocks along the ceiling or walls as you advance (light level 8+ prevents spawning).
5. Listen for mob sounds: zombies groan, skeletons click, spiders hiss, creepers are silent.
6. Engage enemies one at a time when possible. Draw them toward you with footsteps rather than charging into groups.
7. Shield usage: hold right-click with shield raised to block arrows (skeletons) and melee hits. A 1-second block window exists after successfully blocking a hit.
8. Creepers: back away when they start hissing (2-second fuse). Hit them quickly and retreat — kill them before they explode, or lead them into a dead end and run.
9. Spiders can climb walls. Clear ceilings with bow shots if they are on the ceiling.
10. After clearing: ensure all areas are lit. Return to dark corners and place torches anywhere that looks dim.
11. Look for chests, ores, and passages to mark or mine while the cave is clear.

**Result:** A safe, lit cave ready for mining. No mob respawns in lit areas.

### Survive a Night Outside (Emergency Shelter or Combat Stance)
**Prerequisites:** Sword (any material). Optional: bed, shield, bow.

If you are caught outside at night with no shelter:

**Option A — Emergency Dig-In**
1. Dig a 2×1 hole in the ground 2 deep (stand in it).
2. Cover the top with dirt from your inventory (look up and place 2 blocks).
3. You are safe until morning. Mobs cannot reach you underground.
4. Wait for the hostile mob sounds to stop (indicating daytime). Dig out.

**Option B — Pillar Defense**
1. Pillar up 4 blocks (place dirt blocks under your feet while jumping).
2. Mobs cannot reach you at 4 blocks height.
3. Shoot down with bow, or wait until day.
4. At sunrise (about 10 minutes real time), descend.

**Option C — Active Combat**
1. Find an open area with good visibility (no creepers sneaking up blind).
2. Carry torch and place them frequently to reduce spawn zones near you.
3. Kill mobs as they approach. Prioritize: skeletons first (ranged damage), creepers second (explosive), zombies last (slow).
4. Face mobs one at a time. Never let them flank you.
5. Dawn comes 10 minutes into the night cycle. Most hostile surface mobs burn in daylight.

**Result:** Survived the night. Return to base at dawn.

### Defeat a Raid
**Prerequisites:** Full iron armor (diamond preferred), iron/diamond sword, shield, bow + 64 arrows, 64 cooked food, golden apple (optional), fire resistance and strength potions (optional).

Raids are triggered by entering a village with the Bad Omen effect (obtained by killing a Pillager captain with a banner). Raids consist of 3–7 waves of pillagers, vindicators, evokers, and ravagers.

1. Before the raid starts: place beds in strategic spots around the village perimeter to reset spawn point near combat.
2. Build a high ground vantage point: a 3-high pillar with a flat 5×5 platform above the village center.
3. Wave 1–2: Pillagers only. Kill with bow from high ground. Pillagers have crossbows, so shield incoming bolts.
4. Wave 3+: Vindicators appear. They are melee-only but very fast and deal 19 damage per hit. Kill with bow first before they close distance.
5. Evokers (waves 5+): summon vexes (small flying spirits) and fangs. Kill the evoker immediately as priority — they cast effects that ignore shields.
6. Ravager (wave 4+): a large bull-like mob. Hit and retreat. Ravager charges and stuns. Stay mobile, circle it, sword-hit rapidly.
7. After last wave clears: collect loot from fallen enemies (emeralds, crossbows, axes, saddles, totems of undying from evokers).
8. Village produces a chest reward: Hero of the Village effect + merchant trades discounted permanently.

**Result:** Raid defeated. Reward: totem(s) of undying, emeralds, discounted villager trades, Hero of the Village status.

### Build a Mob Grinder (Design, Materials, Placement)
**Prerequisites:** Full iron tools, 100+ stone/cobblestone, 16 hoppers (80 iron + 16 chests), 4 water buckets, torches, high open area or flat underground space. Access to a dark 16×16 room.

Basic dark room mob grinder (no spawner):
1. Build the grinder at Y=30–40 in a location at least 24 blocks from your base or any lit area (mobs only spawn if no player-lit chunks nearby).
2. Build a 16×16 × 3-tall room with no light sources. Floor must be dirt or stone (mobs don't spawn on slabs).
3. Build a 2-block-wide channel on the perimeter of the room floor with water flowing toward one corner (water source at 3 of 4 sides, flowing toward the collection corner).

7. Passive operation: mobs that die from fall damage automatically drop items into hoppers.
8. Looting III sword at collection point dramatically increases rare drops.
9. Output: zombies drop rotten flesh and iron ingots (rare). Skeletons drop bones and arrows. Spiders drop string and eyes.

**Result:** A passive mob grinder that produces bones, arrows, string, rotten flesh, and XP. Run while doing other tasks. Returns to collect every 20–30 minutes.

## Creative and Aesthetic Objectives

Key decorative techniques in Minecraft:

   - Couch: place stair blocks facing outward with a slab beside them.
   - Shelves: place slabs along a wall at multiple heights, fill item frames on the wall above.
   - Fireplace: dig a 2×1 alcove into the floor, place campfire block, surround with stone brick.
4. **Lighting:** Replace raw torches with lanterns (crafted: 1 torch + iron nuggets). Use sea lanterns, glowstone, or shroomlights for different aesthetics.

7. **Bookshelves:** Place bookshelves around an enchanting table to boost enchanting levels (needs 15 bookshelves to reach max enchant).
8. **Banners:** Craft banners (6 wool + 1 stick) and customize with dye patterns at a loom. Hang at entrances and walls.

**Result:** A visually interesting, detailed interior that communicates personality and function. Transforms a functional box into a lived-in space.

### Create a Garden or Park Area
**Prerequisites:** Assorted flowers, saplings, grass seeds, bone meal, gravel/stone path materials, fences or hedges, water bucket.

1. Select an open area adjacent to your settlement, 15×15 minimum.
2. Create a natural border: place 2-high hedges (dark oak leaves or jungle leaves blocks) or low fences around the perimeter.
3. Path system: lay a 3-wide central path from entrance to focal point using gravel, stone, or dirt path. Add crossing paths branching left and right at the midpoint.
4. Planting:
   - Grass areas: apply bone meal to dirt blocks to grow tall grass and flowers spontaneously.
   - Intentional flowers: plant individual flowers from your inventory in color clusters.
   - Trees: plant 4–6 saplings around the garden edge with 4-block spacing. Apply bone meal to grow instantly.
5. Water feature: dig a 3×5 irregular pond in the garden center. Fill with water bucket, place lily pads on the surface.
6. Seating area: place 2–4 stair blocks around the pond as benches.
7. Lighting: place lanterns on fence posts at garden corners.
8. Focal point: center of the garden gets a flower pot on a pillar (3-high pillar with fence post top and pot), or a statue built from colored blocks.

**Result:** A park-like outdoor area with paths, plantings, a water feature, seating, and lighting. Provides a calm aesthetic space and serves as a public landmark.

### Build a Monument or Landmark
**Prerequisites:** 100–500 blocks of a signature material (stone, quartz, obsidian, colored concrete, prismarine), pickaxe, shovel, scaffolding (optional).

1. Choose a prominent location visible from your base — a hilltop, village center, or open plain.
2. Decide on monument type:
   - **Obelisk:** 3×3 base tapering to a single block spire. Height 20–30 blocks.
   - **Statue:** humanoid figure built with block approximation. Head = 3×3×2. Body = 3×5×3. Arms = 1×3×1 each. Legs = 2×4×1 each.
   - **Arch:** Two 10-high pillars bridged by a curved top (use stair blocks for arch curve).
   - **Pyramid:** Wide square base reducing by 2 blocks per layer upward.
3. For obelisk: place 3×3 base 3 high, then 2×2 for 10 blocks, then 1×1 for remaining height. Top with a beacon, sea lantern, or campfire.
4. Light the monument: place torches, lanterns, or glowstone inside or at the base so it is visible at night.
5. Place a sign at the base naming the monument and its builder.
6. Optional: surround base with a 1-block decorative border using a contrasting material.

**Result:** A visible landmark that marks your territory, serves as a navigation beacon, and demonstrates creative build capability.

### Design a Themed Area (Medieval, Modern, Nature)
**Prerequisites:** Clear land area (20×30 minimum), theme-appropriate materials, 3–5 hours build time.

**Medieval Theme:**
- Materials: stone bricks, cobblestone, oak planks, gravel, iron bars, oak fence, stone stairs, mossy stone brick.
- Elements: castle gatehouse (two 8-high towers flanking an arched gate), cobblestone roads with gravel fill, market stalls (fence frame + stair roof + barrel/chest inside), a well (barrel over a square hole), banner-decorated walls.

**Result:** A cohesive themed district that demonstrates intentional aesthetic design. Each theme communicates a different in-world culture or era.

### Terraform a Flat Building Area
**Prerequisites:** Shovel, pickaxe, 100+ dirt or gravel fill material, large uneven terrain plot.

1. Survey the target area by walking its perimeter and noting the highest and lowest points.
2. Decide on target height: choose the average current Y-level of the area, or a specific Y you want (e.g., Y=65).
3. Remove high spots: mine/shovel down all terrain above target Y. Store excavated dirt and stone — you will use it for fill.
4. Fill low spots: dump excavated dirt/gravel into depressions until they reach the target Y.
5. Smooth the surface: use shovel to level any remaining bumps. Walk across the area repeatedly, identifying and correcting uneven spots.
6. Grass regrowth: after a few minutes, grass spreads from adjacent grass blocks onto dirt fill blocks automatically. Apply bone meal to dirt for instant grass conversion.
7. Mark the flattened area boundaries with a foundation outline (cobblestone or stone bricks, 1 block high) so the exact build footprint is defined.
8. Test the surface: place a temporary flat building (dirt walls) to verify the surface is truly flat before committing to final materials.

**Result:** A fully flat, grass-covered building platform ready for construction. Eliminates terrain frustration during build planning and execution.
