# Biome Knowledge

## Overview

### Biome Categories
Biomes divide the world into distinct environments with unique terrain, mobs, plants, and structures. Every surface biome falls into one of four climate bands: temperate, cold, warm, or aquatic. Nether and End biomes are separate dimensions. Your spawn biome determines your early-game strategy. Identify it immediately and plan resource gathering accordingly.

### Temperate Biomes
Temperate biomes include plains, forest, birch forest, dark forest, swamp, mangrove swamp, jungle, and mushroom fields. These are the most resource-rich and commonly spawned biomes. Most surface villages appear here. Temperatures are moderate — no frostbite risk, no extreme heat penalties.

### Cold Biomes
Cold biomes include taiga, old growth taiga (spruce), snowy plains, snowy taiga, grove, jagged peaks, frozen peaks, and stony peaks. These biomes have distinct hostile mobs (strays instead of skeletons in frozen areas). Powder snow is a hazard in several of these biomes — carry leather boots to avoid sinking.

### Warm Biomes
Warm biomes include desert, savanna, badlands, and windswept savanna. These biomes are dry — no rain falls. Fire spreads more aggressively. Desert and badlands have unique surface generation with gold ore and exposed mineshafts.

### Aquatic Biomes
Aquatic biomes include ocean variants (warm, lukewarm, cold, frozen, deep variants), beaches, stony shores, and rivers. Warm oceans have coral reefs. Deep oceans contain ocean monuments. Frozen oceans have icebergs.

## Overworld Biomes

## Plains

### Plains Overview
Plains are flat, open grassland with minimal tree cover. Villages generate frequently here. Horses, donkeys, and rabbits spawn naturally. This is the best starter biome — flat terrain makes base building easy, and villages provide early food, tools, and beds. Locate a village immediately on spawn.

### Plains Resources
Collect wheat, carrots, potatoes, and beetroot from village farms on sight. Plains flowers include dandelions, poppies, azure bluets, oxeye daisies, cornflowers, and tulips — gather for dye production. Bees nest in oak and birch trees at the plains edge. Hay bales in villages convert to 9 wheat each.

### Plains Mobs
Horses spawn in herds of 2–6. Tame them with empty hands and breed with golden apples or golden carrots. Donkeys spawn occasionally — breed with horses to get mules for item transport. Pillager outposts spawn on plains — avoid until equipped with iron armor.

### Sunflower Plains
Sunflower plains is a plains variant covered in sunflowers. Sunflowers always face east — use them as a compass. Sunflowers drop sunflower items for yellow dye. Otherwise identical to plains in mob spawns and village frequency.

## Forest

### Forest Overview
Regular forest is dense with oak and birch trees. Wood is abundant — chop freely. Wolves spawn in groups of 4 here. Tame wolves with bones to create combat companions. Mushrooms (red and brown) grow on forest floors. Bees nest in oak trees with flowers nearby.

### Forest Resources
Oak logs are the most versatile wood type — craft into planks, sticks, doors, slabs, fences. Oak trees drop apples (1 in 200 leaf decay chance) for food and golden apple crafting. Roses (poppies) provide red dye. Forest biomes border many other biomes, making them a reliable wood source early-game.

### Birch Forest
Birch forest has exclusively birch trees, taller and uniform. No wolves spawn here. Birch wood produces white-toned planks for building. Old-growth birch forest has super-tall birch trees (up to 14 blocks). Mushrooms are less common than regular forest. Otherwise similar resources to forest.

### Flower Forest
Flower forest is a rare forest variant densely packed with every temperate flower type. Farm flowers here for dye diversity. Azalea trees appear at the surface above lush cave biomes — finding azalea trees on the surface means a lush cave is directly below. All regular forest mob spawns apply.

## Taiga

### Taiga Overview
Taiga biomes are forested with spruce trees and ferns. Foxes spawn here — foxes pick up items on the ground, breed two foxes with sweet berries to get a trusting fox that fights for you. Sweet berry bushes grow throughout taiga and cause damage when walked through. Villages generate in taiga biomes.

### Taiga Resources
Spruce logs have a dark brown color. Sweet berries are a renewable food source — farm them by breaking bushes with shears or harvesting fully grown berries. Ferns provide green dye via crafting. Podzol soil blocks spawn under spruce trees in old-growth taiga — collect with Silk Touch for mushroom farming.

### Old Growth Taiga (Mega Taiga)
Old growth taiga (formerly mega taiga) has massive 2×2 spruce trees, moss, and ferns. Coarse dirt and podzol cover the ground. These trees yield 30+ logs each — efficient wood farming. Spruce wood has a distinct dark aesthetic for builds. Two variants: regular old growth taiga and old growth spruce taiga (pine).

### Taiga Mobs
Wolves spawn in taiga and old growth taiga. Foxes spawn in taiga and snowy taiga. Rabbits spawn in taiga. Ravagers appear in raids on taiga villages. Strays (skeleton variant) do NOT spawn in regular taiga — only in frozen biomes. Standard hostile mob rules apply at night.

## Jungle

### Jungle Overview
Jungle biomes are dense, tall, and hard to navigate. Jungle trees reach 30+ blocks tall with vine-covered surfaces. Jungle biomes contain bamboo, cocoa beans (on jungle logs), melons, ocelots, and parrots. Jungle temples generate here. Visibility is extremely limited — mark your path or get lost permanently.

### Jungle Resources
Bamboo grows in bamboo jungles (sub-biome) in large patches — harvest bamboo for scaffolding, sticks, and bamboo blocks (1.20+ decoration). Cocoa beans grow on jungle log faces — farm by placing cocoa pods on jungle logs with bonemeal. Melons grow on the jungle floor. Vines are infinite climbers for vertical navigation.

### Jungle Mobs
Ocelots spawn in jungles — they do not become pets but scare creepers away when nearby. Parrots spawn in jungles only — tame with seeds for a shoulder companion that warns of nearby mobs. Spiders and cave spiders are more common in jungle due to dense terrain. Jungle temples have arrow and TNT traps — disarm before looting.

### Jungle Temple
Jungle temples spawn in jungles and bamboo jungles. Each temple has a puzzle with 3 levers controlling pistons — solve it or dig around the pistons to reach the double chest. A second chest is behind the dispenser trap. Chests can contain diamonds, emeralds, iron, gold, horse armor, and enchanted books. Tripwire traps fire arrows — cut the string first.

### Bamboo Jungle
Bamboo jungle is a dense sub-biome filled entirely with bamboo. No other trees grow here. Bamboo is the fastest-growing plant in the game — bonemeal it for instant growth. One bamboo block smelts 0.25 items; use bamboo in bulk for fuel. Pandas spawn only in bamboo jungles — breed with bamboo for the rare brown panda.

## Desert

### Desert Overview
Desert biomes are flat, sandy expanses with no rain and no passive mobs except rabbits. Sand and sandstone make up the terrain. Dead bushes and cacti dot the landscape. Desert temples and desert villages generate here. Husks replace skeletons as the primary night hostile mob.

### Desert Resources
Sand is infinite here — collect for glass production and sandstone. Cactus grows naturally — farm for green dye. Dead bushes drop sticks. Sandstone forms the base layer below sand. Red sand does NOT generate in deserts (badlands only). Fossils made of bone blocks and coal ore generate underground in deserts.

### Desert Temple
Desert temples (pyramids) generate with a floor of orange, blue, and sandstone blocks forming a pattern above a buried chamber. The chamber holds 4 chests with loot — but a pressure plate in the center triggers 9 TNT blocks under the floor. Neutralize the TNT before stepping on the plate: dig beside the pressure plate to expose and remove the TNT from below.

### Desert Village
Desert villages use sandstone construction and have unique layout. Sand-colored buildings. Farms grow wheat, carrots, potatoes, and beetroot. Desert villagers offer trading for sandstone, sand, glass, and TNT. Cartographer villagers sell maps to ocean monuments and woodland mansions. Husks (desert zombies) spawn at night near villages.

### Desert Mobs
Husks are desert-variant zombies that inflict Hunger on hit. They do not burn in sunlight — unlike regular zombies. Rabbits are the only passive mob spawning in deserts (tan and gold colored). Cacti and falling sand are environmental hazards. No rain means lightning never strikes here.

## Savanna

### Savanna Overview
Savanna biomes are warm, flat grasslands with acacia trees. Horses, llamas, and rabbits spawn here. Savanna villages are the most common village type in warm regions. No rain falls in savanna. Acacia wood has a distinctive orange-red color. Windswept savanna (variant) has extreme terrain.

### Savanna Resources
Acacia logs produce orange-toned planks and the unique acacia wood aesthetic. Acacia trees have a wide, flat canopy — efficient to harvest. Orange and yellow tulips for dye. Grass blocks with tall grass. Savanna plateaus generate on top of flat mesa-like hills within savanna biomes.

### Savanna Village
Savanna villages use acacia wood construction with a distinct orange palette. These villages often have larger footprints than plains villages. Farms, smiths, librarians, and weaponsmiths all generate here. Llamas wander near savanna villages in groups. Savanna villages are good for mid-game trading.

### Savanna Mobs
Horses spawn in savanna and plains. Llamas spawn in savanna and windswept hills — lead them to make a caravan. Llamas spit at enemies and carry 3–15 item slots each based on strength. Pillager outposts can generate in savanna. Standard hostile mobs spawn at night.

## Badlands

### Badlands Overview
Badlands (formerly mesa) biomes have red, orange, and yellow terracotta forming layered cliffs and plateaus. Gold ore generates at surface and near-surface levels (not just Y-levels 0-32) — mine badlands terrain aggressively for gold. Abandoned mineshafts generate at the surface level, exposed in canyon walls.

### Badlands Resources
All 16 colors of hardened (stained) terracotta generate in natural layers — white, orange, magenta, light blue, yellow, lime, pink, gray, light gray, cyan, purple, blue, brown, green, red, and brown. Red sand covers the surface instead of regular sand. Cacti grow on red sand. Gold ore is significantly more common here than any other biome.

### Badlands Mineshafts
Abandoned mineshafts in badlands generate at the surface and near the surface (y=40-80). This means exposed mineshaft corridors appear in canyon walls. Walk canyon walls to find open mineshaft entrances. Mineshafts contain cave spider spawners, chests with loot (rails, torches, bread, iron, gold, redstone, diamonds), and rails for minecarts.

### Badlands Variants
Wooded badlands adds oak trees and coarse dirt on top of the plateaus. Eroded badlands has extreme spire formations. All variants share the same terracotta colors and gold ore bonus. No villages, no passive mobs, no structures other than mineshafts. Badlands are hostile territory with limited food sources.

## Swamp

### Swamp Overview
Swamp biomes are flat, waterlogged terrain with oak trees covered in vines, lily pads, blue orchids, and mushrooms. Witch huts spawn here exclusively. Slimes spawn at night on the surface in swamps during full moon or near sea level (y=50-70). Mangrove swamp is a separate variant.

### Swamp Resources
Lily pads are the primary unique drop — collect for decoration and as walkable water surfaces. Blue orchids grow only in swamps — the only source of light blue dye from flowers (before wandering traders). Clay generates under shallow swamp water. Sugar cane grows on swamp shores. Mushrooms are common.

### Witch Hut
Witch huts are small hut structures on stilts over swamp water. A witch spawns inside and respawns. Kill witches for glass bottles, gunpowder, redstone, glowstone, sticks, spider eyes, and sugar. Witch huts can be converted to witch farms with a spawner trap setup. Only one witch spawns per hut naturally.

### Swamp Slimes
Slimes spawn in swamps at surface level between Y=50 and Y=70 during any light level at night, regardless of moon phase. Kill slimes for slimeballs — essential for sticky pistons, leads, and slime blocks. Slimes split into smaller versions on death down to tiny size, which then drop slimeballs. Build a surface slime farm in swamps for reliable slimeball production.

### Mangrove Swamp
Mangrove swamp is a warm swamp variant with mangrove trees growing in water. Mangrove roots, propagules, and mud blocks are unique to this biome. Harvest mangrove propagules by bonemeal-ing or breaking the hanging propagules from leaves. Mud converts to packed mud and then to mud bricks for building. Frogs and tadpoles spawn here — the only natural frog spawn location.

## Mountains

### Mountains Overview
Mountain biomes include several variants: jagged peaks, frozen peaks, stony peaks, meadow, grove, snowy slopes, and windswept hills. Goats spawn in all mountain biomes. Emerald ore generates exclusively in mountain biomes (all mountain variants, not just one). The higher you go, the more exposed and dangerous terrain becomes.

### Emerald Ore in Mountains
Emerald ore generates only in mountain biomes between Y=-16 and Y=320. It generates most frequently between Y=100 and Y=200. Emerald ore drops 1 emerald (or more with Fortune III). Fortune III gives 1–4 emeralds per ore. Mine at high elevation in mountain biomes for the most efficient emerald gathering outside of villager trading.

### Goats
Goats spawn in mountain biomes. Goats ram players and mobs — they will knock you off cliffs. They can jump 10 blocks high. Goats drop goat horns when they ram into solid blocks (2 horns per goat maximum). Goat horns play instrument sounds and are used for decoration. Breed goats with wheat. Screaming goats are rare (1 in 50) and produce different horn sounds.

### Powder Snow
Powder snow generates in grove and snowy slopes biomes as invisible surface traps. Players and mobs without leather boots sink into it and receive Freezing damage over time. Wear leather boots to walk on top of powder snow. Collect powder snow with buckets. Powder snow extinguishes fire and traps mobs that fall in.

### Stony Peaks
Stony peaks are a warm mountain variant (replaces snowy peaks in warm regions) with calcite and gravel bands. No snow here. Iron ore exposed on cliff faces. Goats still spawn. Views from stony peaks allow scouting of biome distribution across the landscape. Build a lookout tower here for navigation.

## Snowy Biomes

### Snowy Plains
Snowy plains (formerly snowy tundra) are flat, snow-covered plains. Igloos generate here — they contain a bed, furnace, and sometimes a basement with a brewing stand, golden apple, and a zombie villager in a cage. Rabbits and polar bears spawn. Strays are more common here at night.

### Igloos
Igloos generate in snowy plains and snowy taiga. Approximately 50% of igloos have a basement beneath a carpet. The basement contains a brewing stand (with awkward potion), golden apple, iron nuggets, coal, and a zombie villager locked in a cage with a cleric villager. Cure the zombie villager with splash potion of Weakness then golden apple for dramatically reduced trade prices.

### Strays
Strays are skeleton variants that spawn in frozen biomes (ice spikes, frozen ocean, snowy plains, snowy taiga). They shoot tipped arrows of Slowness in addition to regular arrows. They will not burn in sunlight in frozen biomes. Kill strays for bones and arrows — and occasionally tipped arrows.

### Ice Spikes
Ice spikes is a rare variant of snowy plains covered in large packed ice spike formations. Packed ice generates here in large amounts — collect with Silk Touch for building material or as a fast travel surface (entities slide on packed ice). Blue ice generates at the base of spikes — even faster sliding than packed ice.

### Snowy Taiga
Snowy taiga combines snow coverage with spruce trees. Foxes and wolves both spawn here. Villages generate in snowy taiga with spruce log construction and unique layouts. Igloos also generate in snowy taiga. Similar resources to regular taiga but with Strays at night and powder snow in some sub-variants.

## Dark Forest

### Dark Forest Overview
Dark forest (formerly roofed forest) has an extremely dense dark oak canopy that blocks nearly all light at ground level, causing hostile mobs to spawn even during daytime. Woodland mansions generate exclusively in dark forest biomes. Mushrooms (giant and small) grow on the forest floor. Bring torches and be ready for daytime mob encounters.

### Dark Forest Navigation
The canopy is 5-7 blocks thick, creating permanent darkness below. Light the ground as you traverse to prevent constant ambushes. Dark oak trees require 2×2 sapling patterns to grow — collect saplings by breaking leaves. Dark oak logs are dark brown and produce dark oak planks. Mushrooms are easy to collect here.

### Woodland Mansion
Woodland mansions are massive 3-floor structures that generate in dark forest biomes, always far from spawn (multiple thousands of blocks away). Obtain a woodland explorer map from a cartographer villager to find one. Mansions contain multiple room types, Evokers and Vindicators as enemies, and chest loot including diamond armor, enchanted books, and totems of undying.

### Woodland Mansion Loot
Evokers drop totems of undying — the only source. Vindicators drop emeralds and iron axes. Rooms contain chests with enchanted books, golden apples, iron tools, diamond tools, and music discs. Evokers can summon fangs and Vexes — kill them first in any fight. Totems of undying save you from one death and are essential for boss fights.

## Mushroom Fields

### Mushroom Fields Overview
Mushroom fields (formerly mushroom island) are the rarest Overworld biome. They generate as islands in deep ocean, usually far from spawn. The ground is mycelium (gray-purple). No hostile mobs spawn naturally here — ever. Not even in caves beneath the surface. This is the safest biome in the game.

### Mushroom Fields Resources
Mooshrooms (red or brown cow variants) spawn exclusively here. Milk mooshrooms with a bowl to get mushroom stew (food). Shear mooshrooms for 5 mushrooms, reverting them to normal cows. Mycelium spreads to adjacent dirt like grass — collect with Silk Touch for mushroom-friendly decoration. Giant mushrooms grow naturally here.

### Using Mushroom Fields as a Base
Build your base on a mushroom island for mob-free construction. No hostile mobs means no creeper explosions, no nighttime ambushes. Mob farms do not work here (no spawning). Bring all resources from elsewhere. The island is defensible and peaceful — ideal for storage builds, breeding programs, or AFK activities.

## Cherry Grove

### Cherry Grove Overview
Cherry grove biomes generate in mountainous terrain and are visually distinct with pink cherry blossom trees, pink petal ground cover, and pink particle effects. Bees spawn here and nest in cherry trees. Cherry wood is a new pink wood type introduced in 1.20. The biome is relatively rare and usually found in or near mountain regions.

### Cherry Grove Resources
Cherry logs produce pink-toned planks, slabs, fences, and doors — a distinct aesthetic unavailable elsewhere. Pink petals (ground cover) drop as items for decoration — 4 petals per block, each pink petal placed adds +1 to the petal count. Bees nest in cherry trees. Collect cherry saplings from leaf decay for farming cherry trees elsewhere.

## Ocean Biomes

### Ocean Overview
Oceans cover approximately 26% of the Overworld surface. Ocean biomes range from warm to frozen. All oceans have a seafloor of gravel, sand, or clay. Ocean monuments generate in deep ocean biomes. Shipwrecks generate across all ocean types. Buried treasure generates near beaches. Dolphins, fish, and squid spawn in all ocean types.

### Warm Ocean
Warm ocean biomes have coral reefs with vibrant coral blocks, coral fans, and sea pickles. Coral requires warm water to survive — it dies instantly outside water unless using Silk Touch. Collect coral blocks with Silk Touch for decoration. Tropical fish spawn here in large variety. No kelp in warm ocean. Pufferfish spawn here — lethal to eat raw (gives Poison, Nausea, Hunger).

### Cold and Frozen Ocean
Cold oceans have salmon and fewer coral features. Frozen oceans have icebergs of ice, packed ice, and blue ice. Polar bears and strays spawn near frozen ocean shores. Blue ice is the fastest sliding block in the game — faster than packed ice, which is faster than regular ice. Mine blue ice from frozen ocean icebergs with Silk Touch. Collect blue ice in bulk for ice boat highways.

### Deep Ocean
Deep ocean biomes go down to Y=-64 (bedrock) and have a flat, deep floor. Ocean monuments generate only in deep ocean variants. Guardian and elder guardian mobs spawn only in and around ocean monuments. Deep ocean has limited light, making navigation dangerous. Drowned mobs (zombie variant) spawn in all deep water.

### Ocean Monument
Ocean monuments generate in deep ocean biomes. They contain 3 elder guardians (bosses) and many guardians. Elder guardians inflict Mining Fatigue III on all nearby players — preventing block breaking for 5 minutes. Use milk to cure it. Ocean monuments have 8 gold blocks in a central room worth 40+ gold ingots. Sponges generate in a room inside. Prismarine, prismarine bricks, and dark prismarine are the unique building materials.

## Beach and Shore

### Beach
Beaches generate at the junction of ocean and land biomes. Sand beaches are where turtles spawn and lay eggs. Protect turtle eggs by standing on them — eggs hatch into baby turtles which grow into turtles that drop scutes. Collect 5 scutes to craft a turtle helmet (Water Breathing). Buried treasure chests generate under sand on beaches marked by an X on buried treasure maps.

### Buried Treasure
Buried treasure maps come from shipwreck chests or ocean ruins. Follow the map to the X marker on a beach. The chest is buried 1-3 blocks under sand or gravel. The chest always contains a Heart of the Sea — needed to craft a conduit. Also contains food, iron, gold, TNT, emeralds, diamonds, and prismarine crystals. One buried treasure per map.

### Stony Shore
Stony shores generate where mountains meet the ocean. Gravel and stone form the shore. No turtles spawn here. No structures generate on stony shores. They are primarily a transitional biome. Salmon spawn in the cold water near stony shores. Mine exposed stone and gravel for early resources.

## River

### River Overview
Rivers are narrow water channels that cut through the landscape and separate biomes. Salmon spawn in rivers. Clay generates on river floors in abundance — collect clay for bricks, flower pots, and terracotta. Sugar cane grows along river banks on sand or dirt adjacent to water. Rivers freeze in cold biomes.

### River Resources
Clay balls from clay blocks can be crafted into clay blocks or smelted into bricks. Bricks form brick blocks for durable construction aesthetics. Sugar cane grows up to 3 blocks tall — farm by placing next to water. Sugar cane is essential for paper (books, maps, enchanting), and sugar (cake, fermented spider eyes). Salmon drop raw salmon and sometimes bones.

## Cave Biomes

### Lush Caves
Lush caves generate underground, typically below azalea trees on the surface. They are filled with moss, glow berries, flowering azalea leaves, dripleaf plants, and spore blossoms. Axolotls spawn in lush cave water pools — the only place axolotls spawn. Collect glow berries as a light source and food. Lush caves are visually stunning and resource-rich.

### Lush Cave Resources
Glow berries grow on cave vines hanging from ceilings — harvest with a right-click or by punching the vine with glow berries. They give 2 hunger points and glow when dropped or in a frame. Dripleaf plants are found here — big dripleaf tilts and drops the player after standing on it. Moss blocks spread to adjacent blocks with bonemeal — farm moss for a renewable stone alternative.

### Axolotls
Axolotls spawn only in lush caves in underground water. They come in 5 colors (cyan, magenta, yellow, white, brown — brown is extremely rare 1-in-1200). Axolotls attack fish, squid, drowned, and guardians. Carry them in buckets. Deploy axolotls from buckets in ocean monuments to assist in clearing guardians. Axolotls grant players Regeneration when the axolotl kills a mob.

### Dripstone Caves
Dripstone caves generate underground and are filled with pointed dripstone stalactites (ceiling) and stalagmites (floor). Falling onto a stalagmite deals extra fall damage — watch footsteps. Stalactites slowly drip water or lava into cauldrons below them — set up cauldrons for passive lava collection (extremely slow but renewable). Dripstone blocks are collectable with any pickaxe.

### Deep Dark
The deep dark biome generates in the deepest underground layers, primarily below Y=-40. It is filled with sculk blocks, sculk sensors, sculk shriekers, and sculk catalysts. The warden spawns here — an extremely dangerous blind mob that detects sound and vibration. Ancient cities generate exclusively in the deep dark.

### Ancient City
Ancient cities are massive structures in the deep dark containing reinforced deepslate (the strongest non-bedrock block), unique decoration, and loot chests. Loot includes echo shards (for recovery compass), swift sneak enchantment books (unique to ancient cities), and other rare enchanted books. Sneak everywhere in ancient cities — running, placing blocks, or breaking blocks summons the Warden. Collect swift sneak III books — they allow faster crouching movement.

### Warden
The warden spawns in the deep dark after 3 sculk shrieker activations. It has 500 health points and kills in 2-3 hits on Normal difficulty. It is completely blind and navigates by sound and vibration (sculk sensors). Sneak to avoid detection. Throw projectiles (snowballs, arrows) away from yourself to distract it. The warden drops only 5 XP — it is not worth fighting. Avoid it entirely.

## Nether Biomes

### Nether Biomes Overview
The Nether has 5 distinct biomes: Nether Wastes, Soul Sand Valley, Crimson Forest, Warped Forest, and Basalt Deltas. Nether Fortresses generate in Nether Wastes and adjacent biomes. Bastion Remnants generate in all biomes except Basalt Deltas. Understanding Nether biomes is critical for navigation and resource gathering.

### Nether Wastes
Nether Wastes is the default, most common Nether biome. Red netherrack forms the terrain. Ghasts fly and shoot fireballs. Zombified Piglins wander in groups — do not attack them; they become hostile as a group. Nether gold ore generates here (smelt for gold nuggets, or use Fortune for multiple). Nether quartz ore is abundant for XP and quartz blocks.

### Soul Sand Valley
Soul Sand Valley is a pale, fog-filled biome with soul sand, soul soil, fossils (bone blocks), and basalt pillars. Skeletons and ghasts spawn here in higher numbers. Blue soul fire burns on soul soil. Soul sand slows movement — avoid walking on it without Soul Speed enchantment boots. Soul Speed III boots are the BiS for Nether travel. Barter with piglins for soul speed books.

### Crimson Forest
Crimson forest is a dense red fungal forest with crimson nylium, huge crimson fungi, crimson roots, and weeping vines. Piglins spawn here — they are neutral unless you remove your gold armor. Hoglins spawn here — they are aggressive and deal knockback. Hoglins flee from warped fungi placed on ground. Crimson stem is a wood-like building block. Collect crimson fungus for growing huge fungi.

### Warped Forest
Warped forest is a blue-green fungal forest with warped nylium, huge warped fungi, warped roots, shroomlights, and twisting vines. Endermen spawn here in high numbers — do not look at them. Warped forest has NO naturally hostile mobs other than Endermen. This makes it the safest Nether biome for exploring and building. Warped stem is a teal wood-like block.

### Basalt Deltas
Basalt deltas are chaotic terrain of basalt and blackstone with lava lakes and magma blocks. Magma cubes spawn abundantly here — kill for magma cream (brewing fire resistance potions). Ghasts also spawn here. Basalt generates in large formations — collect for building. Blackstone replaces cobblestone in all recipes and is more common here than anywhere. Strider mobs spawn in lava here.

### Nether Fortress
Nether Fortresses generate in all Nether biomes (more commonly in Nether Wastes and Soul Sand Valley edge). Find them by traveling in the North/South direction (±X axis) — fortresses align on the X axis. Nether fortresses contain Blaze spawners (collect Blaze Rods for brewing), Wither Skeleton spawners (collect Wither Skeleton Skulls for Wither summoning), Nether wart growing in soul sand patches, and chest loot.

### Bastion Remnant
Bastion Remnants generate in all Nether biomes except Basalt Deltas. They are massive piglin fortresses with 4 types: Bridge, Hoglin Stables, Housing Units, and Treasure Room. The Treasure Room bastion has the most valuable loot — netherite scraps, ancient debris-level loot, gold blocks, and lodestones. Piglins and brutes guard bastions. Piglin brutes are always hostile — kill before looting.

### Striders
Striders spawn in lava in the Nether. They are passive unless attacked. Saddle them with a saddle and guide with a warped fungus on a stick. Riding a strider is the safest way to cross lava oceans. Striders take damage outside of lava — keep them in lava or use a blanket. Zombified striders occasionally spawn on top of regular striders.

## End Biomes

### The End
The End is reached via the End Portal in a stronghold. The central End island has obsidian pillars with End Crystals powering the Ender Dragon. Endermen spawn abundantly here. The Ender Dragon must be defeated to open the exit portal and access outer End islands. Destroy all End Crystals on pillars before engaging the Dragon.

### End Highlands
End Highlands are elevated plateaus on outer End islands (accessed via the End Gateway after defeating the Dragon). End Cities generate only in End Highlands. End Cities contain Shulkers (drop Shulker Shells for shulker boxes), End Rods (light source), and unique loot. End Ships attached to End Cities contain an elytra on a display stand — the only way to obtain elytra.

### End Midlands and Barrens
End Midlands are medium-height outer End terrain. End Barrens are low-altitude flat outer End areas. No End Cities generate in midlands or barrens. Endermen spawn throughout all outer End biomes. Chorus plants grow on outer End islands — punch them at the base to harvest all chorus fruit. Chorus fruit teleports the player when eaten — useful for emergency escapes but random.

### Elytra
Elytra generate exclusively in End Ships attached to End Cities in End Highlands. Every End Ship has exactly one elytra on an item frame inside. End Ships also contain a brewing stand (with potions), chests with loot, and a dragon head on the bow. Elytra enable gliding flight. Combine with fireworks rockets for powered flight. Repair elytra with phantom membranes in an anvil.

### Shulkers
Shulkers are box-shaped mobs that attach to surfaces in End Cities. They shoot homing projectiles that inflict Levitation for 10 seconds — dangerous near edge drops. Shulkers can teleport when hit. They drop shulker shells — craft 2 shells with a chest to make a shulker box (27-slot inventory container that retains contents when broken). Shulker boxes are essential for item transport.

## Biome-Specific Resources

### Rare Resources by Biome
- Emeralds: Mountain biomes (all variants) only, Y=-16 to Y=320.
- Sponge: Ocean monuments in deep ocean.
- Heart of the Sea: Buried treasure on beaches.
- Elytra: End Ships in End Highlands only.
- Totem of Undying: Evokers in Woodland Mansions (dark forest) and Raids.
- Trident: Drowned mobs in ocean/river biomes (rare drop).
- Nether Star: Wither boss (summoned, not biome-specific).
- Shulker Shell: Shulkers in End Cities (End Highlands).
- Ancient Debris: Nether biomes, Y=8-22 (peak at Y=15).
- Echo Shard: Ancient City chests (deep dark).

### Biome-Exclusive Blocks
- Mycelium: Mushroom fields (Silk Touch required).
- Podzol: Old growth taiga (Silk Touch required).

## Villages by Biome

### Village Types Overview
Villages generate in six biome types, each with a distinct visual style and set of buildings. Village type affects the appearance of buildings and some offered trades but the trade mechanics and villager professions are identical across types. Locate villages early for beds (set spawn points), food, iron tools, and trading.

### Plains Village
Plains villages use oak logs and cobblestone construction. They are the most common village type. Typical size: 14-20 buildings. Professions available: farmer, shepherd, fisherman, librarian, cartographer, fletcher, toolsmith, weaponsmith, armorer, leatherworker, butcher, cleric, nitwit. Pillager outposts often generate nearby — clear the outpost to prevent raids.

### Desert Village
Desert villages use sandstone and terracotta construction. No wells (no water feature in desert village center). Hay bales are common. Villagers wear desert-themed clothing. Husks attack villages at night more aggressively than zombies attack other village types. Strong desert village presence makes glass and sandstone tradeable early.

### Taiga Village
Taiga villages use spruce logs and cobblestone construction. Slightly smaller footprint than plains villages. Cobblestone walls replace fences. Villages often built with trapdoors as aesthetic elements on spruce logs. Same professions available as plains. Foxes wander nearby. Snow on roofs if in snowy taiga.

### Snowy Village
Snowy villages use spruce logs and snow blocks. They have smaller, squat buildings with snow-covered roofs. Less common than plains or taiga villages. Igloos sometimes generate nearby separately. These villages are harder to spot in snow — look for torch light. Strays attack villagers at night. Beds here allow snow biome spawn setting.

### Savanna Village
Savanna villages use acacia logs and cobblestone construction. Larger footprints common. Acacia wood orange palette makes them visually distinctive. Llamas wander near savanna villages. No rain means fire spread risk — be careful with torches or flint and steel near acacia wood buildings.

### Swamp Village (None)
Swamp biomes do not generate villages. Witch huts are the only structure. No village trades available in swamp biomes without traveling out. If spawning in a swamp, prioritize scouting adjacent biomes for the nearest village.

## Structures by Biome

### Woodland Mansion
Location: Dark forest biomes, thousands of blocks from spawn. Find via: Cartographer villager map (master-level trade). Contains: Evokers (totem of undying), Vindicators, Allays, chests. Approach: Bring full iron or diamond armor, shield, and plenty of food. Clear room-by-room systematically.

### Ocean Monument
Location: Deep ocean biomes. Find via: Ocean explorer map from cartographer villager. Contains: Elder guardians (Mining Fatigue), guardians, 8 gold blocks, sponge room, prismarine blocks. Approach: Bring milk buckets for Mining Fatigue, night vision potions, full diamond armor, and consider underwater breathing enchantment.

### Stronghold
Location: Underground, accessible via Eye of Ender (throw toward stronghold). Contains: End Portal, silverfish spawners, libraries with enchanted books, chests. End Portal requires 12 Eyes of Ender to activate (some may already be placed). Strongholds are always underground — follow Eyes of Ender to find them.

### Pillager Outpost
Location: Plains, desert, taiga, savanna, snowy tundra biomes. Contains: Pillager captain (Bad Omen on kill), pillagers, iron golem in cage, allays in cage, chest loot. Killing the patrol captain triggers a raid on the nearest village. Clear outposts for map control and to prevent constant patrols.

### Desert Temple
Location: Desert biomes. Contains: 4 loot chests with TNT trap. Approach: Disarm TNT before touching pressure plate. High loot value per structure.

### Jungle Temple
Location: Jungle and bamboo jungle biomes. Contains: 2 chests (one puzzle, one trap), dispenser arrow trap. Approach: Cut tripwire with shears, solve lever puzzle or dig around pistons.

### Buried Treasure
Location: Beaches adjacent to oceans. Find via: Treasure maps from shipwrecks or ocean ruins. Contains: Heart of the Sea plus food, materials, diamonds.

### Witch Hut
Location: Swamp biomes. Contains: Witch mob (respawns), cauldron, crafting table, flower pot with mushroom. Farm witches here for potion ingredients.

### Ruined Portal
Location: All Overworld and Nether biomes (generated everywhere). Contains: Partial obsidian portal frame, chest with gold, flint and steel, obsidian, crying obsidian, golden items. Complete the portal for quick Nether access.

### Shipwreck
Location: Ocean biomes, beaches, on land near ocean. Contains: Up to 3 chests (supply, treasure, map chest). Map chest may have buried treasure map. Supply chest has food. Treasure chest has gold, iron, emeralds, diamonds.

### Nether Fortress
Location: All Nether biomes (most commonly Nether Wastes). Find by: Travel North/South (along X-axis). Contains: Blaze spawners, wither skeleton spawners, nether wart, chests. Essential for brewing (blaze rods) and Wither summoning (skulls).

### Bastion Remnant
Location: All Nether biomes except Basalt Deltas. Contains: Piglins, hoglin stables, piglin brutes, gold blocks, netherite loot (treasure room type). Do not open chests while piglins watch — they aggro. Wear gold armor to remain neutral with piglins (not brutes).

### End City
Location: End Highlands on outer End islands. Find via: End Gateways (shoot ender pearl into gateway after Dragon death). Contains: Shulkers, shulker shells, elytra (on End Ship), valuable enchanted gear. Essential late-game structure for elytra and shulker box farming.

## Biome Navigation and Identification

### Identifying Your Biome
Identify biomes by surface blocks and vegetation: grass color (green = temperate, brownish = savanna, pale = cold), tree types (oak = forest, birch = birch forest, spruce = taiga, acacia = savanna, jungle tree = jungle, dark oak = dark forest), and sand vs grass vs snow on the surface. Carry a clock (4 gold ingots + 1 redstone) to read time. Use the F3 debug screen (Java Edition) to see the exact biome name. Biome boundaries are visible at the transition from one vegetation type to another.

### Biome-Specific Survival Tips
Desert: No rain, no passive mobs except rabbits — bring food. Jungle: Extreme navigation difficulty — mark paths. Swamp: Slimes spawn at full moon on the surface — avoid at night. Mushroom Fields: No hostile mob spawning on mycelium — the only mob-safe biome. Ocean: No wood source — carry extra wood. Taiga: Sweet berry bushes damage you while walking through — use paths. Mountains: Fall risk is extreme — bring water bucket for MLG saves on every excursion.
