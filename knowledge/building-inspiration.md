# Building Inspiration — Creative Ideas for City Building

## Material Ideas

Sand found near water can be smelted into glass. Glass panes make beautiful windows. A building with glass windows looks 10x better than one without.

Stone bricks are easy to make — just craft 4 cobblestone into 4 stone_bricks. They look much cleaner than raw cobblestone for walls and paths.

Clay is found near rivers and lakes. Mine it, smelt the clay balls into bricks, craft 4 bricks into a brick block. Brick buildings have a classic warm look.

Smelt cobblestone twice: cobblestone → stone → smooth_stone. Smooth stone makes clean modern floors and counters.

Granite, diorite, and andesite can be crafted into polished versions (4 raw → 4 polished). Great accent blocks.

Stairs and slabs can be crafted from almost any block type. Use stairs for peaked roofs and slabs for paths and shelves.

Mix at least 2-3 materials in every building. Oak planks + stone bricks + glass panes is a classic combo. Spruce + cobblestone feels rustic. Brick + dark oak feels warm and solid.

## Structure Ideas

A marketplace with open stalls, each with a different colored wool roof. Chest shops underneath.

A lighthouse on a cliff or peninsula with glass windows spiraling up. Torch or lantern at the top.

A bridge connecting two areas — stone brick arches with oak plank walkway and fence railings.

A garden with paths, flowers, water features, and benches made from stairs.

A library with tall windows, bookshelves lining the walls, and a reading area with carpet.

A blacksmith with a stone brick chimney, lava or campfire inside, anvil, and tool racks on the walls.

A watchtower at the city entrance — tall, narrow, stone base with wood upper floors and a lookout platform.

A dock or pier extending into water — oak planks on fence posts, with boats tied up.

An underground storage vault — carved into a hillside with iron doors and organized chest rooms.

A town square with a central fountain (water source block surrounded by stone), benches, and paths radiating outward.

A farm compound with wheat fields, animal pens, a barn, and a silo (tall cylinder of planks).

## Layout Ideas

Build roads FIRST between structures. Cobblestone or stone brick paths 3 blocks wide. Roads create structure.

Vary building heights. Not everything should be the same height — a tower next to a cottage next to a two-story inn.

Leave green space. Not every block needs to be built on. Trees, gardens, and open plazas make a city feel alive.

Build near interesting terrain. A house on a cliff overlooking water. A workshop tucked into a hillside. Use the landscape.

Name every building and district. "The Market Quarter", "Harbor District", "Luna's Tower". Names make places feel real.

## Advanced Techniques

Lanterns and soul lanterns look better than torches for lighting. Craft iron nuggets + torch.

Flower pots with flowers on windowsills add life to buildings.

Trap doors can be used as shutters on windows.

Campfires make great chimneys — place on top of a chimney stack, they produce smoke.

Use logs as corner pillars — stripped logs look even cleaner.

Stairs placed upside-down make good ceiling trim and arch details.
