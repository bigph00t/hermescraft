# Combat Knowledge

## Hostile Mob Overview

Hostile mobs spawn in darkness (light level < 0 in 1.21.1 using the new light system — effectively light level 0 on the block surface). Kill them before they kill you. Always know your exit route.

---

## Zombie

### Zombie — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Melee damage: 3 HP (Easy), 4 HP (Normal), 6 HP (Hard).
- Spawns at night on the surface and in caves at any light level below threshold.
- Burns in sunlight unless wearing a helmet.
- Breaks down doors on Hard difficulty.
- Calls nearby zombies when attacked (zombie alert radius ~40 blocks).
- Drops: rotten flesh, occasionally iron ingot, carrot, potato, iron shovel/sword, armor.

### Zombie — Combat
- Slow movement. Walk backward while attacking with a sword.
- Keep 2-3 block distance when retreating — it cannot sprint-gap-close like you can.
- Use Smite enchantment: +2.5 damage per level vs undead (zombies, skeletons, wither, etc.).
- If surrounded, pillar up 2 blocks. Zombies cannot climb.
- Baby zombies are faster than adults — treat them as a sprint mob, not a walk mob.
- Zombie villagers: cure with golden apple + weakness potion for cheap trades. Don't kill them.

## Skeleton

### Skeleton — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Arrow damage: 1–5 HP depending on range (closer = more damage on Normal).
- Spawns at night and in caves. Burns in sunlight.
- Shoots arrows at ~15 block range. Strafes while shooting.
- Picks up and equips dropped armor/weapons.
- Drops: bones, arrows, occasionally bow (may be enchanted), armor.

### Skeleton — Combat
- Close the gap fast. Skeletons deal more damage at range.
- Sprint toward it in a zigzag to dodge arrows.
- At melee range it can still shoot — keep moving, don't stand still.
- Use a shield: block arrows by raising shield, then close and sword.
- Fight with terrain cover: duck behind blocks to break line of sight, advance.
- Smite V sword kills a skeleton in 2 hits (unarmored).
- In water, skeleton cannot shoot effectively — push it in or fight near water.

## Creeper

### Creeper — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Explosion damage: up to 49 HP at point-blank (Hard). Armor and distance reduce this heavily.
- Fuse time: 1.5 seconds from start of hiss.
- Does not burn in sunlight. Silent approach — no footstep sounds.
- Flees from cats and ocelots.
- Drops: gunpowder, music disc (only if killed by skeleton arrow).
- Charged creeper (struck by lightning): double explosion radius.

### Creeper — Combat
- Listen for the hiss. Back away immediately — you have 1.5 seconds.
- Hit once with sword, back away past 3 blocks, hit again. Repeat. Each hit interrupts the fuse if you retreat fast enough.
- Two hits with a diamond sword kills a creeper before fuse completes if you time retreats.
- Never corner yourself near a creeper.
- Use a bow: kill at safe range before it can close.
- Knockback enchantment on sword pushes it away — useful but risky if walls are behind it.
- Explosion destroys terrain. Don't fight creepers near your base or valuables.
- Shield blocks the explosion damage almost entirely at the moment of detonation.

## Spider

### Spider — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 16 HP (8 hearts).
- Melee damage: 2 HP (Easy), 3 HP (Normal), 4 HP (Hard).
- Can climb walls and ceilings.
- Neutral in daylight (passive unless attacked). Hostile at night and in darkness.
- Cave spider variant is smaller, fits in 1-block gaps, and inflicts poison.
- Drops: string, spider eye.

### Spider — Combat
- Spiders jump at you — maintain mid-range and sidestep.
- A 2-block wide trench stops spiders from reaching you; they can't jump over.
- Bane of Arthropods enchantment: +2.5 damage per level vs spiders, cave spiders, bees, silverfish.
- Cave spiders spawn from spawners in mineshafts. Block all spawner faces with blocks before destroying it, or light it up (torch on top of spawner).
- Cave spider poison deals 1 HP per 1.25 seconds for ~7 seconds. Milk (from a cow) cures poison instantly.
- Milk before entering mineshafts if you expect cave spiders.

## Enderman

### Enderman — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 40 HP (20 hearts).
- Melee damage: 7 HP (Easy), 10 HP (Normal), 15 HP (Hard).
- Teleports when attacked, when wet (rain, water), or when a projectile is fired at it.
- Aggros if you look at its torso/head directly (crosshair on upper body).
- Neutral unless provoked by eye contact or attack.
- Takes damage from water and rain.
- Drops: ender pearls (0–1 per kill).

### Enderman — Combat
- Avoid looking at endermen unless you intend to fight.
- Wear a carved pumpkin as helmet to prevent accidental aggro — it blocks your vision slightly but prevents eye contact from triggering them.
- Fight in shallow water or rain: enderman takes constant damage and teleports erratically.
- If attacked, look at its feet — not its body — to avoid triggering more.
- Stand under a 2-block ceiling: enderman is 3 blocks tall, cannot reach you. Swing at its feet.
- Sweep attacks hit endermen if they're grouped; don't bother — fight one at a time.
- Ender pearls are critical for The End fight. Farm endermen in The End or at night.

## Witch

### Witch — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 26 HP (13 hearts).
- Throws splash potions of poison, slowness, weakness, harming, fire resistance.
- Heals itself with potions of healing and fire resistance.
- Spawns in swamps, during thunderstorms (villager struck by lightning), and in raids.
- Immune to its own potions.
- Drops: glass bottles, glowstone dust, gunpowder, redstone, spider eye, sugar, sticks (rare).

### Witch — Combat
- Close the gap fast: witches need range to throw potions effectively.
- At melee range witches are weak — they deal no melee damage directly.
- Kill before it can throw a healing potion (red particle effect = self-heal incoming).
- If poisoned by witch, drink milk or eat a honey bottle.
- Avoid standing in water near a witch — splash potions hit harder in water (spreads).
- Diamond sword at close range kills in 3-4 hits before it can respond.

## Pillager

### Pillager — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 24 HP (12 hearts).
- Crossbow damage: 6–11 HP per bolt.
- Spawns at pillager outposts (y=64+) and during raids.
- Drops: crossbow (may be enchanted), arrow, dark oak log, occasionally emerald or banner.
- Patrol leaders carry an ominous banner — killing one gives Bad Omen.
- Bad Omen triggers a raid when you enter a village.

### Pillager — Combat
- Use a shield to block crossbow bolts — raise shield while approaching.
- Pillagers must reload after firing. Sprint in during reload and sword.
- Knockback them into environmental hazards (cliffs, lava) at outposts.
- At outpost: clear the top floor first (fewest pillagers), then work down.
- Avoid killing patrol captains near villages unless you want a raid or want Ominous Bottle for Ominous Trials.

## Vindicator

### Vindicator — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 24 HP (12 hearts).
- Axe damage: 9 HP (Easy), 13 HP (Normal), 19.5 HP (Hard). One of the highest melee damages.
- Spawns in woodland mansions and during raids (wave 2+).
- Drops: emerald, iron axe (may be enchanted).
- Sprints at you aggressively.

### Vindicator — Combat
- Do not let vindicators get close — their axe damage is lethal.
- Keep a shield raised and sword ready. Block, then strike during their swing animation.
- Two-hit combo: sprint-attack (crit) + normal hit kills on Normal difficulty with iron sword.
- In woodland mansions, fight vindicators in doorways — they can't surround you.
- Flee if below half health. Vindicators sprint faster than walking — you must sprint to escape.

## Evoker

### Evoker — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 24 HP (12 hearts).
- Three attacks: fang line (deals 6 HP), fang circle (deals 6 HP each), vex summon.
- Vexes summoned in groups of 3; they phase through walls and deal 5.5 HP (Normal).
- No melee attack. Flees from player while casting.
- Drops: totem of undying (always), emerald (sometimes).
- Spawns in woodland mansions and raid waves (wave 5+).

### Evoker — Combat
- Prioritize evoker over everything — it summons vexes that complicate fights.
- Sprint at it constantly so it cannot complete full cast sequences.
- Dodge fang lines: they spawn in a line toward you — sidestep perpendicular.
- After killing evoker, deal with remaining vexes: they die naturally after ~2 minutes but will damage you.
- Totem of undying in your offhand keeps you alive at 1 HP once — carry it going into raids or mansion.

## Ravager

### Ravager — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 100 HP (50 hearts).
- Melee damage: 12 HP (Easy), 18 HP (Normal), 27 HP (Hard). Massive damage.
- Roar attack after being hit: knocks back all nearby entities in a radius, deals 6 HP.
- Destroys leaves, crops, vines, and some non-full blocks with its movement.
- Spawns during raids (wave 3+), sometimes ridden by pillager/vindicator/evoker.
- Drops: saddle (always), emerald (sometimes).
- Immune to knockback.

### Ravager — Combat
- Do not melee unless you have full iron or better armor.
- When ravager is hit, immediately back away to avoid the roar AoE.
- Use a bow or crossbow: maintain distance and whittle down its 100 HP.
- Knock pillager/vindicator riders off first using a knockback weapon or bow.
- Bees can distract ravagers — irrelevant but noted.
- After raid, loot the village chests for emeralds and trade with surviving villagers.

## Phantom

### Phantom — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Swoop damage: 6 HP (Normal).
- Spawns at night or during thunderstorms above players who have not slept for 3+ in-game days.
- Takes damage in sunlight.
- Flies in circles then swoops down to attack. Multiple phantoms spawn at once.
- Drops: phantom membrane (used for elytra repair).

### Phantom — Combat
- Sleep. The simplest counter — sleep every night to reset the spawn timer.
- If you cannot sleep: fight at height, use a bow during swoop approach phase.
- Phantoms fly at y ~20+ above player. They swoop down — shoot them during the dive.
- Cats cause phantoms to flee. Keep a cat nearby.
- If swarmed: pillar up in a 1x1 tower, they cannot easily swoop at you there.
- Looting a bow helps: more membrane drops per kill.

## Drowned

### Drowned — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Melee damage same as zombie: 3–6 HP.
- Some carry a trident: thrown for 9 HP (Normal), melee for 9 HP.
- Spawns in ocean, rivers, and when a zombie is submerged for 30 seconds.
- Slower on land than in water. Very fast swimmer.
- Drops: rotten flesh, sometimes trident, sometimes copper ingot, gold ingot (1.21.x change: trident drop rate ~8.5% without Looting).

### Drowned — Combat
- Never fight drowned in water — they are faster and dodge your attacks.
- Lure them onto land: they follow you onto shore.
- On land, combat is identical to zombies.
- Trident-carrying drowned: dodge the thrown trident (audible whoosh), approach while they retrieve.
- Farm drowned for tridents: build a trap in ocean or river biome. Use Looting III for better drop rates.

## Husk

### Husk — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Melee damage: same as zombie. Inflicts Hunger effect on hit (15 seconds on Normal).
- Does NOT burn in sunlight. Spawns in desert biomes.
- Otherwise behaves exactly like a zombie.
- Drops: rotten flesh, occasionally iron/carrot/potato.

### Husk — Combat
- Same as zombie tactics. The Hunger effect is the main danger — it drains your food bar.
- Carry food when fighting husks or traversing deserts at night.
- Keep food bar at max before engaging groups.

## Stray

### Stray — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Shoots arrows of Slowness (15 seconds per hit on Normal difficulty).
- Does NOT burn in sunlight. Spawns in cold/snowy biomes.
- Otherwise behaves like a skeleton.
- Drops: bones, arrows, tipped arrows of Slowness.

### Stray — Combat
- Same as skeleton tactics. Slowness arrows stack and can immobilize you — close gap fast.
- Sprint in zigzag to close. With Slowness applied, retreat becomes impossible — prioritize offense.
- Strays in ice plains can be hard to see (blue-white coloring). Watch for movement.

## Cave Spider

### Cave Spider — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 12 HP (6 hearts).
- Melee damage: 2 HP (Easy), 3 HP (Normal), 4 HP (Hard). Plus Poison on Normal/Hard.
- Poison: 1 HP per 1.25 seconds, lasts 7 seconds (Normal), 15 seconds (Hard). Does not kill below 1 HP.
- Spawns only from cave spider spawners in mineshafts.
- Smaller than a regular spider — fits through 1×1 gaps.

### Cave Spider — Combat
- Bring milk or honey bottles to mineshafts — neutralizes poison.
- Block all 6 faces of the spawner with solid blocks before engaging to stop spawning. Then break or light the spawner.
- Alternatively: place torches on the spawner — blocks spawning without destroying it.
- Fight in wider corridors. Cave spiders swarm and surround in tight spaces.
- Bane of Arthropods IV+ kills cave spiders in 1 hit with a diamond sword.

## Silverfish

### Silverfish — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 8 HP (4 hearts).
- Melee damage: 1 HP (Easy/Normal), 1.5 HP (Hard). Low but they call reinforcements.
- Spawns from infested stone blocks (strongholds, igloos, extreme hills).
- When attacking, calls all nearby silverfish — they pour out of walls.
- Drops: nothing.

### Silverfish — Combat
- Use Bane of Arthropods — kills silverfish in 1 hit and is the purpose of the enchant here.
- Don't mine infested stone in a stronghold carelessly — you'll trigger swarms.
- Recognize infested stone: slightly darker texture, breaks faster than normal stone.
- Sweep attack with sword hits multiple silverfish at once.
- In strongholds, clear the room before looting. One silverfish can cascade into a dozen.

## Slime

### Slime — Stats and Behavior
- Health: Large=16 HP, Medium=4 HP, Small=1 HP.
- Melee damage: Large=4 HP (Normal), Medium=2 HP, Small=0 HP (no damage).
- Splits into 2-4 smaller slimes on death. Large → 2-4 medium, medium → 2-4 small.
- Spawns in slime chunks (specific chunk seeds) below y=40. Also spawns in swamp biomes at night during certain moon phases.
- Drops: slimeballs (only from small slimes).

### Slime — Combat
- Kill large slimes first, then clean up mediums, ignore smalls (no damage).
- Stone or iron sword sufficient — slimes have low health.
- Don't fight in tight spaces: mediums and smalls flood the area.
- Slimeballs are craft ingredients for leads, sticky pistons, slime blocks. Farm them in slime chunks.
- Standing on a slime block reduces fall damage — use terrain during fights.

## Magma Cube

### Magma Cube — Stats and Behavior
- Health: Large=16 HP, Medium=4 HP, Small=1 HP.
- Melee damage: Large=6 HP (Normal), Medium=3 HP, Small=2.5 HP. Fire damage in addition.
- Behaves like slime — splits on death.
- Spawns in Nether, especially in basalt deltas and nether fortresses.
- Fire resistant — immune to fire, lava.
- Drops: magma cream (only from large/medium on death).

### Magma Cube — Combat
- Same split mechanic as slime. Kill large, then medium, then small.
- Smalls still deal damage unlike slime smalls — don't ignore them.
- Magma cubes jump high — be ready to block or sidestep.
- Wear fire protection armor in the Nether to reduce fire damage from contact.
- In basalt deltas: footing is difficult. Fight on flat surfaces; falling into lava is a constant risk.

## Ghast

### Ghast — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 10 HP (5 hearts).
- Fireball damage: 17 HP (direct hit, Normal), explosion knockback + burn without full armor.
- Floats at high altitude. Fires fireballs every 3 seconds while it has line of sight.
- Crying/open eyes = preparing to fire. Closed eyes = passive.
- Drops: ghast tear, gunpowder. Rare drops.
- Spawns in soul sand valley, basalt deltas, nether wastes.

### Ghast — Combat
- Reflect fireballs: hit them back with any melee attack or arrow. One reflected fireball kills a ghast instantly (deals 1000 HP).
- Timing the reflection: fireball has a short windup. Swing sword when fireball is close.
- A bow works too: ghasts have only 10 HP, so two arrows kill them. Use Flame bow for extra damage.
- Fireballs set terrain on fire — in the Nether this is normal, but protect your structures with non-flammable blocks.
- Don't fight ghasts near lava — getting knocked into lava is lethal.
- Power II bow kills a ghast in one arrow.

## Blaze

### Blaze — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Fireball barrage: shoots 3 fireballs rapidly, each dealing 5 HP fire damage (Normal). Then recharges.
- Deals contact damage (fire).
- Spawns in nether fortresses from blaze spawners.
- Drops: blaze rod (required for brewing stands, eyes of ender).
- Immune to fire.

### Blaze — Combat
- Snowballs deal 3 HP per hit to blazes and extinguish their fire. Carry a stack of snowballs.
- Shield blocks fireballs entirely if raised at the right moment.
- Rush in after its 3-shot burst: it recharges for ~2 seconds. Sword during that window.
- Blaze spawners: find the spawner, build a box around it (leave a 1-block gap to attack through). Farm blaze rods safely.
- Fire Protection armor is mandatory — multiple blazes can kill you in seconds unprotected.
- Blaze rods are critical. Kill at least 12+ blazes to stock up.

## Wither Skeleton

### Wither Skeleton — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 20 HP (10 hearts).
- Melee damage: 7 HP (Normal). Inflicts Wither effect (1 HP per 2 seconds, 10 seconds).
- Spawns only in nether fortresses.
- Does NOT burn in sunlight (in the Nether).
- Drops: coal, bones, stone sword (rarely), wither skeleton skull (2.5% drop rate, 5.5% with Looting III).

### Wither Skeleton — Combat
- Wither effect turns hearts black — you cannot see your actual HP easily. Milk or Totem clears it.
- Fight at range with bow to avoid the Wither debuff.
- At melee: attack from under a 2-block ceiling — wither skeletons are 2.4 blocks tall, can't enter.
- Looting III enchantment is essential for skull farming. Without it, expect 40+ kills per skull.
- Need 3 skulls + 4 soul sand/soil to summon the Wither boss.

## Piglin Brute

### Piglin Brute — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 50 HP (25 hearts).
- Axe damage: 13 HP (Normal). Sprints at you.
- Spawns in bastion remnants, usually guarding treasure rooms.
- Does NOT respond to gold: cannot be appeased with gold armor or gold offerings. Permanently hostile.
- Drops: golden axe (rarely, may be enchanted).

### Piglin Brute — Combat
- One of the most dangerous Nether mobs. Do not engage without iron/diamond armor.
- Use bows to soften from range before entering melee.
- Shield-block its axe swing, then riposte. Axes disable shields for 5 seconds — time your blocks carefully.
- Don't fight multiple brutes at once. Lure them apart using corridors.
- Smite enchantment does NOT apply to piglin brutes (they are not undead).
- Check bastion layout before looting. Treasure rooms often have 2-4 brutes.

## Warden

### Warden — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 500 HP (250 hearts). The highest health mob in the game.
- Melee damage: 30 HP (Easy), 45 HP (Normal), 67.5 HP (Hard). One-shots nearly any armor.
- Sonic boom attack: 10 HP ranged damage that bypasses armor entirely. Fires every 5 seconds.
- Blind: relies on vibration detection and smell, not sight. Walking, sprinting, placing/breaking blocks create vibrations.
- Sniff behavior: if no vibration for ~7 seconds, it sniffs around. Takes ~4.2 seconds for a full dig-in if no disturbance.
- Spawns in Ancient City (Deep Dark biome) after enough sculk shriekers are triggered.
- Drops: nothing (1 sculk catalyst only). Not worth killing.

### Warden — Avoidance (Do Not Fight)
- The warden is designed to be avoided, not fought. Flee whenever possible.
- Sneak at all times in the Ancient City — sneaking produces no vibrations.
- Throw projectiles (snowballs, arrows) away from yourself to distract it.
- If discovered, sprint away: the warden is fast but you can outrun it briefly.
- If cornered: warden digs out after ~60 seconds of no disturbance. Stay still, do not panic-place blocks.
- Do not attempt melee unless you have full Netherite armor, max enchants, and golden apples. Even then it will likely kill you.
- Sonic boom goes through walls — hiding behind blocks doesn't help.
- Multiple shriekers activate a warden: count shrieker activations (max 3 allowed before warden spawns).

## Breeze

### Breeze — Stats and Behavior
- Health: 30 HP (15 hearts).
- Wind charge attack: 1 HP direct damage but massive knockback. Also activates buttons, levers, doors.
- Jumps and leaps constantly. Very mobile. Cannot be hit with arrows while airborne (deflects).
- Spawns in Trial Chambers (underground structures added in 1.21).
- Drops: breeze rod (used for crafting wind charges, mace weapon).

### Breeze — Combat
- Arrows are deflected by the breeze — use melee or wind charges.
- Wind charges (from dropped breeze rods) can be thrown back.
- Chase it and melee: its wind charges do very little damage. Sprint-attack combos work.
- The knockback from wind charges can knock you into hazards. Fight in open areas with no drop-offs.
- Critical hits with the Mace (a weapon introduced in 1.21, made with breeze rods + heavy core) deal massive damage from fall height — jump-attack a breeze for huge damage.
- Mace: 0.5 damage per block fallen stacks onto the base hit. Falling 10 blocks = +5 bonus damage on hit.

## Combat Mechanics

### Critical Hits
- A critical hit deals +50% damage.
- Trigger condition: attack while falling (not on the ground, not on a ladder, not swimming, not affected by blindness).
- Jump, then swing at the peak of the jump or on the way down. Stars particle effect confirms a crit.
- Sprint-attacking does NOT trigger a crit (it triggers a knockback attack instead).
- Crit + sprint-attack are mutually exclusive. Choose crit for more damage, sprint-attack for knockback.
- With a netherite sword: 8 base × 1.5 = 12 HP per critical hit. Sharpness V adds 3 HP → 16.5 HP per crit.

### Attack Cooldown
- Weapons have a cooldown indicator (small bar below hotbar). Wait for 100% charge for full damage.
- Attacking before 100% charge deals proportionally less damage.
- Swords charge fastest (~0.6 seconds). Axes charge slower (~1.0 second) but deal more damage per hit.
- Spam-clicking is ineffective and wastes attacks. Time your swings.

### Sweep Attack
- Swords have a sweep attack: when at full charge, a swing hits all enemies in a short arc.
- Sweep deals 1 HP + (sword damage – 1 HP) to secondary targets.
- Useful against groups of zombies or spiders. Not available with axes.
- Jump before hitting to trigger a crit instead of sweep (if you want single-target burst).

### Shield Mechanics
- Equip a shield in your offhand. Right-click to raise it.
- Blocking reduces melee damage by 100% from the front if within the blocking arc (~180 degrees).
- Blocking reduces arrow damage by 100%.
- Cannot block explosions at full effectiveness — reduces some damage but not all.
- Axes (including axe attacks from vindicators/piglin brutes) disable your shield for 5 seconds on a successful hit. Back away when shield is disabled.
- Blocking a melee attack while stationary and looking at the attacker reflects ~0 damage — you just negate it.
- Banner can be added to shield (purely cosmetic in 1.21.1).

### Knockback Mechanics
- Sprint-attacking deals knockback to targets.
- Knockback I/II enchantment increases knockback distance.
- Use knockback to push mobs off cliffs, into lava, or into cacti.
- Some mobs are immune to knockback: Ravager, Elder Guardian, Warden.

## Weapons

### Sword Damage Values
- Wood sword: 4 HP base damage.
- Stone sword: 5 HP base damage.
- Iron sword: 6 HP base damage.
- Gold sword: 4 HP base damage (same as wood, but very fast cooldown — useless for damage).
- Diamond sword: 7 HP base damage.
- Netherite sword: 8 HP base damage.
- Netherite swords have higher durability and knockback resistance compared to diamond.

### Axe Damage Values
- Wood axe: 7 HP base. Stone axe: 9 HP. Iron axe: 9 HP. Gold axe: 7 HP. Diamond axe: 9 HP. Netherite axe: 10 HP.
- Axes deal more base damage than swords but charge slower (~1 second vs ~0.625 seconds).
- Axes can disable shields. Use axes vs shield-wielding opponents in PvP.
- Axes do NOT have sweep attack — purely single-target.

### Trident
- Melee damage: 9 HP. Thrown damage: 8–9 HP (plus Loyalty for return).
- Impaling enchantment: +2.5 HP per level vs aquatic mobs (including drowned in water).
- Riptide: propels player when thrown in rain/water — cannot be combined with Loyalty or Channeling.
- Channeling: summons lightning during thunderstorms on hit. Converts villagers to witches, pigs to piglins (useful).
- Loyalty I-III: trident returns after throwing. Essential for combat use.

### Mace (New in 1.21)
- Base damage: 6 HP. Made from a breeze rod + heavy core (from Vault in Trial Chambers).
- Smash attack: gains +0.5 HP per block fallen before hitting. No damage cap.
- Jump from height before hitting for massive single-hit damage. Works with crits.
- Smash attack resets fall damage — you don't take fall damage after successfully hitting with mace smash.
- Wind Burst enchantment (mace-only): knocks enemies back on smash hit.
- Density enchantment (mace-only): increases smash damage per block fallen.

### Bow Usage
- Charge time: hold right-click for up to 1 second for full draw (~9 HP at full draw, Normal difficulty).
- Partial draws deal proportionally less damage.
- Flame enchantment: sets targets on fire for additional 4 HP fire damage over time.
- Power I-V: +25% damage per level. Power V = ~12.5 HP per arrow at full draw.
- Infinity: infinite arrows as long as you have at least 1 arrow in inventory. Cannot enchant with Mending.
- Punch I-II: knockback on arrow hit.

### Crossbow Usage
- Charge time: ~1.25 seconds. Must be pre-loaded before combat.
- Damage: 6–11 HP (similar to bow at full draw).
- Quick Charge I-III: reduces loading time significantly.
- Multishot: fires 3 arrows in a spread. Excellent vs groups. Cannot be combined with Piercing.
- Piercing I-IV: arrows pass through entities. Useful for pillager groups.
- Pre-load crossbows before entering combat — they fire instantly when loaded.

## Armor

### Armor Progression and Protection
- Leather armor: total 7 armor points (3.5 armor bars), ~28% damage reduction.
- Chainmail armor: total 12 armor points, ~48% damage reduction.
- Iron armor: total 15 armor points, ~60% damage reduction.
- Gold armor: total 11 armor points, ~44% damage reduction. High enchantability — use for enchanting then discard.
- Diamond armor: total 20 armor points, ~80% damage reduction.
- Netherite armor: total 20 armor points (same as diamond) + knockback resistance + fire resistance + higher durability.
- Full diamond or netherite armor + Protection IV = ~4 HP damage from most un-enchanted hits.

### Armor Toughness
- Diamond and Netherite armor have armor toughness which reduces large-hit damage further.
- Diamond: 2 toughness per piece (8 total). Netherite: 3 toughness per piece (12 total).
- Toughness makes high-damage hits less effective at bypassing armor.

### Armor Enchantments
- Protection I-IV: reduces all damage by 4% per level. Protection IV on all 4 pieces = 64% further reduction.
- Fire Protection I-IV: reduces fire/lava damage specifically. Stacks better than Protection for Nether.
- Blast Protection I-IV: reduces explosion damage. Essential for creeper/ghast/TNT scenarios.
- Projectile Protection I-IV: reduces arrow/projectile damage. Best for skeleton/pillager encounters.
- Do not mix Protection types: use Protection IV on all 4 pieces for general combat, or specialize per biome.
- Thorns I-III: reflects damage back to attacker on each hit. Drains durability faster.
- Feather Falling I-IV on boots: reduces fall damage. Feather Falling IV + Protection IV boots = minimal fall damage.
- Depth Strider I-III: faster movement in water. Useful for drowned/ocean combat.
- Frost Walker: converts water to frosted ice under your feet. Walk on water surfaces.

## Weapon Upgrades and Combat Bonuses

### Sharpness
- Sharpness I-V: +1 HP per level extra damage (actually +0.5 per level in 1.21, with I being +1). Applies to all mobs.
- Sharpness V: +3 HP extra damage. Best general-purpose weapon enchant.
- Cannot be combined with Smite, Bane of Arthropods, or Cleaving (future version).
- Use Sharpness for general combat. Use Smite or Bane only if you're farming specific mobs.

### Smite
- Smite I-V: +2.5 HP per level vs undead mobs only.
- Undead mobs: zombies, skeletons, wither skeletons, drowned, husks, strays, phantoms, the Wither boss, zoglins.
- Smite V: +12.5 HP vs undead. Diamond sword + Smite V = 19.5 HP per hit. Kills most undead in 1 hit.
- Best enchant for skeleton/zombie farms. Use Smite V for End portal stronghold clearing (lots of undead nearby).

### Bane of Arthropods
- Bane of Arthropods I-V: +2.5 HP per level vs arthropod mobs + applies Slowness IV briefly.
- Arthropod mobs: spiders, cave spiders, silverfish, endermites, bees.
- Bane of Arthropods V + diamond sword = 19.5 HP. One-shots unarmored spiders/silverfish.
- Niche use: only worth it if specifically farming spider strings or cave spider spawners.

### Looting
- Looting I-III: increases mob drop quantity and rare drop chance.
- Looting III: +2 common drops, significantly better rare drop rates (e.g., tridents from drowned: 5.5% vs 8.5% on final level with Looting III from 2% base).
- Required for efficient farming: skulls (wither skeletons), tridents (drowned), ender pearls, all rare drops.
- Always have Looting III on your main sword.

### Fire Aspect
- Fire Aspect I-II: sets target on fire. Fire deals additional damage over time.
- Fire Aspect II: 8 HP fire damage over 8 seconds (in addition to sword damage).
- Useful: mobs drop cooked meat when killed by fire — auto-cooks pork/beef/chicken.
- Not useful vs fire-resistant mobs: blazes, magma cubes, ghasts, striders, piglins.

## Food and Healing

### Combat Food Priority
- Golden Apple: restores 4 HP instantly + Absorption IV (8 temp HP) + Regeneration II (4 ticks). Use when low HP in combat.
- Enchanted Golden Apple (Notch Apple): Absorption IV (8 HP), Regeneration II (20 seconds), Fire Resistance, Resistance. Extremely rare — crafted with 8 gold blocks. Use only in boss fights.
- Steak/Cooked Pork: restores 8 food points + 12.8 saturation. Best standard food for healing between fights.
- Cooked Chicken: 6 food, 7.2 saturation. Good fallback.
- Bread: 5 food, 6 saturation. Easy to farm.
- Food only restores HP when food bar is at least 18/20 (natural regen). Keep food bar full.
- Regeneration from high saturation: you regen 1 HP per ~0.5 seconds at full food. This is your primary healing.

### Healing Potions
- Potion of Healing (Instant Health I): restores 4 HP instantly. II restores 8 HP.
- Throw splash potion at feet for AoE heal (useful in group situations, heals allies).
- Splash Potion of Healing damages undead mobs (they take damage from healing). Throw at zombie groups.
- Potion of Regeneration: regenerates HP over time. Duration II + Regeneration II = strong sustained healing.
- Potion of Absorption: grants temporary yellow hearts. Brew from golden apple + awkward potion.

## Boss Fights

### Ender Dragon — Overview
- Health: 200 HP (100 hearts).
- Dragon breath: deals 3 HP per second. Deals damage through armor.
- Fireball: 6 HP splash, ignites terrain.
- Melee: 10 HP (Easy), 15 HP (Normal), 22.5 HP (Hard) if it rams you.
- End crystals on obsidian pillars heal it 1 HP per 0.2 seconds while connected. Destroy all crystals before damaging the dragon.

### Ender Dragon — Crystal Destruction
- Shoot end crystals with bow from ground level. Each crystal explodes when hit.
- Iron cage crystals (2 of them): shoot through the iron bars, aim carefully.
- Use Flame bow or Power bow. Each crystal shot triggers an explosion — don't stand below.
- Bring extra arrows: at least 64. Infinity bow is ideal.
- Crystals respawn if you re-enter The End after a failed fight — they do not respawn mid-fight.

### Ender Dragon — Attack Phase
- Dragon perches on the exit portal fountain to attack.
- Dragon is only vulnerable on its head when perching OR from above when flying.
- Hits to the body and wings deal 0 damage.
- Aim for the head. Bow: 2-4 hits to the head kills it if all crystals are gone.
- Stand on the fountain edge and shoot upward at the head while it bites.
- Endermen fill The End — avoid looking at them. Wear pumpkin head if you can't avoid eye contact.

### Ender Dragon — Dragon Breath
- The dragon spawns a purple acid pool when it perches. Move off the fountain immediately.
- Don't stand in the breath — it deals damage through armor and can't be blocked.
- Snowball/arrow the head while dodging the breath.

### Ender Dragon — Full Preparation Checklist
- Full iron armor minimum, diamond preferred. Enchant with Protection IV.
- 64+ arrows or Infinity bow with Power III+.
- Ender pearls (16+) for vertical movement on pillars.
- Golden apples (4+) for emergencies.
- Blocks (64+) to climb pillars if needed.
- Beds: place a bed in The End (it explodes — use strategically for 20 HP explosion vs dragon head while perching).
- Sword with Sharpness V for perch phase melee (head shots with sword also work).

### Wither Boss — Overview
- Health: 300 HP (150 hearts) in Java Edition 1.21.1. Three heads.
- Spawn: place 4 soul sand/soil in T-shape, place 3 wither skeleton skulls on top. Stand back.
- Skull projectile: black/blue skulls deal 8 HP + Wither effect. Blue skulls can destroy most blocks.
- Star phase (below 50% HP): the wither becomes immune to arrows and gains a shield.
- Drops: nether star (required for beacon).

### Wither — Phase 1 (Above 50% HP)
- Use bow and arrow. Wither flies, making melee difficult initially.
- Smite V bow shreds its HP quickly — it IS undead.
- Keep distance: skull projectiles are fast. Stay mobile.
- Milk of bucket ready: Wither effect can kill you.
- Fight underground or in a tunnel: constrains wither movement, easier to track.
- Best location: bedrock tunnel or obsidian cage underground. Wither cannot break bedrock.

### Wither — Phase 2 (Below 50% HP, Star Phase)
- Wither descends to the ground and gains a shield. Arrows do 0 damage.
- Rush in with sword. Smite V sword with critical hits.
- Wither becomes stunned briefly after each heavy melee hit — exploit these windows.
- Keep Regeneration II potion active and golden apples ready.
- Total fight time with good gear: ~60-90 seconds for phase 2.

### Wither — Tunnel Strategy
- Dig a 3×1 tunnel in a mountain or underground.
- Spawn Wither at the far end. It gets stuck on the ceiling due to low clearance.
- Stand right next to it and sword its body (all 3 heads are reachable).
- It cannot fly or dodge in the tunnel. This trivializes the fight.
- The wither destroys blocks it touches during spawn — use obsidian lining or accept some tunnel damage.

## Raids

### Bad Omen and Raid Trigger
- Bad Omen is applied when you kill an Illager patrol captain (carrying an Ominous Banner).
- In 1.21: Bad Omen is replaced by Ominous Bottle system for Trial Chambers. Classic raids still use old system where entering a village with Bad Omen triggers a raid.
- Raid begins when you enter a village with Bad Omen: a progress bar appears.
- Hero of the Village effect granted after raid victory: massive discount from villager trades (scales with raid difficulty).

### Raid Waves
- Wave 1: Pillagers only.
- Wave 2: Pillagers + Vindicators.
- Wave 3: Pillagers + Vindicators + Ravager.
- Wave 4: Pillagers + Witches + Evokers.
- Wave 5: Pillagers + Vindicators + Evokers + Ravager (ridden by evoker).
- Wave 6: Pillagers + Vindicators.
- Wave 7 (Hard difficulty only): Pillagers + Vindicators + Evokers + Witches + 2 Ravagers.
- Kill all mobs in a wave to start the next wave after a brief delay.

### Raid Strategy
- Prepare a defensive position before entering the village. High ground, walls, or a tower.
- Kill evokers first: they summon vexes and cast fang attacks.
- Kill witches second: their potions can debilitate you quickly.
- Use crossbow with Multishot for packed pillager groups.
- Ravager: use bow from elevation. It cannot pathfind to high ground easily.
- Totems of undying from evokers: equip in offhand before wave 4+. You will die otherwise.
- Village bells: ring them (right-click) to cause nearby illagers to glow and alert villagers to go inside.
- After raid: all surviving villagers gift you items (food, arrows, emeralds) as gratitude.

### Totem of Undying
- Obtained only from evokers (raid or woodland mansion).
- Equip in offhand or mainhand. Activates automatically when you take fatal damage.
- Effect on activation: restores 1 HP, gives Regeneration II for 45 seconds, Absorption II for 5 seconds, Fire Resistance I for 40 seconds.
- Single use — consumed on activation.
- Carry into every dangerous situation: boss fights, raids, deep dark exploration.

## PvE General Tactics

### Terrain Advantage
- High ground: attack from above for easier hits, harder for melee mobs to reach.
- Pillar up 3+ blocks vs melee mobs: most cannot climb. Safe to shoot from above.
- Water: never fight melee vs drowned or other aquatic mobs in water. You move slower.
- Lava: use to funnel mobs (mob funnels to 1-block gap with lava at bottom). Good for farms.
- Corners: back into a corner against 1-2 mobs to limit their flanking. Bad vs 3+ mobs.
- Walls and cover: break skeleton line-of-sight. Use corners to peek-and-shoot.

### Engagement Rules
- Never fight more than 2-3 mobs simultaneously without strong armor.
- Separate groups: run to draw individual mobs, fight one at a time.
- Retreat and reset if HP drops below 6 hearts. Eat food while retreating.
- Don't fight in water if it can be avoided — movement penalty is severe.
- Light up areas before mining or looting to prevent ambushes.

### Night Survival
- Mob spawn light level: mobs spawn on blocks with light level 0 (changed in 1.18+ — light level must be 0 on the block, not just nearby blocks).
- Torches provide light level 14 at placement block. Place torches every 12 blocks to prevent spawning in passages.
- At night on surface: stay in a lit shelter or keep moving. Don't stand still in dark areas.
- Sleeping: sleep immediately at first sunset if possible — skips night, prevents phantom spawn timer increase.
- If caught outside at night: pillar up 5+ blocks on a 1×1 tower. Mobs cannot reach you. Wait for dawn.
- Dawn: zombies and skeletons burn in sunlight after ~20 seconds of exposure. Wait for them to die rather than fighting.

### Fleeing
- Sprint (double-tap W or hold sprint key) to outrun most mobs.
- Mobs that sprint with you: skeletons (sorta), cave spiders (fast), baby zombies (very fast), vindicators, piglin brutes.
- Mobs that cannot keep up with sprinting: zombies, creepers, husks, strays, pillagers (somewhat).
- If fleeing, sprint in a straight line into dense terrain to break line-of-sight for ranged mobs.
- Ender pearls can teleport you away quickly. Keep 2-3 in hotbar for emergencies.

## Mob Farms

### Spawner-Based Farms
- Find a monster spawner (zombie, skeleton, spider, cave spider, blaze).
- Clear a 9×9×9 area around the spawner. Spawner works within 16 blocks of a player.
- Build a water flushing system to move mobs to a kill point.
- Drop chute: 23-block drop reduces most mobs to 1 HP — finish with 1 sword hit for XP and drops.
- Blaze spawner farm: essential for blaze rods. Build in nether fortress, box around spawner.
- Cave spider farm: complex due to their small size, but string drops are valuable.

### Dark Room Mob Farms
- Build a large dark room (far from other light sources) at Y=80-120.
- Pack as many platforms as possible. Mobs spawn, fall into a kill chamber.
- Use water streams to push mobs off platforms into a drop kill shaft.
- Light level 0 required on spawning platforms. Check with a debug screen or experience.
- Only hostile mobs spawn in dark rooms: zombies, skeletons, creepers, spiders. No animals.
- Looting III sword on kill spot for maximum drops.

### AFK Farming
- Position yourself within 128 blocks of farm (spawning range).
- Most spawner farms: stand directly above or at the kill spot.
- AFK fish farms are separate (not combat-related but passive XP).
- Use AFK farms to accumulate XP for enchanting without active combat.

## Quick Reference

### Mob Damage Summary (Normal Difficulty)
- Zombie: 4 HP. Skeleton arrow: 3 HP. Creeper explosion: up to 49 HP. Spider: 3 HP. Enderman: 10 HP. Witch potions: variable. Pillager bolt: 6-11 HP. Vindicator axe: 13 HP. Evoker fang: 6 HP. Ravager: 18 HP. Phantom swoop: 6 HP. Drowned: 4 HP. Blaze fireball: 5 HP. Wither skeleton: 7 HP + wither. Piglin brute: 13 HP. Warden melee: 45 HP. Warden sonic: 10 HP (bypasses armor).

### Priority Kill Order in Groups
1. Evoker (summons vexes, most dangerous caster).
2. Witch (disables you with potions).
3. Vindicator (highest melee damage).
4. Pillager (ranged).
5. Ravager (last — use bow).

### Critical Situations
- On fire: run through water or rain. Don't roll — that's not in vanilla Minecraft.
- Wither effect: drink milk immediately.
- Poison: drink milk or honey bottle. It won't kill you below 1 HP, but pairs with other damage.
- Below 4 hearts: stop fighting. Eat golden apple, retreat, let natural regen work.
- Surrounded with no escape: use Totem of Undying + golden apple combo for emergency survival.
