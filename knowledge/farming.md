# Minecraft 1.21.1 Farming Knowledge

## Crop Farming Basics

### Universal Crop Requirements
Plant crops only on farmland blocks. Till dirt or grass with a hoe to create farmland. Farmland must stay hydrated to support crop growth. Hydrated farmland appears darker than dry farmland. Dry farmland will cause crops to grow slower or not at all. Crops need light level 9 or higher to grow. Use torches or glowstone above crops for indoor farms.

### Hydration Rules
Water hydrates farmland within a 4-block radius on the same Y-level. One water source block at the center of a 9x9 plot hydrates all surrounding farmland. Place water sources every 8 blocks in row farms. Farmland reverts to dirt if no player is nearby and it dries out or gets stepped on by mobs. Use a block above water to prevent mob trampling.

### Bone Meal for Instant Growth
Right-click any growable crop with bone meal to advance its growth stage. Some crops require multiple bone meal applications to reach full maturity. Bone meal does not work on cactus, sugar cane, or nether wart in the Bedrock version — but in Java 1.21.1 bone meal does work on sugar cane and bamboo. Use bone meal to bootstrap early-game food supply.

## Wheat Farming

### Planting and Harvesting Wheat
Plant wheat seeds on hydrated farmland. Obtain wheat seeds by breaking tall grass or harvesting mature wheat. Wheat has 8 growth stages (0–7). Harvest at stage 7 for maximum yield: 1 wheat and 0–3 seeds per plant. Replant seeds immediately after harvesting. Wheat is used to breed cows, sheep, and horses.

### Wheat Yield Optimization
Use Fortune III enchantment on your hoe at harvest to increase seed drops up to 6 per plant. Apply bone meal to skip growth stages. Plant in a 9x9 grid with a central water source for maximum density. Village farms contain pre-grown wheat you can harvest early game.

## Carrot Farming

### Planting and Harvesting Carrots
Carrots are both seed and food — plant the carrot directly on farmland. Carrots have 8 growth stages. Harvest at full maturity for 1–4 carrots per plant. Fortune III hoe increases yield significantly. Use carrots to breed pigs and rabbits. Golden carrots provide the best saturation of any single food item.

---

## Potato Farming

### Planting and Harvesting Potatoes
Plant raw potatoes directly on hydrated farmland. Potatoes have 8 growth stages. Harvest mature plants for 1–4 potatoes plus a rare poisonous potato drop. Cook potatoes in a furnace or smoker for baked potatoes, which restore 5 hunger and 6 saturation. Potatoes breed pigs.

---

## Beetroot Farming

### Planting and Harvesting Beetroot
Plant beetroot seeds on hydrated farmland. Beetroot seeds drop from mature beetroot crops. Beetroot has 4 growth stages — harvest at stage 3. Each harvest gives 1 beetroot and 0–3 seeds. Beetroot restores only 1 hunger; use it mainly for red dye or beetroot soup. Bone meal accelerates beetroot effectively.

---

## Melon Farming

### Growing Melons
Plant melon seeds on hydrated farmland. Melon seeds come from melon slices in a crafting grid or from dungeon chests. The melon stem grows across 8 stages. When the stem is mature it spawns a melon block on any adjacent dirt, farmland, grass, or coarse dirt. Do not harvest the stem — only harvest the melon block. The stem regrows a new melon after each harvest.

### Melon Layout Tips
Leave one empty block on each side of every stem for melon growth. A 1x2 plot per stem (1 farmland + 1 adjacent dirt/grass) is the most compact layout. Melons give 1–8 melon slices when broken. Breaking with Fortune III gives up to 9 slices. Slices restore 2 hunger each.

---

## Pumpkin Farming

### Growing Pumpkins
Plant pumpkin seeds on hydrated farmland the same way as melon seeds. Pumpkin stems behave identically to melon stems. The mature stem spawns a pumpkin block on an adjacent valid block. Harvest only the pumpkin block — leave the stem. Pumpkins are required for iron golem and snow golem construction and for crafting pumpkin pie.

### Pumpkin Pie Crafting
Combine 1 pumpkin + 1 sugar + 1 egg in a crafting grid (no pattern required in 1.21.1 — use the recipe book). Pumpkin pie restores 8 hunger and 4.8 saturation.

---

## Sugar Cane Farming

### Planting Sugar Cane
Place sugar cane on sand, dirt, grass, or coarse dirt that is directly adjacent (horizontally) to water. Sugar cane grows to a maximum height of 3 blocks. Harvest the second and third blocks only — leave the bottom block to regrow. Sugar cane grows approximately 1 block per 18 minutes under ideal conditions.

### Sugar Cane Automation
Place an observer block facing the third sugar cane block. When the cane reaches height 3 the observer detects the block update and triggers a piston. The piston breaks the top two blocks. Use a water stream to funnel cane into hoppers feeding a chest. Sugar is used for cake, pumpkin pie, fermented spider eye, and potions.

---

## Cactus Farming

### Planting Cactus
Place cactus on sand only. Cactus cannot have any block (including other cactus) adjacent horizontally — it pops off immediately. Cactus grows to 3 blocks tall. Harvest upper blocks while leaving the base. Cactus damages mobs and players that touch it. Cook cactus in a furnace for green dye.

### Cactus Farm Design
Stagger cactus in a checkerboard pattern on sand to satisfy the no-adjacent-block rule. Place a block diagonally above each cactus base at height 3 so the growing top hits it and drops as an item. Use hoppers below to collect. No pistons or observers needed for this passive design.

---

## Bamboo Farming

### Planting Bamboo
Place bamboo on grass, dirt, sand, gravel, mycelium, podzol, or coarse dirt. Bamboo grows very fast — up to 16 blocks tall. Harvest from the second block up. Bamboo is used as furnace fuel (very efficient), for scaffolding, and for crafting bamboo wood blocks and bamboo rafts in 1.21.1.

### Bamboo Automation
Use a piston + observer at the desired harvest height. When bamboo grows past the observer's target block the observer fires the piston. Items fall and collect into hoppers. Bamboo burns for 0.25 items per stick equivalent — smelt 0.5 items per bamboo piece.

---

## Nether Wart Farming

### Growing Nether Wart
Plant nether wart on soul sand only. Soul sand can be in the Overworld, Nether, or End — location does not matter. Nether wart does not require light. It has 4 growth stages (0–3). Harvest at stage 3 for 2–4 nether wart drops. Bone meal does not work on nether wart. Nether wart is the base ingredient for almost every potion.

### Nether Wart Farm Layout
Place soul sand in rows. Plant one nether wart per soul sand block. Nether wart takes approximately 15–30 minutes to reach full growth. Harvest manually or use water streams to auto-harvest. Keep a dedicated nether wart farm near your brewing area.

---

## Chorus Fruit Farming

### Growing Chorus Fruit (End Only)
Chorus plants only grow on end stone. Obtain chorus flowers from End City structures or by breaking the top of existing chorus plants. Place a chorus flower on end stone. The plant grows into a branching tree-like structure up to 22 blocks tall. Harvest chorus fruit by breaking any part of the plant. Chorus flowers drop from the tips.

### Chorus Fruit Uses
Eating chorus fruit teleports you randomly within 8 blocks (useful for escaping falls). Popping chorus fruit (smelted) is required to craft purpur blocks and end rods. Farm chorus plants for building materials and emergency teleportation food.

## Tree Farming

### Oak Trees
Plant oak saplings on dirt, grass, podzol, or coarse dirt with 1x1 clear space and at least 5 blocks of vertical clearance. Oak trees grow in 1x1 space minimum. Apply 1–3 bone meal to force instant growth. Oak logs yield sticks, planks, and crafting tables. Oak trees occasionally spawn with apples.

### Birch Trees
Plant birch saplings in a 1x1 space with 6 blocks of vertical clearance. Birch trees are straight and uniform — ideal for efficient wood farms. Apply bone meal to force growth. Birch produces white-toned planks useful for decorative builds.

### Spruce Trees
Plant 1 spruce sapling for a small tree or a 2x2 arrangement for a large mega spruce (up to 30 blocks tall). Mega spruce drops significantly more logs. Apply bone meal to the 2x2 arrangement. Spruce grows in taiga and snowy biomes naturally but farms anywhere with sufficient space.

### Jungle Trees
Plant 1 jungle sapling for a small tree or a 2x2 arrangement for a massive jungle tree (up to 30 blocks tall with vines). The 2x2 jungle tree is the most log-efficient farm. Apply bone meal to any sapling in the 2x2 to grow the whole structure. Jungle trees also have a chance to grow with cocoa pods.

### Acacia Trees
Plant acacia saplings in 1x1 spaces. Acacia trees grow diagonally and require about 6 blocks of horizontal clearance. They grow in savanna biomes but farm anywhere. Acacia produces orange-tinted planks.

### Dark Oak Trees
Always plant dark oak saplings in a 2x2 arrangement — single saplings never grow. Dark oak requires 2x2 minimum and at least 7 blocks of vertical clearance. Apply bone meal to any of the 4 saplings. Dark oak produces large amounts of logs and always generates a dirt platform at its base.

### Cherry Trees
Plant cherry saplings on dirt, grass, or similar. Cherry trees grow in a 1x1 space like birch but produce pink-leafed canopies. Cherry planks are pink and used for decorative builds added in 1.20+. Available in 1.21.1. Apply bone meal to speed growth.

### Mangrove Trees
Propagate mangrove trees using mangrove propagules, which hang from mangrove leaves. Place propagules on mud, dirt, grass, sand, or similar blocks with water nearby. Mangrove trees are the only trees that grow in water. They produce mangrove wood and a large root system. Primarily found in mangrove swamps.

### Tree Farm Design Tips
Space trees at least 2 blocks apart to prevent canopies merging. Use a 5x5 minimum footprint per large tree. Harvest logs from the bottom up and collect saplings from the fallen leaves. Leaves decay within 6–7 blocks from a log, dropping saplings at a 5% rate. Place torches to prevent mob spawning in the farm area.

## Animal Farming

### Breeding Mechanics
Feed two animals of the same species their preferred food to put them in Love Mode. They will breed and produce a baby. All bred animals have a 5-minute (6000 tick) cooldown before they can breed again. Baby animals take 20 minutes (24000 ticks) to grow into adults. Feed babies their preferred food to reduce growth time by 10% per feeding. Keep at least 2 adults in a pen to sustain the herd.

### Animal Pen Design
Build pens with fences and fence gates. Fences are 1.5 blocks tall — mobs cannot jump over them. Use fence gates for player access. Minimum pen size: 5x5 interior. Keep animals separated by species to prevent crowding and breeding confusion. Place a torch inside the pen to prevent hostile mob spawning.

## Cow Farming

### Breeding Cows
Feed wheat to two cows to breed them. Each breeding produces 1 calf. Cows drop 0–2 leather and 1–3 raw beef on death. Cook raw beef for steak, which restores 8 hunger and 12.8 saturation — one of the best food items. Milk cows with an empty bucket to obtain milk, which removes all status effects when drunk.

### Cow Farm Layout
Keep 10–20 cows minimum for a sustainable herd. Kill adults that are not needed as breeders to maintain population and collect resources. A dedicated cow farm produces both food (steak) and leather for enchanting tables and books.

---

## Sheep Farming

### Breeding Sheep
Feed wheat to two sheep to breed them. Sheep drop 0–2 wool and 1–2 raw mutton on death. Shear sheep with shears for 1–3 wool without killing them — wool regrows after the sheep eats grass. Dye a sheep with any dye color before shearing to obtain colored wool. Breed dyed sheep together to guarantee colored offspring.

### Wool Farm Strategy
Maintain a rainbow wool farm with one sheep of each dye color. Shear regularly. Use dispensers with shears and observer-based grass eating detection for semi-automatic wool collection in larger farms.

---

## Pig Farming

### Breeding Pigs
Feed carrots, potatoes, or beetroot to two pigs to breed them. Pigs drop 1–3 raw porkchop on death. Cook porkchop in a furnace for cooked porkchop: 8 hunger, 12.8 saturation. Saddle pigs and use a carrot on a stick to ride and steer them.

---

## Chicken Farming

### Breeding Chickens
Feed any seed type (wheat seeds, melon seeds, pumpkin seeds, beetroot seeds, torchflower seeds, pitcher pod seeds) to two chickens to breed them. Chickens lay eggs every 5–10 minutes. Throw eggs at the ground for a 1-in-8 chance to spawn a chick (1-in-32 chance to spawn 4 chicks). Chickens drop 0–2 feathers and 1 raw chicken on death. Cook raw chicken for food (6 hunger, 7.2 saturation). Raw chicken has a 30% chance to inflict Hunger status — always cook it.

### Egg-Based Chicken Farm
Collect eggs automatically with hoppers under the chickens. Throw eggs into an enclosed area with a lava blade at the adult height to auto-kill adult chickens. Chicks pass under the lava blade safely. This creates a fully automatic cooked chicken and feather farm when combined with a hopper-attached furnace.

---

## Rabbit Farming

### Breeding Rabbits
Feed carrots or dandelions (yellow flowers) to two rabbits to breed them. Rabbits drop 0–1 rabbit hide, 0–1 raw rabbit, and rarely rabbit's foot (2.5% chance). Four rabbit hide crafts into leather. Rabbit's foot is a potion ingredient. Cook raw rabbit for cooked rabbit: 5 hunger, 6 saturation. Craft rabbit stew (cooked rabbit + carrot + mushroom + baked potato + bowl) for 10 hunger and 12 saturation in one item.

---

## Bee Farming and Honey

### Bee Basics
Bees live in bee nests (natural) or beehives (crafted from 6 planks + 3 honeycombs). Each nest/hive holds up to 3 bees. Bees collect pollen from flowers and return to their hive, increasing honey level from 0 to 5. At level 5 the hive is full. Bees become hostile if the hive is harvested without a campfire beneath it.

### Harvesting Honey and Honeycombs
Place a lit campfire directly below a beehive to pacify bees during harvest. Right-click a full hive (honey level 5) with a glass bottle to collect honey bottle (restores 6 hunger + removes poison). Right-click a full hive with shears to collect 3 honeycombs (used to craft more beehives or wax copper). Honey bottles can be crafted into 3 sugar in a crafting grid.

### Moving Beehives
Use shears on a bee nest to collect honeycombs and craft a beehive. To move bees: during night or rain, use a silk touch tool on the hive — this preserves the bees inside. Place the hive near flowers. Attach a lead to a bee to relocate it to a new area.

### Bee Farm Setup
Place beehives next to flower patches for fast pollination. Surround the area with a 2-block-tall fence so bees can fly in and out but stay contained. One bee colony can produce 1 honey bottle per ~2 game hours per bee actively working.

## Mushroom Farming

### Growing Mushrooms
Mushrooms spread to adjacent dirt-type blocks at light level 12 or lower. Place mushrooms in dark rooms (light level 7 or below) to grow a mushroom farm. Mushrooms can also grow on mycelium or podzol at any light level. Mycelium spreads to adjacent dirt, converting it — use this in mushroom farms.

### Bone Meal on Mushrooms
Apply bone meal to a mushroom planted on dirt or mycelium to grow a giant mushroom. Giant mushrooms require a 7x7x8 clear space for brown mushrooms or 7x7x9 for red mushrooms. Break the giant mushroom to collect 0–20 mushroom blocks and mushrooms. This is the fastest way to farm large quantities of mushrooms for mushroom stew.

### Mushroom Stew
Craft mushroom stew with 1 red mushroom + 1 brown mushroom + 1 bowl. Restores 6 hunger and 7.2 saturation. Bowls are reusable — eat stew and the bowl drops. Keep a supply of both mushroom types and wooden bowls.

## Cocoa Bean Farming

### Planting Cocoa Beans
Place cocoa beans on the side of any jungle log (not just jungle wood planks — must be the log block). Cocoa pods have 3 growth stages. Harvest at stage 3 (large orange pod) for 2–3 cocoa beans. Replant one bean immediately on the same log. Bone meal accelerates growth significantly.

### Cocoa Farm Design
Build a column of jungle logs (or a wall of jungle logs). Plant cocoa beans on all four sides of each log. Harvest in rounds as pods mature. Cocoa beans are used exclusively for brown dye and cookies (wheat + cocoa bean = 8 cookies).

## Kelp Farming

### Growing Kelp
Kelp grows naturally in ocean biomes from the seafloor upward. Place kelp on sand, gravel, dirt, or most solid blocks underwater. Kelp grows upward at a random rate up to a maximum height of 26 blocks. Harvest from the second block up — leave the base rooted. Dried kelp (smelted kelp) is an efficient food item: 1 hunger, 0.6 saturation, but extremely fast to eat.

### Kelp as Fuel
Craft 9 dried kelp into 1 dried kelp block. Dried kelp blocks smelt 20 items — more efficient than coal (8 items) per unit. Use a kelp farm to supply fuel for mass smelting operations. Kelp does not need light to grow.

---

## Sea Pickle Farming

### Growing Sea Pickles
Sea pickles grow on coral blocks in warm ocean biomes. Place sea pickles on living coral. Apply bone meal to sea pickles underwater to spread them to adjacent coral blocks. Sea pickles emit light underwater (1 pickle = 6 light, up to 15 at 4 pickles per block). Smelt sea pickles for lime green dye. Sea pickles die if their coral block dies — keep coral alive by keeping it underwater.

## Automatic Farm Designs

### Water Harvesting Farms
Dig a 9x9 plot. Place a water source in the center. Surround with farmland. At harvest time, place a water source at one end of each row — the flowing water breaks all crops and carries them to a collection point. Place a hopper and chest at the low end to collect items. Refill farmland and replant manually or with a dispenser system.

### Observer-Based Auto Farms
Observers detect block state changes including crop growth. Place an observer facing a fully grown crop block. When the crop matures the observer emits a redstone pulse. Connect the pulse to a piston or dispenser. Use a sticky piston to push a water block over the crop row for harvesting. Retract the water, replant with a dispenser loaded with seeds. Combine with hoppers and chests for fully automatic collection.

### Hopper Collection Systems
Dig a trench under your farm. Place hoppers in the trench pointing toward a chest. Items dropped on the farmland surface fall through to hoppers below if there is a 1-block gap (like a slab or pressure plate setup). Alternatively, collect items with a water stream that flows into a hopper funnel. Hoppers can hold 5 stacks of 64 items (320 items total). Chain multiple hoppers for larger farms.

## Composting

### Using the Composter
Place a composter block anywhere. Right-click with plant-based items to add compost layers. The composter needs 7 layers of compost to produce 1 bone meal. Different items have different composting probabilities. High-efficiency items: cactus (50%), kelp (30%), wheat (65%), melon slice (50%), pumpkin (65%), any sapling (65%), hay bale (85%). Low-efficiency: seeds (30%), leaves (30%). Collect bone meal by right-clicking the full (green-topped) composter.

### Composter Efficiency Tips
Use excess crops, seeds, and plant byproducts — never waste them. Wire a composter into a hopper below to auto-eject bone meal into a chest. A high-yield crop farm can sustain a bone meal supply for additional fertilization. Dried kelp blocks give 80% chance per use — very efficient for bulk composting.

## Food Cooking

### Furnace Cooking
Use any fuel in the bottom slot and raw food in the top slot. Furnace cooks 1 item per 10 seconds using 1 coal (smells 8 items). Commonly cooked foods: raw beef → steak, raw porkchop → cooked porkchop, raw chicken → cooked chicken, raw mutton → cooked mutton, raw rabbit → cooked rabbit, potato → baked potato, raw cod → cooked cod, raw salmon → cooked salmon.

### Smoker (2x Speed for Food)
Craft a smoker with 4 logs surrounding a furnace (any log type). Smokers cook food items twice as fast as a regular furnace (5 seconds per item instead of 10). Use the same fuel as a furnace. Smokers only process food items — they cannot smelt ores or craft other items. Build a smoker dedicated to food processing near your farm.

### Campfire Cooking
Place a campfire (flint + steel or craft: 3 sticks + 1 coal + 3 logs in an X pattern). Right-click with raw food to place up to 4 items on the campfire to cook simultaneously. No fuel needed — the campfire burns indefinitely. Cooking takes 30 seconds per item (3x slower than a furnace) but requires no fuel. Use campfires for passive cooking of small batches. Items cook and auto-pop off — place a hopper chest below to catch them.

### Soul Campfire
Craft with soul sand or soul soil instead of logs. Functions identically to a normal campfire for cooking. Also keeps bees calm within 5 blocks (same as regular campfire). Emits blue flame and half the light level.

## Food Saturation and Hunger Mechanics

### How Hunger Works
Hunger is displayed as 10 drumstick icons (20 hunger points total). Hunger depletes from sprinting, jumping, attacking, and regenerating health. When hunger drops to 6 or below you cannot sprint. At 0 hunger you take starvation damage (down to 10 HP on normal, down to 1 HP on hard). Saturation is a hidden buffer that depletes before the visible hunger bar.

### Saturation Priority
Always eat high-saturation foods. Saturation reduces the rate at which the hunger bar depletes. A food with high saturation keeps you full longer per item eaten. Eat when hunger is at 18–20 (nearly full) to maximize saturation absorption — eating at low hunger wastes saturation.

### Key Food Values (Hunger / Saturation)
- Steak: 8 hunger / 12.8 saturation — best general food
- Cooked porkchop: 8 hunger / 12.8 saturation — equal to steak
- Cooked mutton: 6 hunger / 9.6 saturation
- Cooked chicken: 6 hunger / 7.2 saturation
- Cooked salmon: 6 hunger / 9.6 saturation
- Cooked cod: 5 hunger / 6 saturation
- Baked potato: 5 hunger / 6 saturation — cheap and efficient
- Bread: 5 hunger / 6 saturation — easy to mass produce from wheat
- Golden carrot: 6 hunger / 14.4 saturation — highest saturation, best for combat
- Pumpkin pie: 8 hunger / 4.8 saturation — high hunger, low saturation
- Rabbit stew: 10 hunger / 12 saturation — highest hunger restoration in one item
- Beetroot: 1 hunger / 1.2 saturation — poor food value, avoid eating raw
- Apple: 4 hunger / 2.4 saturation — early game only
- Melon slice: 2 hunger / 1.2 saturation — poor, only for early desperate situations
- Dried kelp: 1 hunger / 0.6 saturation — fast to eat, useful for quick top-offs
- Cookie: 2 hunger / 0.4 saturation — poor value, use cocoa for dye instead
- Raw chicken: 2 hunger / 1.2 saturation — 30% chance to give Hunger debuff, always cook

### Best Early Game Food Progression
1. Apples and berries from the environment to survive day 1.
2. Cook any raw meat from animal kills.
3. Plant wheat, carrots, or potatoes immediately on day 1.
4. Transition to baked potatoes or bread once farm matures.
5. Set up cow/pig farm for steak and porkchop as primary long-term food source.
6. Obtain golden carrots for combat and boss fights.

### Hunger Regeneration Thresholds
At 18+ hunger (9+ icons filled) your health regenerates at 1 HP per 0.5 seconds in normal mode. At 20 hunger (full) regen is fastest. Keep hunger topped off during combat and exploration. Eating golden carrots before a boss fight maximizes your health regeneration window during the encounter.
